June 19, 2020June 23, 2020 Bellevue University

June 19, 2020

Watch this website for updates and information related to Bellevue University’s continuing response to the COVID-19 pandemic. If you have any questions please get in touch with us at dso@bellevue.edu —

  • Campus visits have been authorized for prospective students. Visits are limited to individual visitors; not groups. Everyone must wear a mask on campus and in face-to-face meetings.
  • Phase I Guidelines for Coming Back to Campus are available and provide guidance for staff, students and faculty who may be working, learning or interacting on campus.
  • Updated information is available for incoming international students. Please see Updates for International Students in the Updates for Students section.
  • No events or meetings are being held on the Bellevue University campus through June 30, 2020. A decision will be made regarding future events in late June and additional communication will follow.
  • Bellevue University business travel continues to be suspended, unless it is approved by the President’s office.
  • Bellevue University is advising against personal international travel for Bellevue University employees. In accordance with Nebraska’s Directed Health Measure, anyone returning from international travel will be required to quarantine at home for 14 days upon return.
  • If an employee elects to take a personal trip outside Nebraska, they will be responsible for self-monitoring and quarantining at home upon their return, as needed.

March 30, 2020April 6, 2020 Bellevue University

March 30th Updates

  • All residential, in-person classes resuming in a distance learning format. This includes all day and evening residential classes on the Bellevue and Lozier campuses. Current online classes continue with no change in schedule.
  • The OneStop Welcome Center is closed to walk-in traffic. Bruin Support Services will continue to provide services to students and staff via email and phone.
  • The Freeman Lozier Library is adjusting its physical hours of operation. The library building will be open:
    • Monday through Friday, 8am – 6pm
    • Saturday and Sunday, 1 pm – 5pm.
  • Library staff are still available to provide assistance online during regular hours of operation and to students around the globe via the 24/7 Librarian chat on the library website.
  • The computer lab in the Riley Technology Center is closed until further notice. Students may still access computer equipment and services in the library and in the Margre H. Durham Student Center on the main campus and in the northwest Omaha Lozier Professional Center.
  • The Military Veteran Services Center is closed to walk-in traffic. Those needing support from the MVSC are urged to call ahead to 1.866.676.3614 or email mvsc@bellevue.edu.
