Reopening Campus

Information about how the University is actively preparing for the 2020 Fall semester


What You Need to Know

  • ProtecTU: Protecting the wellness of our campus community is very important to us. We are taking significant steps to help keep our students, faculty, and staff healthy.

  • Trinity is actively preparing: The University is making plans for a modified return to campus for the Fall 2020 Semester.

Return to Campus: Fall 2020

Our mission is to help students build lives of meaning and purpose, and to pursue this mission together in community on our red brick campus. However, as we plan our return, the health and wellness of our students, faculty and staff remains paramount.

In response to the tremendous challenges resulting from the COVID-19 pandemic, learning, living, working and playing at Trinity University will proceed differently in the fall. The University established seven cross-campus working groups and a dedicated program office called the Nerve Center to prepare for our reopening, in consultation with physicians, epidemiologists and health officials.

The guiding philosophy of this work is to think of Trinity University as if it were a small town, and to determine what environmental and operational adjustments and capabilities would need to be in place to protect community members. We recently completed Phase I of this work, which represents critical decisions with regard to teaching, learning, working and living on campus.

Throughout June and July, more detailed information on the return to campus for students will be shared on our website and directly with students, faculty and staff.

Health & Wellness

The fundamental principles of our reopening plan call for Trinity to ensure it has the capacity and capabilities to test, trace and coordinate treatment of its community members. To do this, Trinity has partnered with local healthcare providers to enhance our Health Services Team, to provide testing and contact tracing services on our campus, and to supplement the care of any students that require quarantine, isolation and treatment while on campus.

In addition, we must create a first line of defense against COVID-19 by creating an environment and attitude of appropriate behaviors. As such, changes will be made to buildings and classrooms to promote physical distancing. Use of face coverings will be required in interior spaces where others are or may be present. Dining services will be altered to provide for safer dining and grab-and-go options.

We will also implement the use of a daily symptom checker for all students, faculty and staff, and employ contact tracing tools to manage the spread of the disease.

Please see Trinity Universityâ€™s Health Guidelines to Reopening for more detailed information about our evidence-based approach to health and safety.


Teaching & Learning

Some classes and activities will necessarily proceed online, with learning taking place synchronously, just as some students and faculty will need to teach and learn remotely. Classes will also be recorded whenever possible. The University is making a significant investment in new academic technology to support a robust remote learning environment. Students who need to learn remotely will also be supported to ensure they have the tools and resources they need to succeed academically.

Classroom occupancy will be limited to no more than 50 persons, with physical distancing at all times. Face coverings, with very limited exceptions, will be required in classrooms for the protection of everyone. Some outdoor learning spaces are being identified as well.

As we shared last week, the 2020-21 Academic Calendar has been adjusted to maximize in-person class time, ensure robust First Year Experience classes, and mitigate health risks late in the fall semester.

Living

In order to create space for the possibility of students requiring isolation or quarantine, and to de-densify our residence halls, several hundred juniors were granted a release from residency requirements. In addition, residence halls will undergo enhanced cleaning throughout the academic year and residential life staff will receive COVID-19 specific training, though we all share in the responsibility to keep our rooms and spaces clean. Protecting the well-being of our Tiger community will require sincere, diligent dedication from each and every one of us.

Students, faculty, and staff will be asked to sign a health pledge, in which we will commit to responsible action toward achieving a healthy campus, honoring Trinityâ€™s mission of academic and professional excellence.

Because the academic calendar has been adjusted to end in-person classes on Nov. 20, the residence halls will be closing accordingly. Students will receive prorated charges for room and board based on their time on campus. Trinity University treats City Vista as independent apartment living and it will not close during the winter break and at Thanksgiving; itâ€™s important to note, however, that all on-campus services and buildings will be closed after Nov. 20 and resume remotely until the end of the fall semester.

Critical dates and information for both new students and returning students will be added to this website throughout the summer, as plans are formulated.

Dining services will be altered, with no self-service or buffets. Grab-and-go food will be available on upper campus, and changes to seating and service in Mabee Dining Hall will be in place in accordance with health and safety guidelines. Our food service partners are committed to following the same guidelines and procedures as Trinity faculty, students and staff.

Other facilities, such as the Bell Center, Coates Library and CSI, will be open until Nov. 20, but may require adjustments to hours and environment as necessary to allow for extra cleaning and other safety accommodations.

Working

Staff will return to campus in phases during the month of July. Staff who would like to request accommodations or alternative work arrangements based on risk to COVID-19 and/or dependent care needs may do so using this form.

Prior to reporting for their first day of work, staff will receive online training regarding COVID-19. On the first day of return to campus, all staff will undergo testing and orientation on our new policies and protocols. Each day, employees will be asked to check their symptoms using a special application via their phone or computer to receive an â€œall clearâ€ before coming to campus.

Currently, Environmental Health and Safety is meeting with supervisors to make adjustments to work spaces in accordance with our safety guidelines. More details on the return to campus for Faculty will be shared in the coming weeks.

As part of our efforts to create a sense of shared responsibility and commitment to each otherâ€™s health and safety, all members of the Trinity community will be asked to take a Trinity health pledge, which we will share with you in the coming days.

Athletics

Coaches, staff and athletics trainers are already working to make the necessary adjustments to facilities and protocols to protect student athletes. Conversations are underway with the Southern Collegiate Athletics Conference (SCAC) and its member schools to determine the best way to proceed with Fall sports. In addition, we are awaiting more information and guidance from the NCAA, specifically as it pertains to Division III programs. Coaches will communicate new details with students and their families as more information becomes available.

Trinity University COVID-19 Task Forces

The continued spread of the COVID-19 virus, the lack of a vaccine, and the probability of a second spike in cases require Trinity to alter the way we work, teach, learn, and live at Trinity, affecting all divisions and stakeholders. Essentially, we must make fundamental changes to physical facilities, processes, policies, technology, and pedagogy using a process of shared governance and a distributive model for planning and execution.

Goals

  • Ensure the health and safety of students, faculty, and staff following the guidance of public health experts

  • Ensure a robust teaching and learning environment

  • Retain and engage new and returning students

  • Ensure academic freedom, faculty guidelines, and predictability for students

  • Maintain and enhance support for student academic success

Scope

Recommend implementation guidelines for the fall semester that address everything from the overall academic calendar to plans for social distancing in the classroom, during breaks between classes, and library workspace. This includes identifying needs and recommendations for possible changes to the class schedule, remote synchronous or asynchronous education, experiential learning, research labs, and performance spaces.

Seven Task Forces have been established to make decisions in their specific areas. Each Task Force will collaborate with the COVID-19 Nerve Center team to ensure consistent and timely communication of plans and progress and identification of interdependencies among the groups.
