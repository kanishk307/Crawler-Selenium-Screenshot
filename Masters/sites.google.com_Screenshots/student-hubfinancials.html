Financials

⚠️ All students are asked to sign up for an e-refund through the Student Account Suite. If you do not sign up for an electronic refund, checks will be mailed on April 10.

CARES Act Compliance Statement

This statement provides information to comply with reporting requirements for Trinity University’s participation in the Federal Coronavirus Aid, Relief, and Economic Security (CARES) Act and Higher Education Emergency Relief Fund (HEERF).

On April 9, 2020, in a letter from the Department of Education, Trinity learned that it was eligible to receive an allocation from the HEERF program of $1,665,508, of which no less than $832,754 (50%) must be distributed to eligible students in the form of emergency grants related to disruption of instruction due to the Covid-19 pandemic.

On April 23, 2020, Trinity submitted the required grant documents for the first half of the HEERF funds for student emergency grants. Trinity submitted the required certification document at that time.

On May 1, 2020, Trinity received notice from the Department of Education that the HEERF emergency student grant was awarded.

On May 19, 2020, A CARES Act disbursement committee established by Trinity University determined that there were 1181 students enrolled in the spring 2020 semester that were Title IV eligible, which is a federal requirement to receive assistance. Of those, 366 students were Pell Grant eligible. The committee chose to award $828 to all Pell eligible students, and $650 to all other Title IV students (815) that demonstrated need through the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA).

On May 26, 2020, an electronic notice was sent to all eligible students informing them that they would receive their portion of the HEERF grant either electronically to their designated financial account or as a check sent to their address of record. This notice was posted on Trinity’s website on the same day.

On June 3, 2020, the University begins the process of disbursing the designated grants to students.

Student Emergency Fund

The Student Emergency Fund will help provide relief for students experiencing immediate financial challenges related to the coronavirus pandemic, including:

  • Food

  • Housing

  • Medical care

  • Technology

  • Travel

Applying for Student Emergency Funds

Fill out the Google form for requests for financial assistance. Requests are intended to be one-time instances, but multiple requests may be submitted and considered. Once the request is submitted it will be reviewed by the Emergency Fund Committee, and you will receive notification or request for further information as quickly as possible.

Funds will be disbursed as quickly as possible through the end of the semester.

Taxes and Other Guidelines

While there is no cap on the funds requested, amounts more than $500 may require further explanation or may not be able to be granted in their full amount. There is no expectation that this money be repaid.

Per federal guidelines, any funds distributed to students may impact financial aid. Staff from Financial Aid will review requests to ensure that students are not negatively impacted for receiving funds. Please note any payment from this fund may be considered taxable income by the IRS and could be subject to federal tax regulations.

Financial Planning

The Student Emergency Fund is separate from the reimbursement process. View more information on this page to learn what reimbursements and refunds students can expect.

Refunds & Reimbursements

Refundable items:

Room and Meal Plans
Trinity will be reimbursing room and board at a prorated level and is currently processing refunds as expediently as possible. Board will include refunds for both meal swipes and Bonus Bucks. Reimbursement for room and board will be prorated from March 16 - May 12.

Parking
Parking fees will be refunded at a flat rate of $10.

Non-Refundable items:

Course Fees
Course fees for credits earned are not refundable.

Getting Your Refund:

Refunds are expected to be paid out electronically by April 10 or checks will be mailed on April 10.
