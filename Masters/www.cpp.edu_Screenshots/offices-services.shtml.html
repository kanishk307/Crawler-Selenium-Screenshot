    Offices & Services -- Open, Closed, Modified Operations

    Cal Poly Pomona is open. Some campus offices and services have modified hours and operations. This page will be updated as more information is available. (Updated 6/22/2020)

    • Student Health and Wellness Center
      The Student Health Center will continue to provide care and is working on additional telehealth services. The Center will remain open during regular business hours, and all appointments must be made over the phone. In an effort to keep everyone safe, Health Center staff will screen students by phone prior to scheduling an in-person appointment. Please call (909) 869-4000 between 8 a.m. and 5 p.m., Monday through Friday.

       

      The Bronco Wellness Center is providing virtual one-on-one educational sessions on various health topics, along with virtual wellness workshops. These are available on the Wellness Center website.
    • Counseling & Psychological Services

      Students:

      CAPS is available for all student inquiries about services, including brief individual therapy and crisis management, during regular business hours between 8am and 5pm. Although we are unable to provide in-person appointments due to observing social distancing guidelines, we are providing all of our services via phone and zoom formats. Students may reach us by leaving a detailed voicemail at (909) 869-3220, by email at caps@cpp.edu, or by private message to our Instagram or Twitter accounts (@cppcaps). We ask that students leave their name, Bronco ID, an updated phone number, and a good time to reach them. For confidentiality reasons, please be aware that all phone communication from CAPS will come from a number showing No Caller ID. For assistance with a crisis outside of business hours, please call CAPS at (909) 869-3220 and press option 2 when prompted to speak with a live crisis counselor. If you are experiencing an emergency, please dial 911 or call University Police at (909) 869-3220. To protect student privacy, clinical services are not provided through email or text. Students may visit our website for a full listing of services including psychotherapy, crisis management support, groups, outreach, workshops, wellbeing coaching, and on-/off-campus resources at https://www.cpp.edu/caps/index.shtml.

       

      Campus partners, faculty and staff:

      We are happy to support our campus partners in helping students learn more about our services and to offer consultation on issues related to mental health during business hours between 8am and 5pm. You may call or email us requesting a non-emergency consultation with a CAPS clinician. If you are concerned for the immediate well-being of a student who may be an imminent risk of death to self or others, please contact University Police at (909) 869-3220 or dial 911.

    • Housing Services

      Following the Governor's Executive Order directing that all individuals living in California stay at home, University Housing Services and the University Village have worked to reduce the density of the population on campus. Because of this, residents are strongly encouraged to return home, if possible. UHS will continue to serve those students who need to remain on campus, although most services and operations have been modified or closed, including the following:

      • UHS residents can no longer bring outside guests into their rooms/suites. Only visitors assisting residents with moving their belonings will be allowed.
      • Laundry facilities are operating in only Encinitas, Montecito, Sol, Sicomoro, and Secoya.
      • ALL UHS common areas including lounges and study rooms are closed.
      • Any policy violations during this critical time may result in eviction upon first offense.
      • The La Cienega Housing Office and Fitness Express are closed.
      • The main UHS office will operate Monday to Friday, 8 a.m. to noon
      • Mail/packages will operate through Secoya, Monday to Friday, noon to 5 p.m.

      This information, along with detailed information for move-out and cancellation procedures, was emailed to residents on March 20. Please refer to to this email and continue to monitor the Housing website for updates.
