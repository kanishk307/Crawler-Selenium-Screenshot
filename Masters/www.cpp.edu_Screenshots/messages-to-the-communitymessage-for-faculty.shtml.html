    Message for Faculty

    Date: 2/11/2020
    From: Office of Student Health and Wellbeing
    Subject: Message for Faculty

    On 2/4/2020 several colleges and universities joined a telebriefing with the Department of Public Health and received the most current information regarding the Coronavirus. Please note that the news from LA County and other Public Health sources is relatively good—the risk remains very low in California and the United States, as a whole. You can learn all about the virus here- Centers for Disease Control- Corona Virus information

    Federal and local health agencies have expanded precautionary measures to reduce the risk of transmission of the 2019 Novel Coronavirus (2019-nCoV) in the United States. As of 5 p.m. Sunday, February 2, travelers from China are being evaluated by local public health authorities upon arrival to the U.S. and may be subject to movement restrictions (quarantine or social distancing) and monitoring requirements based on their level of risk. If any of our students are asked to engage in social distancing, they will need to stay home and away from school, work, and other places where large groups of people gather.

    The Department of Public Health is asking that faculty work with students to ensure that students are able to complete their 14 days of movement restriction (social distancing or quarantine) without penalty. This means that faculty are being asked to be flexible and work with students to minimize any negative impact on their academic performance while the students comply with instructions given to them from public health officials. Students who need to stay away from campus and are in compliance are protecting the public health of others (students, faculty, staff) and we do not want them to feel penalized for doing so. Students will not always have physical proof of being on social distancing measures or other movement restrictions, however, if they tell you they are working with a public health official, chances are that they are indeed engaging in social distancing.

    As stated before, we care about the health and wellbeing of our students, faculty, staff, and visitors, and want to ensure that precautionary measures are taken so that the coronavirus does not impact our community. Again, there is no current known risk to our community and none of the U.S. cases are connected to Cal Poly Pomona.

    Be sure to refer to our campus Health Alert website and the links to Public Health agencies provided on that page. These agencies are updating the information on a regular basis. CPP Health Alert Page

    Please feel free to contact us or phone (626)533-4459, should you have any questions or concerns.

    We care about the wellbeing of our Cal Poly Community and urge everyone to stay informed on this current health situation.

    Be Well,
    Dr. Leti
