    International Travel, 2/28/2020

    Date: 2/28/2020
    From: Provost and Vice President for Academic Affairs
    To: Campus Community
    Subject: International Travel

    The U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention has issued a Level 3 travel advisory for South Korea, which advises against all non-essential travel. As a result, we are requiring Cal Poly Pomona students who are in South Korea to leave the country, and we are working actively with them to make arrangements to travel safely back to California.

    In addition, with the rapidly evolving situation in parts of Italy, we are working with the Chancellor’s Office in monitoring the CSU’s International Program in Florence, Italy (located in a region which is not among the primary regions where cases have been identified). The CO’s International Programs team is in daily contact with our CSU Florence Center staff and has taken steps to ensure the wellbeing of all CSU participants. As of this writing, there are no plans to suspend the program, but we will continue to follow developments and provide updates as appropriate. We are asking students to stay in close contact with program administrators in case travel restrictions are required in the future.

    We want to reiterate that there have been no cases of COVID-19 at Cal Poly Pomona.

    For general questions or comments about COVID-19, please call 909-869-4000. You can find all the latest information and reports by the Center for Disease Control (CDC), The US Department of State, World Health Organization.

    Any student who is feeling worried or concerned for family or friends overseas should contact Counseling & Psychological Services at (909) 869-3220 to arrange an appointment. Faculty and staff are encouraged to call the CPPLifeMatters at 1-800-367-7474. We are all here for you.

    Your safety, wellbeing, and ability to continue your studies and other activities remain the highest priorities for all of us at Cal Poly Pomona.

    Sincerely,

    Dr. Sylvia Alva, Provost and Vice President for Academic Affairs
    Dr. Eileen Sullivan, Vice President for Student Affairs
    Dr. Leticia Gutierrez-Lopez, Associate Vice President for Student Health and Wellbeing
