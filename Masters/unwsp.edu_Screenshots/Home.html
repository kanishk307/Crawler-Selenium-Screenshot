UNW Updates on the Coronavirus Disease

Friday, May 15, 2020

Fall ’20 Return to Campus


Dear University of Northwestern Students,

Your emails, texts, and social media posts have let us know that you miss your university, your professors, and each other. You want to be on campus for the Fall 2020 semester. We want you to be on campus, too.

I am very pleased to share with you that we plan to open our beautiful and beloved campus for fall classes in August. The decision comes after much research, planning, and prayer by our leadership and emergency response teams as we continue to navigate these unchartered waters.

We will be ready. We have medical, operational, and public safety experts working continually to provide the safest and healthiest environment possible for our students, faculty, and staff.

While this is our plan, we do not know how state and federal restrictions may affect our efforts in the coming weeks or months. Neither do we have any way of knowing whether a resurgence of the coronavirus will impact our collective health and well-being, which could force us to pivot to an alternate scenario. We will continue to update you with any changes.

The momentum is already building for fall as you register for classes and sign up for dorms. Faculty are developing and reviewing lesson plans, and staff are preparing for your return. We can’t wait to see you.

When we are back together, we will be different than the last time we saw each other. This strange and uncertain time has tested us and stretched each of us in ways we could not have imagined. I am moved by the strength and fortitude that you have demonstrated in completing this last semester amid so many challenges.

Now, equipped with new skills and fresh perspectives, we look forward to the fall semester and the opportunities that await us. This is the course of our hearts. We also remember that our sovereign Lord is in control and will be glorified with an outcome that we cannot predict. We humbly submit to His will.

“In their hearts humans plan their course, but the Lord establishes their steps.’’
Proverbs 16:9

Sincerely,

President Alan Cureton

Coronavirus Updates

June 12, 2020: Fall Return to Campus

Northwestern is planning to resume on-campus classes, as scheduled, during the Fall 2020 semester.

Some things will look different than what we've been used to as we anticipate the fall. While specific details continue to be worked out by respective groups on campus, we intend to use the current configuration of on-campus housing and will social distance where possible. When social distancing is not possible, we will provide opportunities to lower the risk of spreading the COVID-19 virus.

Our return-to-campus plan is supported by the current CDC and Minnesota Department of Health (MDH) guidelines for on-campus instruction. It also positions us to pivot to a virtual format at any time should there be a resurgence of the virus.

We invite you to view the Return to Campus webpage as information becomes available throughout the month of June.

April 29, 2020: University of Northwestern's Response to Anti-Asian Discrimination

In this time of COVID-19, racism against Asian Americans has increased in our country. University of Northwestern stands with the Asian American Christian Collaborative as they work to increase awareness and education and support our Asian American brothers and sisters.

I signed the AACC’s Statement of Anti-Asian Racism in the Time of COVID-19 denouncing discrimination and racism. University of Northwestern will not tolerate harmful rhetoric, violence, or oppression. I encourage you to view a video created by Student Life that addresses the challenges of Asian Americans.

I ask you to pray with me for God’s protection and peace for Asian Americans on our campus, in our community, around our state, and across our country during this time and always.

President Alan Cureton

April 9, 2020: President's Message Regarding Minnesota's Continued Stay At Home Order

Dear University of Northwestern Community:

Today, Minnesota Governor Tim Walz directed everyone in the state to continue to "stay at home” and limit movements outside their homes beyond essential needs until May 4. This order extends the Stay at Home Executive Order 20-20, issued on March 25, 2020.

In an effort to slow the spread of COVID-19, UNW will continue to comply with the Executive Order 20-33, so please be aware of the following:

  • Consistent with guidance from the governor’s office, our virtual learning environment will continue as planned so students may complete the semester.
  • Residence hall checkouts remain on hold until the order is lifted. Please do not travel to campus to retrieve personal items or check out of your room while the Minnesota “stay at home” order is in effect. Access to the residence halls will be restricted. If you have questions regarding your checkout, please contact housing@unwsp.edu.
  • Students who received prior approval to live on campus until the end of the semester are still approved to remain on campus. These students will have access to limited services, including food service, public safety, and modified health and counseling services. We expect students remaining on campus to follow all updated housing policies, including social distancing practices. We will continue to follow cleaning protocols already in place to mitigate the risks of COVID-19.
  • During the governor’s order, university academic and administrative buildings will be closed to students and to all non-essential employees. Most services and operations will be available by alternative formats.
  • Only essential employees whose work requires them to be on campus should report to campus. These include but are not limited to employees in Student Life, Health Services, Facilities Services, IT, Public Safety, and faculty who need to be on campus to deliver virtual instruction. These essential employees will follow social distancing and other guidelines to prevent the spread of the coronavirus disease. Again, our goal is to preserve as many services as are needed to ensure the wellbeing of students and the continuation of education through the end of spring semester.
  • If you are uncertain of your role as an essential employee during a furlough, please contact your supervisor.

Students and family members with questions can reference UNW’s COVID-19 FAQ page on the website and more detailed FAQ pages on theRock.

Thank you for your continued understanding, grace, and compliance with the Governor’s Executive Order to “Stay At Home” during these ever-changing times.

Sincerely,

President Alan Cureton

April 7, 2020: President's Message Regarding Commencement and Summer Courses

Dear University of Northwestern Community,

Given the uncertainties of the rapidly evolving COVID-19 situation, the Senior Leadership Team and I have made the decision to postpone Spring 2020 Commencement ceremonies, originally scheduled for May 16. We have also decided to extend virtual learning through the summer.

We know the postponement of graduation ceremonies is a disappointment to our students and families, as well as to our faculty and staff who looked forward to celebrating this milestone with our Class of 2020. However, we are making this decision out of an abundance of caution for the safety and well-being of our community.

Details about the date, time, and venue for commencement will be forthcoming.

For continuing students, the decision has been made to extend our virtual learning community through the summer. This will require that all summer academic programs and courses will be converted to virtual delivery, as feasible. All summer semester online courses will be offered as scheduled. While this type of learning may be new to some students, it is not new to Northwestern. We have been delivering exceptional online education since 1994.

Though summer is still weeks away, deadlines for summer offerings are approaching quickly. Many of you may have questions about specific programs and courses offered this summer. Details about summer offerings, dates, and fees will be provided in the coming days.

We will continue to follow direction from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), Minnesota Department of Health (MDH), and the federal government. Northwestern’s coronavirus web page and FAQs will continue to be updated regularly with additional information and resources.

Please continue to be in prayer for the safety and wellbeing of students, faculty, and staff. The adjustments required in previous weeks have not been easy, but I am proud of how we have come together and made a successful transition to a full virtual learning community.

With gratitude and appreciation,

President Alan Cureton

April 7, 2020: President's Message Regarding Day of Prayer and Fasting

Dear UNW faculty members and media/university staff,

In times of adversity, those who proclaim Christ as their Lord and Savior have a deep appreciation for the importance of and need for prayer. Now is such a time.

Prayer is a key characteristic of the UNW community. It is the thread of our tapestry. Faculty members often begin their classes each day in prayer. Team meetings at UNW begin with prayer. Often, we pray for one another outside of our team meetings or classes, especially when there is a distinct need. Each and every day we pray for our listeners, especially listeners who call needing prayer. When they call to give, we ask: “how may I pray for you?” We have a prayer wall full of these requests at every SHARE event.

We also have an official designated Day of Prayer and Service on the academic calendar because we believe it is important enough that a special day be set aside to focus our attention and energy. We know prayer is vital.

Yes, it is who we are. It is our ethos. Why? Because we know the Lord loves to receive our prayers. We are instructed to pray without ceasing.

So, in times like this, when we battle an invisible enemy – one that is claiming thousands of lives across the globe – we need to pray. COVID-19 is impacting the university and media ministry in multiple ways. It is impacting our students, our listeners, and us as individuals. No one is immune.

Therefore, we must act. In collaboration with the Minnesota National Day of Prayer team, which is calling for a day of prayer and fasting on Maundy Thursday, I am asking that all UNW and media employees join me in a day of prayer and fasting on Thursday, April 9, 2020.

This is completely voluntary. No one will follow up with you. No one will monitor you. I simply ask that you join me in prayer as we pray for our students, our listeners, our families, our friends, our local church, our communities, our nation, and the world. The prayers of the saints are mighty and powerful.

So, join me, if you will, in a day of prayer and fasting this Thursday, April 9th.

President Alan Cureton

March 27, 2020: President's Message Regarding Credits to Student Accounts

Dear Students,

We understand that COVID-19 has impacted you in many ways, including financially. We know the impact of requiring you to move off campus—or to remain on campus as needed—brings with it the question of costs associated with meals and housing. Please know that University of Northwestern will credit Spring Semester 2020 student accounts as follows:

Housing

Residential students who are not preapproved to remain in housing will receive a housing credit based on the reduced occupancy term from the beginning of the spring semester through March 22, 2020 the date of residence hall closure.

Residential students who are preapproved to remain in housing will be billed for the full spring semester.

Residential students who are preapproved to remain in housing past March 22, 2020 who choose to move off-campus prior to May 15, 2020 will receive a credit based on their occupancy duration (email housing@unwsp.edu prior to moving off campus to confirm move-off date).

Dining

Commuter students, and residential students who are not preapproved to remain in housing will receive a dining funds credit based on the amount of dining funds that have been used, up to half of the required amount for their status for spring semester. Any dining funds that students added during spring semester will also be eligible for a dining funds credit if those funds have not been used.

Full meal plans will be left in place for those students who have been preapproved to and currently remain in UNW housing. Dining services will continue to be available to these students while they remain on campus. Those students that were preapproved to continue living on campus but choose to move off campus prior to May 15, 2020 will be issued a pro-rated credit based on the amount of dining funds that have been used.

Students who were a part of the ICS trip during Quad 3 will be issued full credit for their spring semester meal plan charges.

Student Activity Fee

Half of the Spring Semester student activity fee will be credited to student accounts. All other fees have been applied to existing programs and infrastructure for the university and will not be pro-rated.

All fees were reviewed individually for their purpose and application to programs, maintenance of our campus buildings and grounds, and the repair or replacement of equipment, furniture, and fixtures. It was with these factors in mind that crediting half of the spring semester student activity fee was determined.

Applicable credits will be issued to student accounts by April 15, 2020 and applied to any remaining balance on their account at the time of issue. Any credit balance on the student account after applicable credits are applied will be refunded to the student by a check issued by April 20, 2020.

We appreciate your patience and understanding as we work through these and many other issues resulting from COVID-19. We continue to pray for community and country in these unprecedented times and ask that you would do the same.

If you have any questions regarding these adjustments at this time, please refer to FAQ on theROCK. If you still have questions after April 15, 2020, once credits are applied, please email studentaccounts@unwsp.edu.

President Alan Cureton

March 19, 2020: Latest Decision Regarding Housing

Hello,

By now we trust that you have read the President’s most recent communication regarding UNW’s move to a virtual learning community through the remainder of the Spring 2020 semester.

Consequently, we will begin final room checkouts immediately, continuing through the end of the semester, Friday, May 15th. However, the preferred checkout timeframe is between now and April 5th. For those who are unable to checkout by April 5th, please contact housing@unwsp.edu. As a reminder, the Residence Halls are closed beginning March 22nd - except to complete the checkout process or for those with a formal, approved continued housing exception request.

Due to these unique circumstances, we are employing a ‘self-checkout’ process. Please read the below details thoroughly to ensure a proper checkout. The following items have been placed under the door in each room.

  • Checkout instructions on green paper (one per resident)
  • A key envelope (one for each resident; individually labeled)
  • A maintenance request form (one per room)

The instructions include the steps below as well as a URL to the online Self-Checkout Form that you must fill out to complete your checkout.

In order to check out, you will need to confirm the following actions/answer the following question:

  • All your belongings (including personal furniture) have been removed from your room.
  • All trash has been discarded in the dumpsters outside.
  • All college furniture has been left in its current configuration.
  • All walls and ceilings have been cleared of your materials.
  • Room has been cleaned to a reasonable standard.
  • Maintenance request form has been completed and left on vanity.
  • Leave your key in the key envelope in the top desk drawer.
  • All doors and windows have been shut and locked.
  • What damages in the room are you claiming as your own or splitting with roommates?

Once you have taken the steps above and filled out the online Self-Checkout Form, you do not need to contact a Residence Life staff member. Your portion of the checkout is complete and will be processed.

In order to better support CDC and Minnesota Department of Health (MDH) guidelines, we are limiting the number of students in the Residence Halls at the same time. Using the below designated link for the building you reside in, select a self-checkout date and time:

  • Akenson – (ttsu.me/rzet5d)
  • Hartill – (ttsu.me/s8tumh)
  • KnuHa – (ttsu.me/atjan2)
  • Knutson – (ttsu.me/85jvc8)
  • Moyer – (ttsu.me/rubend)
  • Pittman North – (ttsu.me/awpyjc)
  • Ramseyer – (ttsu.me/hspbd7)

When you arrive to checkout, please complete your checkout in a timely manner and do your best to adhere to CDC and MDH guidelines to limit exposure.

Note: If you are currently in self-quarantine due to travel to a CDC Level 3 country, have shown any symptoms of COVID-19 or have been in contact with anyone that has been confirmed to have COVID-19, do not come to campus for checkouts at this time. Instead, please continue to follow CDC and MDH guidelines as they relate to self-quarantine before coming to campus for your checkout.

Information regarding financial adjustments will be forthcoming. We appreciate your prayers and patience through this fluid situation. We already miss seeing you on campus and look forward to greeting returning students in August!

Sincerely,

The Residence Life Team

March 16, 2020: President's Message

Dear University of Northwestern Community,

We, as a people of faith, serve the one true God who is not surprised by the current state of our nation, healthcare, fears, and future. This is a rare opportunity for us to pray corporately as the body of Christ and to rely on His sovereignty as we make and implement decisions.

The Senior Leadership Team and Pandemic Response Team are continuously monitoring the COVID-19 developments. As you are aware, this continues to be a rapidly changing situation.

Given the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), Minnesota Department of Health (MDH) and the federal government’s recent guidance, University of Northwestern will be implementing procedures to limit the spread of COVID-19 in our communities and among our faculty, staff and students. Our goal is to minimize risk to our community as much as possible.

Employees:

Effective immediately or by March 17, the following instructions are in effect:

  • You should connect with your supervisor to determine whether you can do your work remotely;
  • If you are able to perform only part of your work remotely, please discuss how to handle the remainder of your worktime with your supervisor;
  • If you need to be on campus, please practice social distancing as emphasized by the CDC;
  • If you or someone in your household is sick, please stay home (see FAQ regarding pay implications);

  • Student employees, please refer to the FAQ regarding student employment.

Because the nature of this virus continues to evolve quickly, we will be making a decision about the remainder of the semester by April 3. As previously communicated, on-campus classes are suspended until March 23 after which they will resume in the virtual instruction learning environment. Should the situation warrant a change to this current plan, updates will be provided.

Access to all residence halls will be restricted starting at 6 p.m. on Sunday, March 22, 2020. The expectation is that you return home or find other off campus housing options. Fellow believers, these are indeed trying times. Let me remind you that we serve a great God. He is trustworthy and merciful. We are trusting Him for this situation and for the decisions we must make as an institution.

Please continue to pray.

President Alan Cureton

March 13, 2020: President's Message

Dear University of Northwestern Community,

As we continue to monitor the spread of COVID-19, know that we are taking every precaution to ensure the health and safety of our students, faculty, and staff.

In order to do so, we have had to make difficult decisions, one of which is to suspend the start of on-campus classes until Monday, March 23 when all courses will move to ONLINE instruction. Students who are currently taking classes online will continue as scheduled.

At this point, our plan is to resume on-campus classes on Monday, April 6. Chapel will resume at this time as well.

Following guidelines set by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), the Minnesota Department of Health (MDH), and our own health services experts, we encourage our community members to limit exposure to the virus by setting appropriate physical distances in social situations. Also, avoid hand shaking, touching your face and be diligent about washing your hands.

What you should know:

  • On-campus classes are suspended until Monday, March 23, when they will resume in an ONLINE format
  • Current online classes will continue as scheduled
  • On-campus classes are intended to resume Monday, April 6

To answer questions about COVID-19’s effect on our campus, I have included a summary of decisions made by the UNW Pandemic Response Team pertaining to areas of campus life at Northwestern:

  • HOUSING
    Residence Halls will remain open. Students who are currently staying off campus are encouraged not to return to campus until April 3 if at all possible. Students who are currently staying on campus and have the ability to go home or find other temporary off-campus housing options are encouraged to do so. However, Northwestern recognizes that some students have limited housing options and that staying on campus is necessary at this time. Those students will be allowed to stay on campus.
  • DINING
    UNW Dining Services will be available for students and will remain open as long as we have students living on campus. Information regarding adjusted dining hours and locations during this time will be available soon.
  • STUDENT SERVICES
    Further communication will be forthcoming about the operation of student services, including Counseling Services.
  • TRAVEL
    RETURNING TRAVELERS: If within the 14 days of your return from any domestic or international travel you develop symptoms associated with coronavirus, contact your local provider, urgent care, or emergency care for direction, and then notify the UNWSP Health Services. Faculty and staff should notify your supervisor and Human Resources. Effective Monday, March 16, Health Services will not accept walk-in patients; you will need to call first to schedule an appointment.

    UNW TRAVEL: We are suspending all non-essential university-sponsored domestic and international air, bus, and train travel effective March 16 through May 31. A faculty or staff member who believes that he/she has an essential business reason to travel can seek approval through their division leader, but the expectation is that such approval will be rare.

    PERSONAL TRAVEL: We urge all travelers to carefully consider their intended destinations for personal trips. Review restrictions or quarantine measures that may be recommended or required by the CDC upon arrival or return. Personal international travel is strongly discouraged.
  • EVENTS
    We are in the process of evaluating the potential risk different campus events pose to the community. Student activities are cancelled through April 6.
  • ATHLETICS
    Neither the NCAA nor the Upper Midwest Athletic Conference (UMAC) has recommended canceling sporting events at this time. Spring sports and athletic competitions will continue as scheduled for student-athletes, coaches, training staff, and athletic administrators until further notice. Any spring sport student-athlete or staff member who does not feel comfortable staying on campus for practice or attending competitions will be supported in his/her decision to remain home. Spring athletes remaining on campus will do so under a condition of social distancing, which includes no visitors. Please continue to monitor unweagles.com for the latest information. All NCAA championships, meetings and conferences will proceed as scheduled.
  • STAFF
    Campus operations will continue and campus will remain open. All faculty and staff should report to work as scheduled. Human Resources will provide an FAQ regarding employment-related issues and benefit policies by the end of this week.

We will continue to monitor the changing dynamics related to COVID-19 and make future decisions accordingly. As soon as we are able, we will resume classes and all activities that make Northwestern an amazing institution.

I understand that this is a difficult and uncertain time for our campus, state, nation, and world. It is our utmost goal to protect you – our students, faculty, and staff. February 28, 2020: President's Message

Dear University of Northwestern Community,

We understand you may have questions and concerns regarding the coronavirus outbreak.

As you are aware, there are confirmed cases around the world as well as in the United States. On February 26, updated information from the Center for Disease Control (CDC) website stated, “…this virus is not currently spreading in the community in the United States.”

As we approach travel season during our upcoming spring break, we recommend appropriate prevention methods in accordance with the CDC, including:

  • Avoid close contact with people who are sick;
  • Cover your cough with your elbow or sneeze into a tissue, then discard the tissue in a trash receptacle and follow up with hand sanitizer that is at least 60% alcohol;
  • Avoid touching your face, eyes, nose, and mouth;
  • Wash your hands frequently for at least 20 seconds; and
  • If soap and water are not available, use hand sanitizer.

Coronavirus can cause typical cold and flu-like symptoms. However, individuals can be infected with coronavirus without displaying symptoms. In the elderly or individuals with a compromised immune system, it can cause more severe symptoms and complications, including death.

Please review the symptoms related to the coronavirus via the CDC and Minnesota Health links below. If you experience fever, cough, shortness of breath, or are exposed to anyone with a confirmed case of coronavirus, we recommend that you call UNWSP Health Services or your primary care provider during business hours for further direction. After hours and on weekends, please call a local urgent care or emergency care facility. Do not physically come to UNWSP Health Services with these symptoms.

In the event of a local coronavirus outbreak, all walk-in services will be suspended. Students will only be seen after an appointment is scheduled by phone. Health Services will continue to see students for all other illnesses (including influenza) and injuries at this time.

Our utmost concern is for the health and safety of our UNW community. Because of the diligent work of the Emergency Response Team, Northwestern has specific action plans in place to proactively respond to the health and safety needs of our community. Leadership will continue to closely monitor, update, and modify action plans and communication should the coronavirus arrive in Minnesota, or more importantly, on campus. We will continually update you as plans evolve. Please be aware that our plans may also be directed by the CDC and the Minnesota Department of Health (MDH).
