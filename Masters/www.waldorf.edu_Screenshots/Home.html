Guidance for Online Students

All courses for online degree programs will continue without interruption. If you are impacted by COVID-19 or have any questions, please contact your student services representative.

Guidance for Residential Students

​​​​Coronavirus Update 7/2/2020:

Students. Please reference waldorf.edu/fall-arrivals for important move-in/check-in dates, times and additional information.

Don't forget we do have a COVID-19 FAQ page available. Please take a moment to visit this page, familiarize yourself with the subjects covered, and utilize this reference when you have questions.

4:45 p.m.

 

I'm sick. What should I do?

As is normal this time of year, we are seeing cases of influenza, colds and other respiratory illnesses in our students, faculty and staff. If you develop a fever, cough or difficulty breathing, if you have been in close contact with a person known to have COVID-19 or if you have recently traveled to an area with ongoing community spread, please call ahead before deciding to go in.

Students: Call the Student Health Center at 641.585.8157 or Nurse Mary at 641.590.0619

Faculty & Staff: Call the Student Health Center, Nurse Mary or call your primary care provider.

What to do to stay healthy?

Waldorf recommends the following CDC prevention guidelines, including:

  • Wash your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds several times a day
  • Avoid people who are sick
  • Cover your mouth and nose with the crook of your elbow or tissue when you cough or sneeze
  • Avoid touching your eyes, nose and mouth
  • Clean and disinfect frequently touched objects and surfaces
  • Stay home when you are sick, except to get medical care

Additional Information about COVID-19 can be found on the following websites:

  • Center for Disease Control
