COVID-19 (Coronavirus) Information

UW-Whitewater cares deeply about the health and safety of our community.

Senior leadership is meeting regularly regarding COVID-19 and has been doing so since mid-January. Campus leaders continue to follow the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, as well as county and state public health protocols for dealing with the virus. UW-Whitewater has extensive emergency management plans, which address these situations.

COVID-19 is not specific to an ethnicity or race—disease does not discriminate. Please remember our campus values as you interact with others. Racist behaviors, discrimination or stereotyping are not tolerated at UW–Whitewater. If you experience harassment or discrimination, students, faculty and staff are encouraged to file a hate bias report.

Is the university closed?

The university is not closed, but operations have been changed to prioritize the safety of our campus community and the continuation of course delivery.

To lessen the risk to our community as much as possible, UW-Whitewater has suspended face-to-face instruction, moving entirely to alternative delivery for the 2020 spring and summer terms. ICIT is offering a guide for working and learning remotely. 

All campus buildings have been locked since 5 p.m. Friday, March 20. This step will allow facilities staff to disinfect/sanitize buildings, while keeping them safe and secure. Buildings are accessible to those who have a key card that allows access into the buildings.

Public spaces such as the University Center, Williams Center, Andersen Library and the Community Engagement Center are closed to the public in keeping with recommendations to limit mass gatherings.

Is registration going to be delayed for Summer or Fall 2020 terms?

There is no change in the timeline for registering. Reach out to your academic advisor for questions or concerns.

What’s happening with spring commencement?

A decision on spring commencement will be made at a later date, but given the gravity of this situation and the orders from Gov. Evers and the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention to limit gatherings to no more than 10 people, we may also be looking at an alternative delivery of commencement as well.

Remember, there is no ceremony requirement to receive your degree. If you are approved for graduation in May and complete your degree requirements, your graduation date will still be May 2020, even if we do not have traditional commencement ceremonies.

Again, no final decision has been made on spring commencement and we will announce the decision at a later date.

What about math placements or SOAR for incoming freshmen?

Placement tests will not need to be completed. SOAR dates, which begin May 28, are still in place. Transfer SOAR dates, April 10 and May 8, will be offered in an alternative format.

I missed Chancellor Watson’s Facebook Live sessions. Can I still watch them?

Chancellor Watson’s live stream sessions can found on the UW-Whitewater Facebook page, under the video tab. https://www.facebook.com/pg/uwwhitewater/videos/

What are the plans for the Fall 2020 semester?

It is the university’s intention to have students, faculty and staff return to both the Whitewater and Rock County campuses in the fall. Practices are still being established as we seek advice from faculty and staff on safety protocols, lowering classroom density, and self-protection. We plan to have our residence halls open, and teaching will resume in the classroom in modified and flexible ways. To provide safe environments for all members of our community, we will follow the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention’s guidelines, along with those of the state and local health departments, the State of Wisconsin and the UW System. A comprehensive plan is being created and will be shared in the coming weeks.

How do I change my class to Satisfactory/No Credit or withdraw from a course?

A summary of how to do these actions, and other relevant information can be found at on the Registrar’s page.

My class load includes student teaching, science labs, art studios, dance and music lessons or video production, etc. How will this work?

Please contact your instructor or department chair. They will have information specific to the needs based on your college. As long as students successfully complete courses, this change in delivery will not impact the path to graduation.

How do I access tutoring services?

Tutoring services are moving online. Webex and other media sources will be used to continue tutoring services.

I’m having Wi-Fi issues at home. What do I do?

Contact ICIT or the help desk at 262-472-4357 or helpdesk@uww.edu.

Will we be moving to a pass/fail grading system?

Undergraduate students will have the option to switch to “satisfactory” or “no credit” in most courses. Students will be able to make this decision by May 27, and they are urged to consult with their advisers before making their decisions.

How will I return my textbooks?

Whitewater campus Textbook Rental
The rental return deadline for the spring semester has been extended to June 2. For your safety, we recommend shipping your books to Textbook Rental via media mail.

Our address is 724 W. Starin Rd. Whitewater, WI 53190.

If you don’t want to ship, we will be open from 8 a.m. to 4:15 p.m. with a designated drop off area to safely return your books.

Rock County campus Textbook Rental
The rental return deadline for the spring semester has been extended to June 2. For your safety, we recommend shipping your books to Rock County Textbook Rental via media mail.

Our address is 2909 Kellogg Ave, Janesville, WI 53546

For any in-store pick up or drop off questions e-mail bookstore@uww.edu

How do I access library resources?

Library resources remain accessible even though the library buildings are closed. You can review the guide to library services and support.

Do I need to move out of the residence halls?

We ask all students to leave the residence halls and return home. We recognize that some students may be unable to return to their permanent residence for various reasons and will need to stay in their residence halls. For instance, some international students will not be able to return home at this time, and some may need to stay at UW-Whitewater for other reasons.

University Housing has developed an exemption process for our students. University Housing has emailed all residents with detailed information and directions regarding the check-out and exemption process. Dining opportunities will be available for those who remain on campus.

Will there be refunds for housing and dining?

University of Wisconsin System President Ray Cross announced that all UW System institutions will refund the prorated charges for housing and dining for the remainder of the spring semester for students who have vacated their respective universities. Prorated reimbursements will exclude the period of the originally scheduled spring break and shall be issued by the close of the spring academic semester.

Is the Williams Center open? I need to retrieve my belongings and I’d like to know if I’ll be reimbursed for a portion of my membership.

Williams Center is closed. If you need to collect your belongings, please call the Williams Center at 262-472-1145. Reimbursements are being discussed at the UW System level and information will be communicated as it is available.

Is Andersen Library open? How do I access their resources? What if I need to return a book?

Andersen Library is closed. Staff is still available to help, so please call 262-472-5511 and they will work with you to fulfill your needs. Due dates for books have been extended, so contact the library for more information on how to return your books.

I work an on-campus student job. Will I be able to work remotely?

Please contact your supervisor for further direction.

I’m not feeling well, what do I do?

If you are sick, stay home.

If you develop symptoms — such as a fever, cough or shortness of breath — within 14 days of your return from personal or official travel to a  country with a COVID-19 outbreak  OR have been in direct (face-to-face) contact with someone who has confirmed COVID-19, please take the steps listed below.

  • Call your health provider in advance.  Please do not show up at a clinic, urgent care or other health facility without calling first. Your provider will need to take special measures to protect other people in the clinic. Telemedicine may also be available, enabling you to consult a provider from home.
    • Students at UW-Whitewater can contact UHCS at 262-472-1300
    • Faculty or staff should contact their primary care provider

I feel anxious or worried about coronavirus. What should I do?

We understand that the situation can be concerning. If you would like to talk with someone, support is available to students through counseling services at UHCS 262-472-1305 or visit the UHCS website.

See information above about preventing the spread.

Where can I get tested for COVID-19?

University Health and Counseling Services does not provide coronavirus testing. Contact the Walworth County Health Department at 262-741-3445 for information on testing sites. The Wisconsin Department of Health Services also maintains a map with community testing sites at https://www.dhs.wisconsin.gov/covid-19/community-testing.htm.

What should I do if I have an underlying health condition or am immunosuppressed?

People with preexisting health conditions are at higher risk to develop complications from a COVID-19 infection. Your health is the top priority, so public health officials may recommend that you stay home if there are growing numbers of those infected with the virus.  

Should I wear a mask?

Please see guidelines for Cloth Face Covering (PDF).

I have family and friends in an area directly affected by the novel coronavirus. How can I manage my concern for them?

As with any natural or human-inflicted disaster, the COVID-19 outbreak presents an added layer of stress and worry for members of our UW-Whitewater community who have personal connections to the affected area. This is a critically-important time for all of us to reinforce a community of care on our campus and support one another. If you would like to talk with someone, support is available through counseling services at 262-472-1305.

How can I continue my counseling sessions?

Counseling sessions have been moved to phone calls. If you need information on how to set up a counseling session, please call 262-472-1300.

Are there free meals available?

Please know that there are free meals and food resources available within the community and at our Warhawk Pantry in Drumlin Hall. Review the list of free food and meal options in Whitewater.

The Wisconsin Dept. of Health Services COVID-19 website has a section on General Travel Guidance. 

All university-sponsored travel is cancelled until further notice, with limited exceptions from deans, vice chancellors and the athletic director.

We strongly advise you to reconsider non-essential personal travel, including travel over Spring Break. Documented cases are growing rapidly both domestically and internationally. You may face a higher risk of infection, significant delays returning, and/or the requirement to self-isolate or self-quarantine upon your return, all of which could significantly impact your professional and personal obligations at great individual expense.

All campus community members should be aware that if you travel, you may be required to self-quarantine for 14 days depending on where you’re traveling to and from, even if you do not exhibit symptoms.
