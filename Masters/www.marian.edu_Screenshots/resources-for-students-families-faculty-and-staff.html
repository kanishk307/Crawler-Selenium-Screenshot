Emergency federal CARES Act funds

Marian University completed, signed, and submitted the 2020 CARES HEERF Certification and Agreement indicating that the institution agrees to utilize the designated funds received under Section 18004(a)(1) of the CARES Act to provide Emergency Financial Aid Grants to students.

Upon submittal of this form, Marian University received $902,636 to be used to provide emergency financial aid grants  directly to students, as mandated by the guidelines.

As of May 13, 2020, Marian opened its CARES Act application for eligible students to apply for the emergency grant funding. Marian began distributing the CARES Act funds to students on May 20, 2020.

Marian estimated about 2,500 students would be eligible to receive funding. Marian required that students were Title IV eligible and met the requirements mandated by the CARES Act to determine eligibility for students.

The max amount of funding that could be awarded to an individual student was based upon the maximum Pell grant amount that students could receive for the 2019-2020 year.

As of June 29, 2020, Marian has distributed $748,636 in funding to about 490 students. Determination of eligibility for awarding was based on an application created by the university.

At this time, the application for the CARES Act funds at Marian University has been closed.

  • Registration

    For more resources related to registration for summer 2020 and fall 2020 courses visit the Office of the Registrar.

  • Withdrawing from a Course Before March 27

    The university has extended the withdraw from a course deadline to Thursday, April 23, 2020 for all semester long courses. No withdrawals will be allowed after this date.

    Now through March 27, MUHUB has been set to allow all students to drop a course with a W grade for the spring 2020 semester, as long as no financial restrictions exist.

  • Withdrawing from a Course After March 27 but before April 23

    After March 27, the Office of the Registrar will accept an email from students to drop a course with a W grade. It is the students responsibility to email the office from their MU email with the course they need to drop. The Office of the Registrar expects that students communicate the course they plan to drop with their advisor and instructor. The Office of the Registrar will not require scheduling forms be signed for spring 2020 drops. All emails should be sent to regis@marian.edu.

  • Schedule Overrides

    Students needing registration overrides due to schedule conflicts for summer or fall 2020 will need to be registered (not planned) in classes before any registration overrides. The Office of the Registrar is accepting emails for this, too, but we will require that appropriate individuals (i.e. instructors and advisors) are ccd on the email to the office, especially as it relates to time conflicts.

  • Options to Help You Register and Manage your Student Account

    Marian University is committed doing what is best for its students and their families. While these are temporary adjustments, we hope that they provide options to assist you in navigating a very difficult time in the life of our community.

    Effective immediately, financial restrictions that normally prevent registration for summer and/or fall 2020 have been adjusted for Marian University students who are registered for spring 2020 courses. The specific adjustments are noted below. Please register today for upcoming classes!

    Students in all programs other than the MAP program and MAP guest students may register for summer or fall 2020 courses with an overall unpaid balance of up to and including $1,500.00.

    • This temporarily replaces the policy that allows registration for any student in this category to register with an unpaid overall balance of up to $500.00.
    • Students may carry up to a $1,500.00 overall balance beyond the July 15, 2020 published due date for tuition and fees.
    • Students who are required to be clear through the Knight Quest clearance process (traditional undergraduates and St. Joe Indy students) must have the full balance due to the University paid in full or set up on a payment plan with Marians approved payment plan provider by the assigned Knight Quest date.
    • Students who are not required to be clear through the Knight Quest clearance process must have the full balance due to the University paid in full or set up on a payment plan with Marians approved payment plan provider by Friday, August 21, 2020.

    Students in the MAP program may remain in the current term or session, as well as register for a future course with an overall unpaid balance of up to and including $1,215.00.

    • Courses will not be dropped for students with a balance of $1,215.00 or below.
    • Tuition over and above the $1,215.00 threshold remains due on the first day of each term or session.
    • This temporary policy begins immediately and will continue through the third summer term that begins on July 20, 2020.The policy of tuition being due on the first day of each term will resume with the first fall term beginning on August 24, 2020. The carryover balance must also be paid by August 24, 2020.

    Note: Guest students who are taking MAP courses must still have paid in full balances by the first day of each term or session to remain in courses. Guest students are not eligible for this temporary change.

    Effective immediately, Marian University has temporarily removed the assessment of credit card fees normally charged by our vendor when payments are made online for either a one-time tuition payment or a payment plan installment. This change is in effect through May 31, 2020.

    Additionally, you may now set up person proxy access for MUHUB. To get started, visit the Office of the Registrar's website for instructions. 

    Our team is working remotely from our individual homes to support you and respond to your questions and concerns. If you are concerned about your student account and would like to talk with a staff member, please email us at busoff@marian.edu or call 317.955.6020.  

Virtual Tutoring: Peer Tutoring, Speaking Studio, and Writing Center

Expand all Collapse all

  • Peer Tutoring

    All Tutoring services and Supplemental Instruction (SI) sessions are now exclusively online. There are no face-to-face, in-person tutoring sessions being offered. MU students can book appointments through our website.

    Book an appointment

    After booking an appointment, students should receive confirmation and a web link provided by the tutor for the session. The virtual appointment will use an online tool (GoBoard.com) and will require a working webcam and microphone. GoBoard is web-based and at no point should a student need to download any software. 

    Students or MU faculty and staff who have questions can reach out to mcheng@marian.edu or adwenger@marian.edu.

  • Speaking Studio

    The Speaking Studio is still here to help you harness your strengths through this challenging time. Currently, we are only offering online appointments (via Webex). You can schedule an appointment with one of our consultants at muspeakingstudio.setmore.com. Once you make an appointment, your consultant will contact you via email with more details about how to connect to their virtual conference room. Please email us at speakingstudio@marian.edu with questions or concerns and follow us on Instagram and Facebook for updates, tips, and tricks as we navigate this time together. Be well and speak on. More information about what were made of here: marian.edu/speakingstudio.

  • The Writing Center

    The Marian University Writing Center tutors are available to work virtually with students, staff, faculty, and Indianapolis community members during our regular hours (9 a.m.-9 p.m. Monday- Thursday, 11 a.m.-5 p.m. Friday, and 3-9 p.m. Sunday). You may schedule an appointment or walk-in virtually by visiting our website at www.marian.edu/writingcenter. After selecting a meeting time, a tutor will contact you with directions to join the virtual session. Undergraduate, graduate, and medical school tutors are available. Additionally, we will monitor our email during open hours. Contact us if you need anything at writingcenter@marian.edu.

      • Oldenburg Hall

      • Arena/Convocation Center
      • Drew Family Health and Fitness Center

      • Mother Theresa Hackelmeier Memorial Library

      Residence halls including Doyle Hall, University Hall, and Drew Hall are already on 24/7 card access for authorized residents only. In addition, access to Clare Hall for business purposes will require your Marian University ID. This will be re-assessed daily and any updates will be shared via email.

    • Undergraduate Student Organizations

      Student Government Association Executive Board elections open March 24 and close March 31.

      Student Organization Registration Renewal Training will occur online Tuesday, April 7, and Wednesday, April 8. One representative from each organization should attend. More information will be shared with student leaders closer to the date.

      Budgets requests were due by March 24 to Student Government Association via Connect: https://connect.marian.edu/actioncenter/organization/student-government-association-of-marian-university.

      All organizations should wrap up the semester by having officer elections/ transitions by the end of the academic year

      Please continue to meet virtually with your organization: Microsoft Teams, Google Hangout, and Zoom are all good resources.

      Even though students are not on campus, we can still plan for the fall semester. Register for the Fall Activities Fair today!

    • Rental/Book Returns

      FedEx Rental Return Label

      Students will receive a rental reminder email that includes a hassle-free option for students to download a free FedEx return shipping label and packing slip. This allows students to ship their rentals back to the store at no cost. Emails will be sent 14 days prior to the rental due date of May 1, 2020.  A second email reminder is sent 3 days prior, and the 3rd is received 1 day prior to the rental due date. 

      The free FedEx return label download is available for both in-store and online renters. The free FedEx return label can be downloaded multiple times, but used only once per rental due date. The free return label will NOT be available after the May 1, 2020 rental due date has passed. Students are instructed to write their return shipping address on the shipping label and the packing slip should be included in the box for easy identification at the store.

      Book Buyback Program

      Since this service is available all year long, no changes will occur with the book buyback program. Currently the bookstore will be open for any students who would like to come in and attempt to sell back their used textbooks. If for any reason the situation changes and the store is closed and/or students are unable to come into the store we advise you to keep the books you want to attempt to sell back. The buyback program is limited to Marian University students only and must be done in the store by the student.

      Marian University Bookstore

      The bookstore is currently closed to the public, but the staff are here to answer your questions via phone or email from 11 a.m.-4 p.m. Monday through Friday. All orders must be placed online, and the bookstore website is offering FREE GROUND SHIPPING on all orders to mitigate in-person contact. Please visit the bookstore website for updates or call (317) 955-6314 with questions.

    • Mail Room

      At the time, the Marian University Mail Room is not open to the public. 

      Residential Students Who have Left Campus Housing:
      Mail and packages for students who have moved to their homes or other places has been forwarded to the address on record as the student's permanent, home address. Whenever possible, packages that have been forwarded to students have been forwarded in a manner that allows tracking. If you have questions about a specific package, email your question and the tracking number to mailroom@marian.edu.

      Residential Students who are Currently in Campus Residence Hall Housing, Overlook Residents and Campus Apartment Residents:
      US mail will be delivered by a member of the Residence Life Staff to individual student mailboxes. Packages will be delivered via the campus package lockers (Pack City) located near the Dining Commons in Clare Hall. ID card access will allow all students access to Clare Hall between the hours of 7:30 a.m. and 7 p.m. daily. Package recipients will be notified via an email to their Marian University email. The email contains package retrieval information and directions. Packages that are too large for the package lockers will be kept in the mail room. To pick up a large package, email mailroom@marian.edu to schedule a time to do so.  

      There will be a campus and US mail receptacle outside the mail room in Clare Hall. The receptacle will be checked and emptied one time per week day between the hours of 8 a.m. and noon.     • What Marian is doing

      Marian University is committed to the health and wellness of our students, faculty, and staff. We are continuing to closely monitor the COVID-19 pandemic and are making decisions based on the most up-to-date health information. There are presently no diagnoses of COVID-19 on Marian Universitys campus, and the university continues to follow the guidance of our local, state, and federal agencies.

      The university has taken numerous precautions to protect students, faculty, and staff including:

      • Transitioned all academic instruction to online delivery.
      • Recommended that students move out of on-campus residence halls, and return to their permanent residences. Students who have a need to remain in the residence halls are being accommodated.
      • Offering online peer tutoring and related services.
      • Canceled practices for all student-athletes.
      • Closed the fitness center indefinitely.
      • Temporarily closed all group gathering areas on campus.
      • Supported students studying abroad in returning to the U.S.

      Marian Universitys Student Health Center remains open to serve students. Call (317) 955-6154 to schedule an appointment.

      To prevent the spread of COVID-19, students should follow the CDC guidelines:

      • Stay home if you are sick, except to get medical care.
      • Cover your mouth and nose with a tissue when you cough or sneeze or use the inside of your elbow.
      • Throw used tissues in the trash.
      • Practice social distancing. Do not meet in groups and do not participate in activities in crowded places.
      • Immediately wash your hands with soap and water for at least 20 seconds. If soap and water are not readily available, clean your hands with a hand sanitizer that contains at least 60% alcohol.
      • Clean AND disinfect frequently touched services daily. This includes tables, doorknobs, light switches, countertops, handles, desks, phones, keyboards, toilets, faucets, and sinks.
      • If surfaces are dirty, clean them: Use detergent or soap and water prior to disinfection.
      • Avoid contact with people who are sick.

    • Counseling and Consultation Services

      Understandably, the global crisis is taking an emotional toll on all around us. Please know that your Marian campus community is here to help and support you, and Counseling and Consultation services are available virtually. Call (317) 955-6150 to schedule an appointment.

      Additionally, here are some tips to help you manage stress or anxiety that can arise in the midst of uncertainty or fears about the ongoing health and safety of you and your loved ones. 

      • Acknowledge your feelings and emotions. Allow yourself time to reflect on what you are feeling and how you may be reacting to or coping with these feelings.
      • Create a routine. Try to develop a daily schedule to create structure and maintain some consistency to your days. Structure assists with containing anxiety.
      • Pay attention to positive news instead of only focusing on negative and fear-producing reports.
      • Seek accurate information from credible news sources. The Center for Disease Control and Prevention and your local or state public health agencies are examples of resources to access factual information.
      • Stay connected. Maintaining social connections can foster a sense of normalcy. Reaching out to people you trust is one of the best ways to reduce anxiety, depression, loneliness, and feelings of isolation during social distancing and quarantine. Connect with family and friends through the telephone, email, text messages, and social media. Talk face to face with friends and family through Facetime, Skype, or Zoom.
      • Practice calming rituals: Stay grounded in the present moment, which can help you maintain an internal sense of stability and balance when outside events feel threatening. Remember to focus on what you know is true, what is present in the moment, what is within your control versus what is not. This focus will assist you in both preventing and getting out of anxiety-fueled thought spirals.
