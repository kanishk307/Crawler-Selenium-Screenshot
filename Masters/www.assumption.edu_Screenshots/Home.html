A Proactive Approach to the COVID-19 Situation

University administrators are closely monitoring the rapidly changing international, national and local COVID-19 situation. Our approach to this global health crisis has been two-fold: promote and maintain the safety of our campus community and ensure the completion of academic programs with as few disruptions as possible. We take seriously the safety of our students and the campus community, a community that includes several individuals at high risk for serious illness from coronavirus.

Since the outbreak, Assumption has proactively responded to the coronavirus situation since concerns first arose in January:

  • Reminded students of flu prevention efforts
  • Suspended all University-related international travel and non-essential domestic travel
  • Urged students studying abroad to return to the United States
  • Temporarily suspended Rome Campus operations
  • Students studying in Florence through the AIFS program as well as the Rome students have returned to the U.S., are self-isolating for two weeks and may return to campus after the isolation period with a physician’s approval.
  • Strongly, and several times, discouraged members of our community from traveling outside of the country during spring break.
  • All public spaces, residence halls and bathrooms were thoroughly cleaned and disinfected during spring break
  • Informed those who did travel outside of the U.S. that they may not return to campus for 14 days following re-entry to the U.S.
  • Extended spring break by three days; temporarily transitioning to remote learning.
    • Offered on-campus housing to international students, students living more than five hours from campus and those with internships.
  • Extended remote learning to April 13; Spring sports canceled
  • Extended remote learning through the end of the semester; students invited to move out of residence halls; Commencement rescheduled to August
  • Supporting the needs of the Class of 2024
    • Extended the enrollment deposit deadline to June 1
    • Decreased enrollment deposit from $400 to $50
  • Created a $3 million fund to provide additional financial aid for students

COVID-19 Information

  • Students, faculty or staff who are not feeling well and are concerned they may have coronavirus should seek immediate medical attention. Individuals should contact their primary care doctor or The Wellness Center, explain their symptoms then schedule an appointment. After seeing a medical professional, they will work closely with the Massachusetts Department of Public Health to determine if the individual should be tested for coronavirus or if they should self-isolate. As a reminder, anytime members of the Assumption community feel ill, they should avoid contact with others. Students should contact their professors and Eloise Knowlton, Ph.D., Associate Vice President for Academic Affairs at 508-767-7486 with regard to missed classes.

    Members of the Assumption community should first call The Wellness Center rather than visiting the office without prior contact with staff.

  • Once coronavirus began to spread in the northern region of Italy, which is located a distance from our Rome Campus, the University suspended all University-sponsored and independent student travel to the Lombardy and Veneto regions of Italy. Shortly thereafter, the Tuscany region was added to the suspended travel list.

    Following a Level 3 and 4 Travel Advisory put into place urging individuals to reconsider travel and not travel to certain areas of the country, respectively, University officials made the decision to temporarily suspend the spring Rome program and is arranged for students to travel back to the United States. Since students did not receive the full Rome experience, Assumption will explore the possibility of providing this group of students a future opportunity to experience the Rome program in full. 

    14-Day Restriction from Campus Following International Travel

    Students returning to the United States from Assumption's campus in Rome, Italy were instructed to self-isolate at home for a minimum of 14 days, and are not permitted on campus during the 14-day period. Following the end of that self-isolation period, they will be welcomed back to campus if they have been cleared by their physician.

  • Assumption has offered to all students participating in study abroad the option to return to the United States to complete their academic program. Shortly thereafter, the Centers for Disease Control issued Guidance for Student Foreign Travel for Institutions of Higher Education. This guidance states, that colleges and universities should consider:

    • Postponing or canceling foreign exchange programs
    • Asking current program participants to return to the United States

    The CDC advises that “students may face unpredictable circumstances, travel restrictions, challenges in returning home or accessing health care while abroad.” Unlike our Rome Campus, we cannot dictate the operations of third-party study abroad providers. However, please know that Assumption students participating in these study abroad programs still have the option to return to the United States, where Assumption will develop a program that enables them to complete their course of study.

  • During this time of uncertainty with COVID-19, IBH remains focused on the well-being and health of Assumption employees.
      
    In addition to the resources that are available through the Portal, IBH is providing access to a recorded webinar on Emotional Health & Resilience: Strategies for Managing Stress, Anxiety and Fear with COVID-19. IBH has also published several new overview sheets that cover top-of-mind of topics such as managing stress, self-care, resilience and coping with anxiety. These materials are available to anyone to view and download.

    From all the associates at IBH, we remain strong in our vision to assist Assumpiton employees and help them navigate through times of crisis, and every day moving forward to help them their best.
