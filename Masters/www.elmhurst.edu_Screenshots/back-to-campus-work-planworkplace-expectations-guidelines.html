    Workplace Expectations and Guidelines

    All staff members are expected to fully comply with the policies, protocols and guidelines outlined in this document as part of Elmhurst College’s back to campus planning. Failure to do so may result in corrective action.

    Symptom Monitoring Requirement

    Staff members who have been permitted to return to the workplace must conduct symptom monitoring every day before reporting to work. You must be free of any symptoms potentially related to COVID-19 or have had an evaluation by your doctor or the campus Wellness Center.

    At this time, these symptoms include one or more of the following:

    • Cough
    • Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
    • Fever
    • Chills
    • Repeated shaking with chills
    • Runny nose or new sinus congestion
    • Muscle pain
    • Headache
    • Sore throat
    • Fatigue
    • New gastrointestinal symptoms
    • Loss of taste or smell

    If you have any symptoms, you must call your doctor for guidance and then update your supervisor accordingly. You should self-isolate until cleared by your doctor with a note approving your return to work.

    If you test positive for COVID-19, please notify the following individuals immediately (while medical information is confidential, it is acceptable to inform employees they may have come into contact with a fellow employee that has been exposed to the virus that causes COVID-19):

    • Your immediate supervisor.
    • Phil Riordan, Vice President for Student Affairs, phil.riordan@elmhurst.edu, and Marc Molina, Executive Director of Security and Emergency Management, marc.molina@elmhurst.edu.
    • Your area vice president (by email).

    Elevated Risk Factors

    According to the CDC, certain factors may put individuals at a higher risk for severe illness if they are infected with COVID-19. Those factors can include:

    • Older adults (age 65 and older)
    • People with HIV
    • Asthma (moderate to severe)
    • Chronic lung disease
    • Diabetes
    • Serious heart conditions
    • Chronic kidney disease being treated by dialysis
    • Severe obesity
    • Being immunocompromised

    Staff members whose health condition falls within one of the CDC high-risk categories or who are pregnant seeking workplace adjustment—and those who wish to seek ADA reasonable accommodations related to returning to the workplace—should contact their supervisor and the Human Resources Department at hr@elmhurst.edu. Reasonable accommodations will be provided as the employee continues to work from home.
