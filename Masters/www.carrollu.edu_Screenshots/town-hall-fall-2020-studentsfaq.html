Carroll University Student/Parent Fall 2020 Town Halls FAQs

Coronavirus (COVID-19) Update | Last updated June 22, 2020

We have curated and provided answers to the most frequently asked questions from the Student/Parent Town Halls held on May 27 and 28, 2020. We appreciate your patience as we continue to finalize our plans for Fall 2020.

Important Dates

Why is fall semester 2020 starting and ending earlier than usual?

If COVID-19 resurges in the fall as some have predicted, we want to minimize the impact of potential spread associated with our entire student body leaving, having multiple contact with others and then returning to campus. That is why we will be ending the semester on Thanksgiving break. We will hold classes on Labor Day and Fall Break as well. Please see details below.

  • Classes will begin on Monday, August 24th.
  • Classes will be held on Labor Day and Fall Break to allow in class instruction to conclude on Tuesday, November 24, 2020.
  • Reading Day will be Monday, November 30, 2020.
  • Finals (Online) will be held Tuesday, December 1 to Friday, December 4, 2020.

Will January 2021 classes be held?

Yes, those will be held as planned, January 4 – 15, 2021.

When will the 2021 spring semester start?

The spring semester is scheduled as planned for January 19 – April 27, 2021.

Will you adjust your plans if necessary?

Yes, due to the unpredictable nature of the COVID-19 virus and its spread, we must continue to be flexible and prepare for a variety of scenarios. We continue to constantly monitor the situation and our response and decision making will be guided by the American College Health Association (ACHA), the CDC and local Waukesha public health officials.

Housing

Will move-in look different this year?

Yes, to allow for social distancing, move-in will be done in small groups and in multiple phases. Students will sign up for and be assigned specific move-in dates/times. Our small groups will begin their move-in starting with first year students on August 17 – August 19, 2020. Returner move-in will occur from August 20 – 23, 2020.

How will COVID-19 impact the number of students living in residence halls?

Carroll will reduce density in its residence halls by offering single occupancy only, housing one student per bedroom, in traditional halls. Our goal is to balance the on-campus living experience with the health and safety of our students, faculty and staff informed by ACHA and CDC guidelines.

Why allow double occupancy in apartments/suites, but not traditional residence halls?

American College Health Association (ACHA) guidelines still recommend the safest campus housing, under the threat of COVID-19, is with single-room occupancy. Additional guidance suggests double occupancy is best undertaken when residents have at least 100 sq. ft. per person. This was the reason for our original decision. However, having received appeals by many of our students in apartments and suites to maintain their current living arrangement, we agreed to do so, but are asking that each person be required to sign a waiver, recognizing this is their choice of living arrangement.

What if I don’t want to live in double-occupancy housing?

If you are currently assigned to a double or triple occupancy room and prefer not to have a roommate please email your request to the Housing Office at housing@carrollu.edu by July 1 and our staff will work with you, one on one, to find the best solution.

Does the shift to single occupancy rooms only mean that more students will be allowed to live off campus?

Yes, to allow for the reduction in the number of spaces available for on-campus living, we will waive the 3-year residency requirement for the 2020-21 school year (only this year) for juniors and sophomores with 60 or more credits. Seniors will be exempt from the 3-year residency rule per usual.

What is the deadline for changing housing and deciding to live off campus?

The priority deadline for notification of your intent to cancel on-campus housing is July 1, 2020. This will assist in our efforts to accommodate requests for those who need to live on-campus. To do so, you must complete the cancellation form.

Once processed, you’ll be notified. Should you wish to apply to live off-campus, a new off-campus application will need to be submitted.

If I cancel my on-campus application, will I receive a refund on my housing deposit?

Students who cancel their on-campus housing application by the priority deadline of July 1 and have an approved off-campus application will receive a refund on the $250 deposit and will be exempt from the normal cancellation fees.

Can I have a roommate if I sign a waiver?

Yes, if you are living in apartments or suites only, we will agree to allow for roommates, but ask that each person be required to sign a waiver, recognizing this is their choice of living arrangement. More information about the waiver agreement will be available soon.

I’m in a traditional residence hall. Can I have a roommate?

Unfortunately, no. American College Health Association (ACHA) guidelines still recommend the safest campus housing, under the threat of COVID-19, is with single-room occupancy. Traditional residence halls will continue to have single occupancy to reduce density on each floor based on the number of students who will share restroom facilities.

Can my room assignment be next door to or across the hall from the person I was going to room with?

We will consider these requests when assigning rooms, but logistics may make that difficult to accommodate on a large scale. Unfortunately, we are unable to make any guarantees as to final location/proximity of room assignments.

Will traditional residence hall rates be reduced?

Students in traditional halls will be assigned a single room, however, will be charged the double room rate that would be typical of those accommodations. Students in suites and apartments will pay the standard 2020-2021 room rates as advertised.

Will international students be guaranteed a room on campus?

International students will be housed similarly to domestic students. For all students, this may be at an off-campus location.

When will I receive my room assignment?

Notification of room assignments will be sent out to students in late July.

Are guests (including parents) allowed to visit me in the residence halls?

We are asking that students do not invite outside guests, including parents, to the residence halls. Our goal is to minimize the flow of traffic through the residences and potential exposure to the COVID-19 virus. We would encourage you to meet guests to campus at an outdoor location and to observe all Carroll University safety protocols, including social distancing and wearing a mask.

How do you intend to address concerns in regard to shared bathrooms?

We are working with the Waukesha County Health Department to discuss health and safety protocols related to shared bathrooms.

Can freshmen have cars on campus?

We do not allow freshmen to bring cars to campus.

Who can I contact about housing if I have more questions?

The best way to contact us with any additional questions is to email the Housing Office at housing@carrollu.edu.

Dining

Will students who live on campus still be required to select a dining package?

Residential freshmen, sophomores and juniors are required to purchase a meal plan. Residential seniors are not required to purchase meal plans.

Will there be changes to the dining experience?

With health and safety top of mind, plans are underway to reduce density and change the way food is served in the Main Dining Room. We’re exploring a variety of options that will continue to provide the quality experience and convenience our students expect from their food service. That could include opening additional dining locations, offering mobile ordering along with pickup/delivery and more.

Tuition / Scholarships / Work Study / Campus Visits

Will there be any change in tuition?

There will be no changes to the tuition rates published for the 2020-21 academic year. Those fees were approved by the Carroll University Board of Trustees last year.

If I defer coming back in the fall semester, or for a year, what happens to my scholarship(s)?

We will hold them steady – but they are prorated for each semester. For example, a $10,000 scholarship would be applied over two semesters – a student deferring one semester would see a $5,000 credit for that scholarship.

Are there any changes to work study for students?

There are no changes planned to the work study program.

Will on-campus jobs be available this year? Can I live at home and do work study?

Yes, there will be on campus jobs available for students, and those will be open to both students living on campus as well as commuter students.

Will there be any impact if I have received a scholarship but decide to live at home rather than on campus?

No, but there may be some impact to need-based aid in some cases.

Will there be an opportunity for first year students to come to campus in person this summer to revisit?

Yes, our Admissions team will be offering opportunities for students to revisit campus in small groups. If you are interested in scheduling a visit, please reach out to your personal counselor or contact us at: admission@carrollu.edu.

Academics

How will clinicals be impacted?

We understand the clinical experience is critical to students in our health science programs. Our faculty in those programs are working diligently with our hospital system partners to determine clinical placements, however each hospital system is different. We will have more information in regard to those placements by July 1, 2020.

What are the plans and requirements for Cross Cultural Experiences (CCEs)?

All Fall 2020 study abroad and Carroll course CCE travel has been canceled. The in-class portion of the Carroll course CCEs will run even with travel canceled. We aim to achieve the class learning outcomes in alternate ways so that students can continue on with their four year plans including completing CCS400. All Winter 2021 study abroad and Carroll course CCEs have been canceled, including the in-class portion. Students should work with faculty and CCE advisors to discuss alternate options. We will delay the acceptance of Carroll course CCE applications for Spring 2021until September. More details will be sent via campus email.

Will classes be reduced in size to accommodate for social distancing? Will that impact my ability to get the classes I need?

We’re working with our facilities team to develop a plan to ensure that classrooms allow for appropriate social distancing between students. We will not remove a student from a course for which they are currently registered due to this reconfiguration. We will adjust course delivery as needed to accommodate those who are registered.

With safety protocols such as social distancing and the wearing of masks in public, how will students participate in programs such as theater, musical ensembles and marching band?

The visual and performing arts are, and will continue to be, an important part of the Carroll experience. Our faculty is committed to finding innovative ways to deliver these programs while prioritizing the health and safety of our students, faculty and staff. There will be more information and details on what those programs will look like in the coming weeks.

In addition to in-class instruction, will all classes be live streamed?

We are still in the process of finalizing our course delivery modes.

What if I don’t feel safe going into classrooms? Can I take classes online?

We are working with our facilities team to implement a variety of new protocols focusing on the health and safety of our students, faculty and staff. This includes reconfiguring classrooms to allow for social distancing, requiring masks to be worn in public spaces, providing additional hand sanitizing stations in buildings and cleaning with CDC-approved disinfectant. While the expectation is for in-class instruction, there will be a need for flexible delivery to ensure safety. In addition, a small number of courses may be delivered fully online.

Will there be any changes to convocations?

The convocation requirement at Carroll has not changed. As of now we are planning to host convocation events with appropriate social distancing measures while following current guidelines for gatherings. In addition, we plan to host all convocations on Microsoft Teams so that those who cannot attend in person are able to attend, and participate, virtually.

How will students participate in labs work and maintain social distancing?

We understand this will be a challenge and we are working on the best delivery of labs to minimize disruption to the educational experience while protecting the health and safety of our faculty and students.

Will academic supports such as tutoring /SI and academic advising be in person?

Yes, we will be providing in-person academic support and services this fall, with appropriate safety protocols in place. Hours and availability of services may have changed — it is recommended that students contact those offices directly for the most recent updates.

Commuter Students

How will you handle increased need for parking?

We understand the demand on parking will increase in anticipation of more students living off campus this year. We are exploring availability of off-campus parking to meet that demand.

If I commute, where will I hang out in between classes?

We are working with our facilities team to identify additional public spaces across campus that could be repurposed as additional lounge and study spaces for commuter students.

COVID-Specific Questions

Will I be required to wear a face mask? When and where?

The expectation is that all students, faculty and staff wear a face mask whenever they are in a public area of campus. This is in accordance with CDC guidelines. All students, faculty and staff will be supplied with one Pioneer Pride face covering. Students will not be asked to wear a face mask when inside their residence hall room. Students who reside in suite-style rooms or apartments will need to work together to determine the use of face coverings in their shared spaces.

How will you enforce the use of face masks and social distancing?

We are working on a public health campaign to be distributed over the summer. The campaign will show modeling of these behaviors and will educate students about the importance of protecting their own health and also being considerate of the impact their actions could have on the health of others. These protocols are aligned with the tenets of our Carroll Ethos — Respect, Integrity and Stewardship. If it becomes necessary, we will utilize the Carroll University conduct system to ensure that our students are following these important safety precautions.

If a student becomes ill and tests positive for COVID-19 what is the protocol? Will there be quarantine areas?

Any student testing positive for COVID-19 will be asked to isolate immediately. We will provide locations for temporary quarantine, but we encourage students who have tested positive to return home to quarantine.

Will COVID-19 tests be required before students arrive on campus?

We are still seeking guidance from public health authorities on our testing policy for students.

Will the campus clinic have expanded services, including COVID-19 testing?

We have not made any decisions yet but are working with public health authorities to explore the viability of adding COVID-19 testing to the services offered by the Student Health Center.

Will student’s temperatures be taken before entering a class?

We have not yet determined if this will be part of our safety protocols.

Miscellaneous

Will facilities such as the library, learning commons, RecSports, etc. be open?

We plan to have all facilities open this fall. There may be modifications to hours and number of individuals allowed to access those facilities at one time, to comply with social distancing guidelines. As we near the beginning of the fall semester, more information will be provided.

Do you plan to offer first year Orientation and the 6-week challenge?

Yes, although they may look a little different than in previous years. We are looking forward to welcoming our incoming students with a robust orientation program that will provide them with the tools they need for a successful college experience, including both social activities and learning opportunities.

Is there any word on fall sports?

The CCIW President’s Council continues to meet regularly and our athletic director is in contact with the NCAA for planning. While no firm plans have been announced, flexible scheduling and timing of seasons is being discussed. We will send out updated information on this as soon as it becomes available.

First. Foremost. Forward.
