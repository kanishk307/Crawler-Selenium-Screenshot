Carroll University Spring Break Extended Update

Coronavirus (COVID-19) Update March 11, 2020

Dear Carroll Colleagues, Students and Parents,

With rising concerns in regard to the spread of COVID-19, I am reaching out to inform you that Carroll University is extending spring break for students through Friday, March 20. We plan to resume in-person classes on Monday, March 23. This decision has been made to help ensure the health and safety of our campus community. We currently have no suspected nor confirmed cases of the virus on campus. News reports earlier today indicate there is one confirmed case in Waukesha County.

We appreciate your patience and will continue to update you as this situation evolves. You will be notified by Friday, March 20 if plans change in regard to the resumption of in-person classes on March 23.

Residence Hall Students
Students will be able to return to their residence halls on Sunday, March 22 starting at 12:00pm. If you are currently staying in a campus residence hall and are unable to make arrangements to return or stay home from Sunday March 15 through Sunday March 22 at noon, or if you need to retrieve items from your room, please contact housing@carrollu.edu.

Any Carroll University students who are unable to return or stay home and are experiencing flu-like symptoms should call ahead to Carroll’s Student Health Center at 262.524.7233 or contact them via email at healthcenter@carrollu.edu to arrange a consultation.

Clinical Placements
Students with clinical placements scheduled to resume next week will be contacted by their program faculty with further information.

Student Athletes
CCIW presidents are meeting tomorrow morning to make a decision on the future of athletic competitions. Additional information will be forthcoming.

Campus Activities
All Carroll University student events and activities scheduled for next week are canceled, with the exception of athletic competitions. CCIW updates are anticipated tomorrow, as indicated above.

Staff and Faculty
Faculty and staff are expected to report to work as usual. We are following CDC guidelines and are in close contact with authorities from Waukesha County Public Health.

The symptoms of COVID-19 and flu or other respiratory illness may be similar, including fever, cough and body aches. Please do not come to work if you are feeling ill. Carroll employees who are experiencing flu-like symptoms are advised to contact their health care provider directly. If you have specific questions or concerns, please contact your supervisor directly.

What do students, faculty and staff need to know in regard to spring break travel?

Employees or students who may have visited an area designated as a Level 3 Travel Health Notice in the past 14 days are asked to follow these CDC guidelines, which include staying home and self-quarantining for 14 days prior to returning to Carroll, even if you are not experiencing any symptoms. If this applies to you and you are a Carroll employee, please notify your supervisor directly. Students should notify the Student Health Center and follow up with their individual faculty members to make arrangements, if possible, to continue their studies during their period of self-quarantine, which would extend past the date of our anticipated resumption of in-person classes on March 23.

We ask that all employees contact their supervisors directly to review any upcoming travel plans for university-related business.

What are we doing to keep campus healthy?

Carroll is following CDC and Waukesha County Public Health guidelines in regard to COVID-19. Clinical protocols will be adjusted as these organizations issue new guidelines related to screening or testing to rapidly identify, evaluate and support students, faculty and staff.

Facilities staff are implementing additional environmental cleaning procedures, including new hand sanitizing stands in building lobbies. We are also posting CDC guidelines with information on precautions that individuals should take to stay safe and well throughout campus.

What is the impact to University-sponsored international travel?

In alignment with CDC guidelines, we are canceling university-sponsored travel to any country that is at a Level 3 Travel Health Notice status. We recognize that this situation evolves daily, and therefore reserve the right to make necessary adjustments on a course-by-course basis. Notification has been sent to students to inform them that all Cross-Cultural Experience travel to Italy scheduled for this spring and summer, as well as university-sponsored study abroad trips to Italy, are canceled.

We are closely monitoring this situation and how it may impact the remainder of our faculty-led Cross-Cultural Experiences and study abroad programs. Should further cancelations be necessary, participants will be notified.

How is Carroll preparing in the event that classes need to shift to online delivery?

We are working with our faculty to prepare them for the possibility of online delivery of classes, should that become necessary at any point during the remainder of the spring semester. Our intent is to minimize any disruption in the academic experience while continuing to deliver excellence in instructional quality.

I have more questions – where can I find information?

We have created a webpage with links to information from state, national and international public health authorities, as well as Carroll-specific COVID-19 updates.

We realize you may have additional concerns or questions not addressed here. Please contact us at COVID-19info@carrollu.edu and we will direct those to the most appropriate area/individual.

Sincerely,

Cindy Gnadinger
President
