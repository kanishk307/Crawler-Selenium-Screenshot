Fall 2020 Students and Parents Town Hall

Find answers to the most frequently asked questions from the Student/Parent Town Halls held on May 27 and 28, 2020.

Meeting Summary

Carroll University Students and Parents,

Thank you to those of you who were able to join President Gnadinger for the Student and Parent Town Halls held on May 27 and 28, 2020. A number of important updates in regard to the fall 2020 semester were shared. While these updates are summarized below, if you were unable to attend, you can find a recording of the Town Hall at:

Town Hall Meeting — Fall 2020 Students and Parents — May 27, 2020

Our Fall 2020 Planning Task Force has been working diligently, with the goal of bringing students back to campus in the fall, while keeping the health and safety of our campus as our top priority. We are following the guidelines of the American College Health Association and the CDC, as well as consulting with public health officials as we develop our plans. The complexities and unknowns associated with COVID-19 mean we need to be flexible in our thinking, and that this semester will look different than other fall semesters.

In the coming weeks, we will continue to share further details in regard to the updates below, so that you can plan for those changes. Please make sure to check your email regularly. If you have questions in the meantime, please send those to:

Housing: housing@carrollu.edu
Admissions: admission@carrollu.edu
Student Affairs/Student Life: studentaffairs@carrollu.edu

Academic Calendar Changes

We will be shifting the academic calendar to start and end the fall semester earlier than usual. If COVID-19 resurges in the fall as some have predicted, we want to minimize the impact of potential spread associated with our entire student body leaving, having multiple contact with others and then returning to campus. That is why we will be ending the semester on Thanksgiving break. We will hold classes on Labor Day and Fall Break as well. Please see details below.

  • Move-in days for first year students will take place from August 17–19, 2020.
  • Move-in days for returning students will take place from August 20–23, 2020.
  • Classes will begin on Monday, August 24, 2020.
  • Classes will be held on Labor Day and Fall Break to allow in class instruction to conclude on Tuesday, November 24, 2020.
  • Reading Day will be Monday, November 30, 2020.
  • Finals (Online) will be held Tuesday, December 1 to Friday, December 4, 2020.

Academic Delivery

In class instruction will be the expectation. However, we will need to be flexible in delivery and to quickly adapt, and pivot, when necessary. There are exceptions that may apply when considering building and room density. We’re working with our Office of Information Technology to explore the capability for live streaming and lecture capturing.

Housing

Last updated June 22, 2020

We heard from many of you and understand the concerns you expressed about being relocated from double occupancy rooms in apartments or suites. After thoughtful discussion among our Fall 2020 Planning Task Force, we have adjusted our housing model to permit double occupancy in apartments and double suites. Traditional residence halls will continue to have single occupancy to reduce density on each floor based on the number of students who will share restroom facilities.

Students in traditional halls will be assigned a single room, however, will be charged the double room rate that would be typical of those accommodations. Students in suites and apartments will pay the standard 2020-2021 room rates as advertised.

Please understand the American College Health Association (ACHA) guidelines still recommend the safest campus housing, under the threat of COVID-19, is with single-room occupancy. Additional guidance suggests double occupancy is best undertaken when residents have at least 100 sq. ft. per person. This was the reason for our original decision. However, having received appeals by many of our students in apartments and suites to maintain their current living arrangement, we will agree to do so, but ask that each person be required to sign a waiver, recognizing this is their choice of living arrangement.

To allow for the reduction in the number of spaces available for on-campus living, we will continue to waive the 3-year residency requirement for the 2020-21 school year (only this year), and deposits will be returned to those students who cancel their on-campus housing application by the priority deadline of July 1, 2020 and have an approved off-campus application.

Dining

Campus dining will also be modified, with health and safety as a top priority. We are planning to add multiple distribution and seating locations for existing dining options. We are also considering movable ordering, delivery, grab and go and new pick up locations. Other considerations include creative solutions to finding additional and expanded dining options.

To minimize MDR traffic, the limited dining room seating that will be available will be reserved for students only.

Athletics

The CCIW President’s Council continues to meet regularly and our athletic director is in contact with the NCAA for planning. While no firm plans have been announced, flexible scheduling and timing of seasons is being discussed. We will send out updated information on this as soon as it becomes available.

New Protocols on Campus

As we prepare to return to campus this fall, we are establishing new protocols designed to keep our students, employees and guests safe and healthy.

  • Face coverings will be required when in public areas. We are ordering Pio Pride face coverings that will be available this fall. 
  • We will continue social distancing practices.
  • Classroom seating will follow CDC guidelines. 
  • Frequency of cleaning and cleaning materials will follow CDC guidelines.
  • Student activities will follow CDC guidelines.
  • Greek Life, Student Organizations, Student Senate will all be active in various formats. 
  • Testing and contact tracing are being discussed and planned with Waukesha County Public Health Department.

We appreciate your patience and understanding as we plan for a semester guided by the health and safety of our campus community, first and foremost. We’re looking forward to welcoming our students back in the fall.

Sincerely,

Dr. Theresa Barry
Vice President of Student Affairs
