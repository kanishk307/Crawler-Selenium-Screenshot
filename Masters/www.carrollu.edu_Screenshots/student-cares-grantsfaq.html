Carroll University CARES Act Emergency Student Grants FAQs

What is the CARES Act?

The Coronavirus Aid, Relief, and Economic Security Act (CARES Act) was signed into law on March 27, 2020. Part of the CARES Act, the Higher Education Emergency Relief Fund (HEERF), allocated money to higher education institutions like Carroll University impacted by the COVID-19 pandemic. Carroll is required by the CARES Act to use at least 50% of this funding to provide emergency grants to students for expenses caused by the disruption of campus operations due to the pandemic.

What expenses can I include in my CARES Act grant application?

Eligible expenses are unforeseen things you had to pay for related to your cost of attendance, such as food, housing, course materials, technology, health care, and child-care, which were caused by the disruption to your semester. According to the Department of Education, tuition and lost wages are not eligible expenses. You should only include expenses you incurred on or after March 13.

Do I need to fill out an application to be considered for a CARES Act grant?

Yes. Eligible students will be asked to complete a brief application to identify which type of eligible expenses, and the total dollar amount of eligible expenses, the student has incurred since March 13, 2020, which is the first date expenses can be claimed under the CARES Act.

How do I apply for a CARES Act student grant?

Eligible students received an email via their Carroll University email address. The email will contain a link to the CARES Act emergency student grant application. A second round of funding is now available and eligble students will receive an email via their Carroll University email address on Tuesday, June 23, 2020. Please contact the Carroll University Help Desk at 262.524.7229 or helpdesk@carrollu.edu if you cannot access your Carroll email.

Is there a deadline to apply for a CARES Act grant?

Applications for the second round will be accepted through July 15, 2020. Applications will be reviewed and awarded based on expenses incurred, financial need, and availability of remaining grant funds.

Who is eligible to receive a CARES Act grant?

All current students (undergraduate, graduate and part-time) enrolled in Spring/Summer 2020 affected by the COVID-19 pandemic who have successfully completed a 2019-20 FAFSA (or are eligible to file a FAFSA) are eligible to receive CARES Act Funds. The U.S. Department of Education limited these grants to students who are eligible for Title IV federal aid. As a result, the university cannot give these funds to DACA, undocumented or international students.

The second CARES Act emergency grant application is available to eligible returning Carroll students who already applied for CARES Act emergency grant funds, however those students may only request funds for new expenses incurred after their original request. Eligible students who are newly enrolled for Summer 2020 classes or first-time requests from returning students will also be considered. 

What are my options if I’m not eligible for Title IV federal aid?

Students who are not eligible for Title IV, such as DACA, undocumented or international students, may contact the Chaplain’s Office at chaplain@carrollu.edu to request financial assistance through other emergency funds available to students in need. Carroll University’s Student Emergency Fund, unrelated to CARES Act grants, is open to all students.

What if I think that I am eligible for Title IV federal aid, but did not file a 2019-20 FAFSA?

Students who are eligible for federal aid but who have not yet filed a 2019-2020 FAFSA can still do so at studentaid.gov. Please contact the Carroll University Financial Aid office at finaid@carrollu.edu if you have questions about filing a FAFSA. Only students who have filed a FAFSA will be eligible for a CARES Act emergency student grant.

How much funding is available?

The emergency grants will range from $150 to $1000. In order to assist as many students as possible, $1000 is the maximum amount that any student will receive through a CARES Act emergency student grant.

Do I need to submit receipts or document how I spent my grant?

No. Students may want retain documentation for tax purposes, but will not be asked to submit receipts documenting their expenses to the university.

How will I receive the CARES Act grant funds?

Students who have direct deposit information on file with the Carroll University Business Office will receive their CARES Act emergency grant funds via direct deposit. Students who have not provided direct deposit information will receive a check that will be mailed to the permanent address on file. Students who have relocated should update their address now by emailing reg@carrollu.edu to make sure they receive their grant.

Do I need to pay back the CARES Act grant funds I receive?

No. These funds are to assist you and will not need to be repaid to the university.

Do I need to keep track of how I use the CARES Act grant funds I receive?

No. students who receive CARES Act emergency grant funds are not required to record or track how it is spent.

Can I submit more than one application?

No. Students should submit only one application.

How will I be notified if I receive CARES Act grant funds?

Students who apply for and receive CARES Act emergency grants funds will be notified via their Carroll email address.

If I have an outstanding balance, will my CARES Act grant be applied directly to my student account?

No. The university will not apply any CARES Act emergency grant funds towards an outstanding balance on a student’s account.

Will receiving CARES Act grant funds impact my NCAA eligibility?

CARES Act funds do not adversely affect NCAA eligibility. Student athletes and non-athletes are encouraged to apply If they are eligible and have a qualifying situation.

Are the CARES Act grant funds taxable?

The CARES Act emergency grant funds may be taxable. Carroll University is currently waiting for additional guidance regarding taxability of these funds.

Who should I contact if I have other questions?

If you have other questions, please email covid-19info@carrollu.edu.
