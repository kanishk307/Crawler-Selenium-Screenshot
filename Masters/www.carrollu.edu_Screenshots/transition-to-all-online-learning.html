Carroll University Transitioning to All Online Learning

Coronavirus (COVID-19) Update March 13, 2020

Dear Carroll Colleagues, Students and Parents,

In the past 48 hours, concerns regarding the spread of the COVD-19 pandemic have grown. The health and safety of our campus community is always our top priority. Guided by that focus, we have made the decision to transition all learning to online instruction beginning Monday, March 23, and to continue it through April 5. Our extended spring break will continue as planned through Friday, March 20. As of today, we are planning to resume face-to-face classes on Monday, April 6. Students may return to their residence halls on Sunday, April 5 at noon, see details below. Due to the rapidly-changing scope of this pandemic and its impact, we urge you to check your campus email frequently for updates. Those will also be posted here on this website.

We realize you may have additional concerns or questions not addressed here. Please contact us at COVID-19info@carrollu.edu and we will direct those to the most appropriate area/individual.

Making the Transition to Online Learning

You will be contacted soon by your professor(s) for specific instruction about your courses and how the move to online learning will impact your curriculum. Our Information Technology Services team is ready to serve as a resource to assist you in navigating this transition.  The Information Technology team is updating the portal with additional resources and links to enable students and faculty to access tools needed for online instruction and learning. We are committed to continuing to deliver the quality academic instruction you expect from Carroll University.

We realize there are courses that involve labs, clinicals, performances or other assignments that may be challenging to deliver online. Our faculty are working diligently to accommodate these unprecedented circumstances, which will require adaptability and flexibility in the ways we meet course requirements.

Class Registration/Drop Date/Incomplete Date

NOTE: class registration will take place as planned. No changes have been made to the registration schedule.

The drop date for the spring semester has been extended one week, to April 6.

For those students who were granted an incomplete during the fall 2019 semester, the date to complete the work has been extended to March 27.

Residence Hall Students

At this time, we are planning for students to return to their residence halls on Sunday, April 5, starting at 12 p.m. If you are currently staying in a campus residence hall and are unable to make arrangements to return or stay home from Sunday, March 15, through Sunday, April 5, at noon, please contact housing@carrollu.edu. If you need to retrieve items from your room, you’ll find more information on the process here.

Limited dining services will be available for those who remain on campus during this time.

We understand there will be questions in regard to the financial impact of the move to online learning, including questions of housing and meal plan costs. We appreciate your patience in this quickly-changing situation. Know that we are working to provide you with further updates and information as quickly as possible.

Any Carroll University students who are unable to return or stay home and are experiencing flu-like symptoms should call ahead to Carroll’s Student Health Center at 262.524.7233 or contact it via email at healthcenter@carrollu.edu to arrange a consultation.

All Cross-Cultural Experience Travel Canceled

Due to the growing scope of the COVID-19 pandemic and the unpredictability associated with its spread, Carroll has canceled the travel portion of all spring 2020 Cross-Cultural Experiences (CCEs). The grade and credit for this course will be awarded based solely on the class work students complete during the regular semester. Students should continue participating in that class, as successful completion of the course will fulfill the CCE requirement. Any payments made to the university for the travel costs associated with this course will be refunded in full. Students who were planning to participate in these experiences have been contacted directly with details by the Associate Dean for General Education.

Athletics

The Presidents of the nine member institutions of the College Conference of Illinois and Wisconsin (CCIW) have met on several occasions via teleconference to determine the best course of action for our institutions, student-athletes, coaches and staff. The CCIW Council of Presidents have made the difficult decision to cancel all conference competition for the remainder of the winter and spring seasons.

Campus Events

All Carroll University student events and activities scheduled through Saturday, April 4, are canceled. All other campus events with anticipated attendance of more than 250 people, per recommendations by the Wisconsin Public Health Department, are canceled until further notice.

Faculty and Staff – Safety Precautions

Faculty and staff will report to work as usual. Any faculty or staff who have traveled to an international location or domestic state with known community spread must self-quarantine for 14 days. Please contact your supervisor directly if this applies to you to discuss remote work options.

All employees must contact their supervisor directly to review any university-related travel plans. The Department of Human Resources will be sending additional information regarding the allowance of sick time, increased flexibility and working remotely.

Please Continue to Check Often for Regular Updates

We will continue to update you via email and on our website in regard to the impact this extraordinary situation will have on our campus community. We appreciate your understanding and patience as we make the best decisions for our campus, with its continued health, safety and well-being our primary focus.

Sincerely,

Cindy Gnadinger
President
