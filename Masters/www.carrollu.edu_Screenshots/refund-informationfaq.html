How does the housing and meal plan credit work?

Housing and meal plan credits will be applied first to any outstanding balance on a student account. If a credit balance results following the housing and/or meal plan credit, it will carry forward to be applied to expenses in a future term, either summer or fall 2020. Graduating seniors and students who do not return in fall 2020 will receive a refund check for any credit balance.

When will credits or refund checks be issued/received?

Credits for housing and meal plans were posted to student accounts in April. Refunds were processed for graduating seniors in early May. Refunds for non-returning students will be processed after the fall semester begins or upon official notification of withdrawal, if sooner.

I’ll be living off campus next year. Will my housing and meal plan credit be applied to tuition?

Yes. Housing and meal plan credits will be available toward any charges incurred in the next term in which a student enrolls. (summer or fall 2020) They do not have to apply just to future housing or meal plan charges.

What if I don’t return next semester?

Refund checks for any students other than graduating seniors who do not return in fall 2020 will be processed after the fall semester begins.

I would rather have a refund check than a credit toward future charges. Is that possible?

Refund checks will be available only to graduating seniors and students who do not return for fall 2020.

I’m paying interest on student loans. Can I get a refund check rather than a credit so that I can pay on my loans?

The federal government is offering various relief opportunities for federal student loans, including suspending interest and payments. Please visit studentaid.gov/announcements-events/coronavirus for further information.

Will credits be applied to summer?

Yes. Housing and meal plan credits will be applied to the next term in which a student incurs expenses, either summer or fall 2020.

I’m graduating but returning as a graduate student this fall. Can my credit be applied to graduate school?

Credit balances will be issued as refund checks to graduating seniors. If you wish to apply your credit toward fall 2020 graduate charges, please contact the business office at busoff@carrollu.edu and they can assist you further.

Will meal plan credits be prorated based on actual usage?

No. The meal plan refund will be 50% of the cost of the current meal plan purchased.

Will there be any difference in credit made for meals vs. dining dollars?

Meal plan credits will not differentiate between meals and dining dollars. Again, the meal plan credit will be calculated as 50% of the cost of the current meal plan purchased.

Refund Questions Other Than Housing and Meal Plans

Will there be any refunds other than housing and meal plans due to completing the semester online rather than on campus?

There will be no reimbursement for charges or fees, other than those outlined for housing and meal plans. Instruction for spring semester 2020 continued online and resources were reallocated to accommodate the many changes brought about by the COVID-19 pandemic.

Tuition

There are no refunds for tuition. Instruction moved online for the remainder of the semester and accommodations were made to allow completion of all courses.

Parking

No parking fees will be refunded. Income from parking fees is used to offset a wide variety of parking-related expenses (such as annual parking lot maintenance, shuttle driver salaries, etc.) The fees only partially reimburse these expenses.

Completion fee

Income from completion fees is used to offset the various expenses related to commencement. Although commencement was not be held on campus this year, it was not canceled. Completion fees will be reallocated to the expenses related to virtual commencement as well as future events being planned to honor our 2020 graduates.

Summer 2020 classes will be offered online. Will tuition for those classes be discounted?

No, summer tuition rates will not be discounted. Online classes will cover the same material and provide the same learning outcomes/expectations as would classes meeting in person. Certain course fees may be adjusted on a course-by-course basis if materials expenses are reduced due to offering a course online. Students will also gain access to additional campus services such as the library and athletic facilities, with no additional fee, as the State of Wisconsin Badger Bounce Back plan allows.

I was enrolled in a payment plan for spring semester and still owe a balance. When is that due?

Payment in full for the spring semester is now due. If you need more time or need to arrange for a revised payment plan, please contact the business office at busoff@carrollu.edu.

I owe a balance and am unable to make payments during these uncertain times. Can you work with me?

We understand these are uncertain times and that many students and families may find it difficult to pay their student account right now. If you need to arrange for a payment plan, please contact the business office at busoff@carrollu.edu.
