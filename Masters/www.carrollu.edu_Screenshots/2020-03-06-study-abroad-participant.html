Carroll University Spring 2020 Study Abroad Participant

Coronavirus Update March 6, 2020

We want to inform study abroad participants of decisions being made by our campus leadership regarding University-sponsored travel in light of the current COVID-19 Coronavirus outbreak worldwide. We understand that you are hearing information from many different sources. We want to assure you that we appreciate the academic and financial investment that accompanies your study abroad experience. Our priority is always to keep our students and the greater campus community safe.

Our team is monitoring the situation utilizing CDC Travel Health Notices, U.S. Department of State Travel Advisories, the University’s insurance carriers and information from the fields of international education and public health. The University will require that all travelers return home when a country is designated a Level 3, Warning Alert, or Level 4, Travel Restriction. Because this is a rapidly evolving situation, it is difficult to predict if or when a country will meet this threshold. We understand that this uncertainty may make decisions difficult.

Even though you may not be in a country designated as Level 3 or 4, we realize that some students may not be comfortable being abroad during this outbreak. If this is the case, we have created a plan to support that decision. Students who decide to return to the United States, and to Carroll, prior to March 16th may complete the spring semester on campus. We ask for notification by Monday, March 9.

Should you desire to come home now, we will offer the following assistance:

Travel Arrangement Support
Travel assistance for booking return flights will be provided, if needed.

Housing Support
Students who live on-campus will be provided complimentary on-campus housing accommodations for the remainder of the semester.

Financial Support
Your study abroad fee of $360 will be reimbursed upon return. We will also provide up to $500 toward the cost of flight changes.

Academic Assistance
You will be granted 12 credits for a successful combination of work completed to date and work to be completed upon return to Carroll. We will enroll you in Carroll courses for the second half of the semester in order to earn your full semester credits.

As a reminder, this opportunity is optional at this time. If you prefer to make a decision to return later in the semester, we will work to support you to the best of our ability, understanding that some circumstances could be beyond our control, including flights and the granting of credits.

For Questions/More Information
Please do not hesitate to contact Megan Couch (mcouch@carrollu.edu) or Megan Baker (mmbaker@carrollu.edu) for more details.
