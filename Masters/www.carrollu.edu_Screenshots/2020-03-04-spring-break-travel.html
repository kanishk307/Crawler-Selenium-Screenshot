Spring Break Travel

Coronavirus Update March 4, 2020

Carroll University would like to remind you if you are planning to travel for spring break, please review Centers for Disease Control travel guidelines, and visit our page for tips to stay safe and well when it comes to the coronavirus (COVD-19). To get comprehensive information about the virus, including prevention and symptoms, view this Q&A from the World Health Organization.

The CDC says the immediate risk to the general U.S. public is considered low at this time, although individuals who are older and/or have underlying conditions such as diabetes are at higher risk. There are currently no confirmed or suspected cases at Carroll University or in Waukesha County.

Here is what our Carroll Student Health Center is doing about COVID-19:

  • Staying in close communication with the Waukesha Public Health Department for updates and guidance.
  • Actively screening ill patients for travel history.
  • Making masks available for patients with upper respiratory symptoms when they enter the health center.
  • Making sure to immediately contact the Waukesha Health Department and take steps in accordance with CDC guidelines if an ill patient would be identified as an at-risk patient.

If you are feeling ill, seek medical care. But before you visit your provider, call the physician, ER, etc., to tell them about your recent travel and your symptoms.

We hope you will use this information to have a safe and healthy spring break.
