    Guidance for Reopening

    Saint Francis University Pandemic Response Team 

    The Saint Francis University Pandemic Response team meets frequently to implement, monitor and evaluate containment strategies to, where possible, minimize the risk of impact from an infectious disease on our campus community.  The cross-functional committee is composed of individuals from areas such as health services, risk management, physical plant and human resources.  The committee is in constant communication with public health officials and reviews all relevant guidance in an effort to keep Saint Francis University as healthy and safe as possible.

    Daily Wellness Check-in and Other Guidance

    Personal Workspace Disinfecting

    We all need to play our part in making sure COVID-19 stays away from campus. The cleaning team uses Quat-Stat 5, a chemical that disinfects surfaces from COVID-19 in daily cleaning. Each department will also receive their own Quat-Stat 5 spray bottle to keep campus extra clean.      

    • How to use Quat-Stat 5
    In the Case of Symptoms

    myhealth@school (Student Health): As an accredited health care facility, our Student Health Center has staff who are trained in current infectious disease control procedures, and protocols are in place if someone were to present with symptoms requiring testing, isolation, or care. myhealth@school has been planning for weeks to ensure they have ample medical supplies and staffing to be prepared to care for our student community. Call (814) 472-3008 immediately if you have symptoms or have been in contact with a confirmed case. We have established protocol to work with the Department of Health, and they will handle all suspected cases and determine the testing needs.
