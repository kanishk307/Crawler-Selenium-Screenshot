    Preparing for the Coronavirus

    Canisius College encourages all members of our campus community to prepare for all types of emergency situations including unexpected illness.  Below is a list of suggestions to help you and your family plan.
     

    Health Care Access

    Make sure you have a primary care doctor and updated information on how to reach them, if you get sick. If your doctor has a health portal, be sure to set up an account. Learn how to send messages to your health care provider from your health portal. Monitor for updates from your health care provider. Check if your health insurance plan has a telemedicine or nurse on call benefits and how to access those services.  If you do not have health insurance coverage, please check the marketplace in your state and enroll in a plan.
     

    Stock Up on Medicines and Sick Supplies

    Stock at lease a one-month supply of medicines currently prescribed by your doctor. Check expiration dates on all medications and renew expired medicines now. This includes emergency medicines such as epi pens or albuterol inhalers. Keep an adequate supply of "over the counter" medicines like acetaminophen, ibuprofen, cough medicine, decongestant, Gatorade and other medicines on hand. If you have a small child or baby at home, be sure to purchase needed medications for them as well, including Pedialyte. If you do not have one, purchase a thermometer and follow manufacturer recommendations on how to use and clean after use, in addition to between sick persons.

    Food and Household Items

    Keep an adequate supply of food on hand including canned and frozen foods.   

    Make sure you have an ample supply of toilet paper, paper towel, facial tissues, personal care items, soap, shampoo, laundry detergent, bleach, 60% or more alcohol-based hand sanitizer and disinfecting spray or wipes. Consider stocking up on bottled water.
     

    Design a Care Plan

    Designate a sick room in your home or apartment.  A room with a private bathroom is recommended.

    Locate people who will care for you if you get sick.  Resident students, who get sick or are placed on quarantine after an exposure, will be asked by the college to go home.  Develop a travel plan home.  It is best to arrange travel in a private car as public transportation may not be available for you. Resident students who are sick or placed on quarantine after an exposure, who are unable to go home, should develop a plan reside with a relative or friend.  Resident students who are not able to locate alternate housing should contact the Office of Student Life now to discuss housing options.

    Set up a transportation plan. It is recommended you drive in a private care to your medical appointments and if you need to go home to recover or monitor for symptoms.

    If you have a chronic medical condition that places you at higher risk for serious complications if you get sick with a viral illness, consult your health care provider now to determine a plan to protect yourself.  You should also discuss remote learning or work opportunities with your professors or supervisor.
     

    School and Work Plan

    Students should check with faculty now to see what remotely learning opportunities are available to you if you are sick or quarantined.  If needed obtain a computer and check network connections at home. Students can contact ITS for help with personal computers and network connections.  Employees of the college should talk to their supervisors about working from home. All campus members should know how to report an illness or quarantine to the college.
     

    Stay Healthy

    Avoid people who appear sick, are coughing, blowing their nose or sneezing.  Stay 6 feet or more away. Do not share food, water bottles, eating utensils or other items that have contact with the salvia of other persons. Keep your home, dorm room and office clean and tidy.  Clean surfaces regularly with disinfecting wipes or spray.  Remember to wipe down phones, cell phones, keyboards, remote controllers, video game controllers and desk tops.  Wash your hands frequently throughout the day especially before eating, after using the bathroom, before preparing food and after coughing or sneezing. Cover your cough and sneeze with a tissue, discard the tissue in the trash and wash your hands. If you do not have a tissue ,sneeze or cough into your bent elbow and wash your hands. Step away from others ( at least 6 feet) if are going to sneeze or cough. Avoid crowds, if possible.

    If you are sick with a fever do not come to campus, attend class or report to work. Students should contact Student Health or their local doctor, employees should contact their physicians for medical evaluation.
