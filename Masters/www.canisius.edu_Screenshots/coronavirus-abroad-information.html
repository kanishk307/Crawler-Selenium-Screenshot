    Coronavirus Information for Students Studying Abroad and Families

    The health and safety of all our students is our top priority and we are taking steps to assure that. Canisius College is monitoring the COVID-19 situation abroad closely and will keep students informed as new information becomes available. 

    In recent days, new outbreaks were reported across the globe, with the number of diagnosed cases rapidly increasing. Because we do not know when the situation will stabilize, we have been in constant contact with students and families and have strongly recommended that students attending other universities for study abroad experiences return home immediately.  Canisius will continue to monitor the situation and respond appropriately as new recommendations are being made by the CDC, WHO and host univerisities.

    In addition, the Study Abroad Office wants to remind you to follow these guidelines as you live, study and travel outside the United States during this global health situation:

    • Stay aware of the latest information on the COVID-2019 outbreak available on the World Health web site, through local health officials in your host country and from your host university.
    • Regularly monitor travel advisories from the World Health Organization and CDC and follow recommendations. We are encouraging you to modify travel and avoid areas where there are travel advisories for COVID-19.
    • Register with the US State Department Smart Traveler Enrollment Program (STEP).
    • STEP will provide you with current travel advisories as well as ensure that the US consulate or embassy can contact you in the event of an emergency.
    • Check that you have a copy of your GeoBlue Insurance card.  Take a picture of your card (front and back) to save on your cell phone or other mobile devise in the event you need to access health care.  On the back of the card is a phone number for GeoBlue member services available 24/7/365 to assist you in locating medical care whenever you may be.
    • Download the GeoBlue mobile app for current health alerts and information regarding health care in your location.
    • Regularly check information being provided at your host university about the coronavirus situation (COVID-19) and recommendations for reporting illness, accessing medical care and quarantine or isolation guidance.
    • It might be prudent to consider stocking up on food, drinks, personal care items, etc. you may need if you must stay in your room for a period of time.
    • Check with each of your professors for guidance on how to continue your course work if there is a quarantine or if you get sick and unable to attend class.
    • Follow all infection control and health guidance made by your host institution and health officials in your host country.
    • Be sure you have provided your host university with your current phone number and the phone number and contact information for your emergency contact.

    A Message from the Canisius Student Health Department 

    Transmission of COVID-19 occurs when droplets from an infected person are released into the air when the ill individual coughs, sneezes or talks. These droplets can be transmitted to eyes, nose or mouth of individuals in close contact to the sick person.  Droplets can also land on surfaces and possibly be transmitted from surfaces by touching that surface with your hand and then touching your eyes, nose or mouth.  It is not known how long COVID-19 survives on surfaces or how long it lingers in the air. Student Health recommends you follow these infection control and prevention recommendations:

    • Avoid sick persons who are coughing, sneezing, blowing their nose or appear ill.  Stay at least 6 feet away.
    • Do not share eating utensils, cups, drinks, food, water bottles or other items that have been in contact with another person’s saliva or mouth.
    • Wash your hands frequently during the day and especially before and after eating, after using the bathroom, before preparing food and after coughing, sneezing or blowing your nose.
    • Wash your hands under running water. Soap up your hands and rub them for 20 seconds (sing Happy Birthday twice) and then rinse. Dry hands with a clean paper towel, turn off faucets with the paper towel and discard the towel. 
    • If soap and water are not available, use a 60 % alcohol based sanitizer.  Apply the sanitizer to your hands and rub your hands together until the hands are dry.
    • Keep your residence clean.  Regularly wipe down frequently touched surfaces with a regular household disinfectant include door knobs, cell phones, key boards, and video game controllers. 
    • Cough and sneeze into a clean tissue, discard the tissue and wash your hands.
    • Purchase a thermometer and facemasks or ask your university health center if they can give you these items. 
    • If you feel sick, take your temperature.  Remember in the United States we use Fahrenheit readings while Celsius readings are used more frequently around the world.  A fever is a temperature of 100 Fahrenheit or 37.7 degrees Celsius.  If you have a fever, Student Health strongly recommends that you seek medical evaluation.
    • If you experience a fever, stay out of class, do not travel, avoid contact with others and follow the recommendations of your host institution on reporting illness and where to go to for medical evaluation.  If you are sick with a fever, use a facemask when you have to leave your residence to get medical care.
    • If you are traveling and develop a fever, use the GeoBlue customer service number on your ID card or the GeoBlue mobile app to locate a health care provider.  Do not leave your hotel or air bnb and avoid contact with other except to seek medical evaluation.  Wear a facemask when you travel to the medical provider. 
    • Be sure to have a supply of fever reducing medicine on hand to use such as acetaminophen or ibuprofen. In Europe and Australia acetaminophen (Tylenol) is sold under the name of paracetamol (Panadol).  Ibuprofen is another fever reducing medicine available and is sold under that name.  Student Health recommends following the dosing on the bottle or asking the pharmacist at the store where you are purchasing the medication.
