COVID-19 Telecommuting Requests, Guidance for Appropriate Administrators

Monday, March 16, 2020 - 10:45pm

TO: Sonoma State Appropriate Administrators
FR: Joyce Lopes, Vice President of Administration and Finance
RE: COVID-19 Telecommuting Requests - Guidance for Appropriate Administrators

As a follow up to the email Telecommuting Procedures in Response to COVID-19 Pandemic, sent earlier today, the following guidance is being provided for any employee requests for telecommuting during the COVID-19 period of declared emergency.

  1. Employees who would like to be considered for telecommuting should submit the request to their Appropriate Administrator (AA) with the understanding that not all positions are suitable for remote work. In addition, there are some positions that may be designated as essential positions for on-campus work. 
  2. AAs should work with their respective Dean/AVP to approve employees’ request for remote work. It should be noted that approvals are subject to change with notification to the employee should the emergency require such changes. If approved, go to step 3. If denied, continue to step 2a.
    1. If the work performed by the employee is deemed unsuitable for telecommuting based on the information provided, the AA will review with the AVP for HR prior to communicating the decision to the employee.  
    2. Denied requests will include an explanation and, when applicable, steps that would be necessary for the request to be reconsidered. If an employee wishes to appeal a denial, they may submit a written appeal to HR (hr@sonoma.edu) for reconsideration.
  3. Deans/AVPs should then submit the attached COVID-19 Telecommuting Request Summary to HR (hr@sonoma.edu) for final review and approval.
  4. HR will then distribute the telecommuting agreement form to employees approved to telecommute during the COVID-19 Pandemic.
  5. Employees should then sign and submit the telecommuting agreement to the AA.  The agreement includes:
    1. The work location
    2. Telecommuting schedule (generally consistent with regular hours) 
    3. Proposed start date
  6. AAs should then forward telecommuting agreements to HR for placement in employee personnel files. 

If you have questions about telecommuting, please email hr@sonoma.edu. Thank you for helping all of us stay healthy and well at Sonoma State.
