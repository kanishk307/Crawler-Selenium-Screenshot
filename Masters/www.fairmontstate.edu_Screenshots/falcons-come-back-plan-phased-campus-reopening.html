FALCONS COME BACK—Fairmont State University’s Plan for Phased Reopening

If you have a specific question regarding general University procedures, please email coronavirus@fairmontstate.edu.

Since the imminent arrival of COVID-19 to the Mountain State, Fairmont State University has continued to work with the Marion County Health Department and state health officials, in addition to monitoring CDC guidelines in order to make the decisions that will keep the Falcon Family safe.

Monday, May 4, the Governor’s Stay at Home order expired and a new Safer at Home Order began. With this, it is now time to look to the future and begin planning to bring our Falcon Family home.

This following plan is separated into three phases and is based on current recommendations from the Governor’s Office and local health officials. A change in these recommendations will impact Fairmont State’s phased reopening.

PHASE ONE (May 8-18) — Residential Move Out

Keeping in line with the Governor’s guidelines and local health official’s recommendations, we have created a move-out system that should maximize social distancing while students remove belongings from their residence halls.

To maintain social distancing and limit contact, students must register for a three hour move-out period from May 8-18. The student and one guest will be required to wear masks and gloves during this time and their temperature will be taken before being allowed to enter a campus building.

Each evening, a cleaning crew will sanitize all residence halls common areas in preparation for the next day’s move out. For more information on phase one, please visit www.fairmontstate.edu.covid19 and click on the “student” tab.

At the conclusion of move-out, all campus buildings and dorm rooms will be sanitized. This process is expected to occur through May 31.

PHASE TWO (June 1-30) — Limited Employee Return to Campus

As previously announced, Fairmont State’s summer term will be held exclusively online and through digital modalities. Because of this, the public, students, or faculty will not be able to return to campus during phase two. Support from the Library will continue to be available as it has during the shutdown of campus.

All campus summer camps and events including Falcon Camp and events sponsored by Athletics have been canceled through June 30.

Phase two will allow for limited staff to return to campus from June 1-30. This phase will be focused on staff in Hardway Hall, Colebank and the Turley Center. These locations have been identified due to staff members' immediate need for access to files and systems in order to execute their job duties.

All employees who are deemed to be successfully completing their job duties from home will be required to continue working from home during this phase. Employee names will be sent to the Emergency Operations team through each vice president. The team will then review office locations and work procedures in order to determine how each employee can safely return to campus.

Employees will receive notice from Human Resources (HR) about their return to campus date. Only employees who have received approval from HR should return to campus. Employees, including faculty, who are found on campus and have not been approved to return will face disciplinary action.

Employees approved to return to campus will be required to wear a facemask at all times. If an employee works in an office and can close their office door, they may remove their mask. When another employee enters the space, the mask should be put back on.

Work locations and operations are likely to be impacted and a return to campus does not signal a return to normal. Vice Presidents will notify employees of new safety protocols put in place for employee protection. Employees will be required to follow these safety protocols.

PHASE THREE (July 1 – Aug 15) — Fall Term Return to Campus

Fairmont State University plans to safely return to campus for the beginning of the fall term including face-to-face classes. University Leadership will continue to monitor state and federal recommendations for higher education institutions on how to safely return.

The months of July and August will see more employees return to campus as needed. Due to the unprecedented impact of COVID-19, it is not responsible to formalize plans at this time. In the following weeks, this plan will be updated and the Falcon Community will be notified.
