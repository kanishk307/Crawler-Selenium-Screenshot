Academics

What will happen for the Fall 2020 semester?

Bryn Mawr College will offer in-person and remote education during Fall 2020. Our Academics page includes information on the revised academic calendar, registration, and related details.

Important Fall 2020 details of note:

  • Nov. 20 is the last day of in-person instruction. Thanksgiving Break begins after the last class on Nov. 20.
  • Dorms close Nov. 20-25, with move-out by appointment and details to be determined.
  • All classes resume remotely on Nov. 30, through the end of the semester (Dec. 10). Exams will be conducted remotely (Dec. 13-18).

Admissions

How should I explore and apply to Bryn Mawr during this time?

Prospective students and families can find full information at: COVID-19 Information and Updates from Admissions

Admitted students and families can also find admissions information at: COVID-19 Information and Updates from Admissions

While the Office of Admissions has canceled in-person visits and events, prospective students are welcome to contact the office for a virtual visit: admissions@brynmawr.edu. 

How will school closings affect applicants to Bryn Mawr College?

If a student's school has closed or moved its coursework online in an effort to stop the spread of COVID-19, that student's application will not be affected. Bryn Mawr's Office of Admissions representatives understand that sweeping measures must be taken during this unprecedented time. Please contact Admissions with any questions or concerns.

Campus and Events

What are the College's guidelines for social distancing and wearing of masks while on campus?

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) advises everyone to wear a mask around other people. It is not necessary to wear a mask when alone in one's room or office. Masks do not replace social distancing, so please continue to maintain six foot or greater distances from others.

In Summer 2020, masks are available from the service window at the mail room in the Campus Center, Monday through Friday from 11 a.m. to 2 p.m. and upon request. Be sure to bring your OneCard so that you can get into the Campus Center, as the doors are ordinarily locked. You will not need your card to obtain masks.

You may also choose to wear a homemade or other cloth mask. If you have questions about universal masking, please contact Environmental Health and Safety at dabramow@brynmawr.edu.

Employees may find further information on the on-campus mask policy page in the Human Resources section.

How will campus spaces be used during this time?

Read the Campus Spaces page to learn more about classroom plans, distancing while on campus, cleaning, and community engagement.

Will the College hold in-person events?

At this time, all College events on campus are canceled or delivered virtually. Specific plans for future events are to be determined.

Dining Services 

Will there be any changes to the Dining program? 

Yes. We will continue to provide a full meal plan for all resident students, and we will continue to serve through New Dorm and Erdman dining halls. It is anticipated that dine-in service will be possible in the dining centers at various times during the academic year, while at other times all service will be takeout only. 

There will be no self-service of food, and food options may be more limited. There will be times that students may be allowed to walk through the line and have their food choices plated by Dining staff, but there will be other times when only a select set of pre-prepared/boxed meals will be available. 

Unlimited access will be temporarily unavailable, but additional snack items will be available to take with your meal.

When dine-in service is available, seating will be limited; capacities will be significantly reduced to facilitate social distancing. Additional outdoor seating will be made available. Take out limits have been lifted, and we expect to be able to occasionally distribute to-go meals at Wyndham and/or Pem Arch.

We hope to open Uncommon Grounds, but it will be open for take-out only.  An online pre-order/pre-pay link will be provided. Visits to Uncommon Ground will be for quick pick-up only.  Lusty Cup is not expected to reopen in the Fall.

Dining Services will continue to provide allergen-free options. The FREE room in Erdman will continue to be available. Please contact Natalie the dietitian at nzaparzyns@brynmawr.edu to discuss your specific needs.

Finances 

Undergraduate Tuition and Billing 

Are there any changes to Fall tuition rates? 

In recognition of the current global economic condition, the College will not increase tuition, fees, and room and board rates for 2020-21. The College will continue to meet full, demonstrated need. Financial Aid recipients can learn more about how this impacts aid in the Financial Aid section below. 

When will I receive my fall bill? Are there any changes to the due date? 

Your Fall bill will be available in Nelnet in mid-July. Notifications of billing statements will be emailed to students and authorized parties when the statement is available to view. Payment will be due August 15, 2020 for the Fall semester. For more information about billing and payment options, visit Student Accounts.

If I withdraw from the College will I receive a refund? 

The College will continue to use the withdraw calendar to determine refunds of tuition and fees if a student withdraws during the term.

Undergraduate Financial Aid 

Tuition, fees, and room and board charges are being adjusted to 2019-20 rates. How will this impact my financial aid? 

In recognition of the current global economic condition, the College will not increase tuition, fees, and room and board rates for 2020-21 as previously announced. Students who do not receive need based financial aid or only receive a merit scholarship will see this decrease through the lowered charges on their bill. Students who are eligible for need based financial aid grants from Bryn Mawr during the 2020-21 academic year will have their financial aid eligibility based on the lowered costs per Federal regulations. However, students eligible for a need based Bryn Mawr Grant in 2020-21 will also see a one-time Tuition Adjustment Grant on their financial aid award in order for students on need based aid to see the impact of the tuition adjustment. As a result of the one-time Tuition Adjustment Grant, you may see a change in the make-up of your Federal and State financial aid (such as Subsidized vs Unsubsidized Federal Direct Loans) due to Federal rules and regulations.

When will I receive my financial aid award? 

For returning students, students need to complete the Commitment to Enroll Form by the due date of July 9 in order for the Office of Financial Aid to accurately assess eligibility and provide a financial aid award. If you have completed the Commitment to Enroll Form by July 9 and completed your financial aid application by June 15, you will be notified of your financial aid award in mid-July, before your billing statement is sent to you. If your financial aid file is not complete, files will be reviewed in the order they are completed. To check on the status of your financial aid application requirements, please check your financial aid portal.

For incoming students, you received your financial aid award at the time of admission. The Office of Financial Aid will be adjusting your financial aid awards after receiving your Commitment to Enroll Form, due by July 9. If your financial aid application is complete, you will receive a notification of your revised financial aid award by mid-July before your billing statement is posted. If you received an estimated financial aid award at the time of admission and did not complete outstanding requirements, your financial aid eligibility will not be reassessed and will not appear on your billing statement until your missing materials are submitted. Please check on the status of your financial aid application requirements on your financial aid portal.

How will my financial aid change if I live with my family while I complete my fall courses? 

If you are living with your family, you will not be charged for room and board on your student account. Per our regular financial aid policy, if you are a need based financial aid recipient your financial aid grant will decrease as you will not be incurring the cost for room and board. A small budget for living expenses will be included in your financial aid calculation to help cover the cost of meals and other expenses while living at home.

How will my financial aid change if I live on campus vs. in an apartment off campus in the Bryn Mawr area? 

If you are approved to live off campus by Residence Life and will be living in an apartment off campus, you will not be charged for room and board on your student account. If you are a need based financial aid recipient, the cost of attendance used to calculate your financial aid eligibility will be the same as on campus room and board.

How will my aid change if I am not enrolled full time at Bryn Mawr College? 

Traditional undergraduate students must be enrolled full time at Bryn Mawr College to receive merit and/or need based grant assistance under BMC policy. Students must be enrolled at least half time to receive Federal and State aid assistance. McBride Scholars will still receive grants under our regular policies.

Can I appeal my financial aid award if my parents lost income due to COVID? 

In March, the financial aid office posted on their website that appeals due to loss of income as a result of COVID were not being reviewed. The situation was rapidly evolving, and the college was not certain we would be able to respond to individual appeal requests. At this time, in recognition of the dramatic changes COVID has had on family’s financial situations, the Financial Aid Appeal Committee will begin to review changes in income circumstances in accordance with our current appeal process and policies for loss of income which includes taking a three year average of income from 2018, 2019, and 2020. Per our policy, we do not consider changes in assets. Please see the financial aid website for instructions on requirement documents to submit with your appeal. Appeals will not be reviewed until initial financial aid awards are sent to students in mid-July. At that time, we will begin to review appeals in the order they are received. Appeals must be submitted with all required documentation by August 15 to be reviewed before the first day of classes. 

If I am an incoming student and decide to take a gap year, will I receive my aid when I enroll in the Fall of 2021?

If you were awarded a merit scholarship at the time of admission, you will still be awarded the scholarship when you enroll if you decide to take a gap year. Your merit scholarship is available for 8 total semesters. You must reapply for need based aid every year, and any changes to your family’s situation such as income, assets, and number in college will impact your aid eligibility in future years.

As a continuing student, what will happen to my aid if I take a personal leave this fall?

Students are eligible for a total of 8 semesters of financial aid from the college (this includes both merit scholarships and need based grant). These 8 semesters do not have to be sequential if you decide to take a personal leave this fall. If a student decides to take a personal leave before a semester begins, they will have that semester of eligibility to use in the future. Students who take a leave of absence in the middle of a semester will have that semester used towards their overall eligibility, even if they are only enrolled for a partial semester.

Campus Employment 

Will I be able to work on campus this fall either through work study or campus employment? 

Students who return to Bryn Mawr in person will be able to pursue work opportunities on campus, but positions will be limited. The College is still evaluating the safety of various student positions, how various departments might utilize student workers, and how many opportunities will be available. More information about student employment will be available as the beginning of the term approaches.

If work study or campus employment are part of my financial aid award, what will happen if I decide not to return to campus, or I return to campus but cannot work my full hours due to COVID limitations on campus?

Due to the Tuition Adjustment Grant that will be awarded to students who receive need based financial aid, Federal Work Study and Campus Employment will not be used to meet a student’s need for the 2020-21 academic year. However, eligible students may still see work opportunities on their financial aid award as a potential resource to help cover their expected family contribution. Students who return to Bryn Mawr in person will be able to pursue work opportunities on campus. The College is still evaluating the safety of various student positions, how various departments might utilize student workers, and how many opportunities will be available. More information about student employment will be available as the beginning of the term approaches.

Financial Aid

Tuition, fees, and room and board charges are being adjusted to 2019-20 rates. How will this impact my financial aid? 

In recognition of the current global economic condition, the College has decided not to increase tuition and fee rates for 2020-21 as previously announced for the Fall and Spring semesters. No changes will retroactively be applied to Summer courses. Students who receive a Social Work Grant will see their tuition discounts for Fall and Spring calculated on the new lowered tuition rates. However, students will also see a one-time Tuition Adjustment Grant on their financial aid award so that students see the impact of the tuition change and have a reduction in out of pocket costs. Federal aid may be adjusted in accordance with Federal rules as a result of this Tuition Adjustment Grant, particularly if a student borrows loans to their cost of attendance.

When will I receive a revised financial aid award? 

If students previously received a financial aid award, we will be working on adjustments in the coming weeks. You will be notified via email when your financial aid award is adjusted. If you have not yet received a financial aid award, please check your financial aid portal as soon as possible to review missing required documents from your financial aid application.

International Students 

Where can international students find more information or assistance on their status?

International Students must abide by specific regulations regarding their student visas. Though regulations may change by September, there are some federal restrictions regarding remote and distance learning that pertain to students with F-1 visa. There are also travel restrictions in place from various countries to the U.S. While the current conditions and restrictions could change at any time, you should be guided by current rules in choosing your courses. If you have an F-1 visa and are currently living in the U.S. — even on campus — as of now only one course may be taken entirely remotely. Please, therefore, keep this current regulation in mind as you choose your courses. All international students are urged to review and consider pertinent information on services and advising, including the frequently asked questions for international student fall enrollment.

Transportation Services 

Will the College provide bus transportation between Bryn Mawr’s campus and Haverford College or Swarthmore? 

Yes, there will be bus service that will run between Haverford and Bryn Mawr’s campuses (commonly called the “Blue Bus”), but the experience and the schedule will be different. The schedule will change because the class schedules have changed. Hours and days of operation may also change.

More importantly, to maintain social distancing, it is likely that only ten (10) students will be allowed on a Blue Bus per trip (one student in every other row of a school bus). With this greatly reduced capacity, students will need to plan ahead; students will need to be at the bus stop much earlier than they normally would (particularly in bad weather). We are considering whether a reservation system will be beneficial for facilitating travel; no decisions about that have yet been made. Students should also consider alternates like walking between campuses (the distance is approximately 1.5 miles or a 25-30 minute walk) or they can potentially request a parking permit from Campus Safety if they have a personal vehicle that they can use to drive themselves from one campus to the other.

Transportation to Swarthmore will not be provided. When considering Swarthmore classes, please focus only on those that you can engage in remotely.

As they will be elsewhere on campus, masks are required for the driver and all passengers, and leaving some open windows on the bus is recommended.

Travel

How has Bryn Mawr College limited College-sponsored travel?  

All College-sponsored travel has been canceled for the Fall Semester unless it is deemed essential.

What guidance on travel has the College issued?

Faculty, students, and staff should limit all nonessential personal travel unless it involves no increased risk.

Faculty, students and staff whose travel puts them at additional risk to COVID-19 exposure must be tested before they return to campus or self-quarantine for 14 days.

For general guidelines and helpful information, visit the College's web pages on travel resources.
