Visual Cues Across Campus

Click here to view a variety of measures and visual cues across campus that have been installed to mitigate the spread of COVID-19.

Campus Scenarios

Bathrooms – Special features have been added to bathrooms, such as hands-free foot openers at the bottom of pull doors. While stalls inside restrooms provide barrier protection, always use your cloth face covering inside the restroom. When using entry areas, sinks or paper towel dispensers, practice physical distancing by waiting for others to finish. Also rest assured that HPU’s Clean Team is disinfecting these areas regularly.

Elevators – Practice physical distancing when riding the elevator with others. If the elevator is too crowded, wait until the next available elevator provides physical distancing opportunities.

Dining – HPU Dining has enhanced their services to provide meals safely. Follow all cues posted in dining spaces, such as waiting for a dining team member to serve you as all self-serve areas have been removed. Always practice physical distancing and wear your cloth face covering.

Keyboard/technology use – Utilize and frequently clean your own workspace technology, including laptop and keyboard, whenever possible. At areas on campus where you must use different technology, such as Smith Library, special disinfecting and check-out/return protocols are in place for your safety. For example, you can check out a sanitized mouse and a keyboard at Smith Library, and it will be sanitized after you return it. Please follow all protocols and contact the librarian on duty if you have questions.

Books – Utilize and frequently clean your own books whenever possible. At areas on campus where you must use other books, such as Smith Library, special disinfecting and check-out/return protocols are in place for your safety. For example, books that are checked out from the library will be quarantined for several days after being returned. Please follow all protocols and contact the librarian on duty if you have questions.

Labs – Special cleaning protocol has been implemented inside labs based on its unique technology and learning purposes. Faculty will instruct students on cleaning protocol on the first day of classes.

Alternatives to viewing printed documents – Large monitors are available for use around campus, such as those in Cottrell Hall conference rooms, which are available for students to reserve through the Campus Concierge. Word documents, PDFs and other materials that were once printed can alternatively be viewed on these screens to eliminate touching and sharing printed documents.

Clean and dirty pens – Utilize and frequently clean your own writing utensils whenever possible. In office areas where students may need to borrow a pen, you will notice areas designated for “clean” pens and “dirty” pens. Use the clean pens as needed, and put them in the designated “dirty” area afterward so they can be sanitized.

Group projects – Find common areas or reserve spaces that allow for physical distancing when group project work is best completed in person. WebEx is HPU’s designated digital meeting space that is available to all students when group projects can be completed virtually. Physical spaces can be reserved through the Campus Concierge. Remember to always wear your cloth face coverings and practice physical distancing. There are many spaces on campus, including Smith Library, Cottrell Hall, and the R.G. Wanek Center Great Day Bakery commons area and the Learning Commons satellite library, that will provide spaces conducive to physical distancing and effective collaboration.

Campus recreation – You will notice that HPU has enhanced its Campus Recreation Services equipment in Slane Fitness Center and satellite centers by moving and marking to permit safe physical distancing. Follow all of these visual cues and posted protocols. To regulate capacity in Slane, students will sign-up for a timeslot using IMLeagues sign-up software (the same way they would sign-up for a Group Exercise class or Intramurals). Students will wipe down equipment following each use. Intramural schedule will change to lower contact activities. Pool capacities will be limited per county guidelines and regulated by lifeguards. Group Exercise classes will be offered virtually.

CARES Act Emergency Financial Aid Grants

Under the Coronavirus Aid, Relief and Economic Security Act (CARES Act), High Point University signed and returned to the Department of Education the Certification and Agreement to be eligible to receive its distribution amount from the established Higher Education Emergency Relief Fund (HEERF). Of the $1,302,970 in HEERF funding received, the university acknowledges at least 50 percent of this funding will be used to provide Emergency Financial Aid Grants to students for expenses related to the disruption of campus operations due to coronavirus.

Eligibility:

Degree-seeking students with a valid FAFSA on file with the university who were enrolled in the Spring 2020 semester are eligible for consideration. For High Point University, there were approximately 3,075 students who met this criterion necessary to be eligible to receive these grant funds.

The CARES Emergency Grant is a one-time federal grant not intended to provide ongoing relief for recurring expenses. Funds are not to be awarded for lost income resulting from under-employment or unemployment or as a result of not receiving other stimulus funding awarded under the CARES act or other coronavirus relief. CARES Grant funds are not intended to cover tuition and associated fees, non-essential personal expenses or expenses incurred as a result of overspending or lack of planning.

Applying:

The University has elected to use an application process to evaluate student’s eligibility and qualifying expenses related to the disruption of campus operations due to the coronavirus.  Students may submit an online application and supporting documentation here.

Applications will be reviewed by the Emergency Fund Committee made up of staff members from the Offices of Student Financial Services, Student Life, Experiential Learning & Career Development, and Business & Financial Affairs. Applicants may be required to complete a questionnaire, be interviewed by a member of the committee, and/or provide additional substantiation for your request by way of receipts for expenses incurred. Many factors are taken into consideration in deciding to approve or deny a request and in determining amounts. These factors include, but are not limited to:

  • The number of applications received compared to the funds available at the time
  • The nature and urgency of the student’s emergency
  • The specific and immediate impact the crisis may have on the student’s living and academic circumstances
  • Other resources or support that the student may have available to them
  • If approved, students will receive financial assistance as a cash grant. The University will issue a check directly to the student, which will be mailed to the address on file through the University’s Accounts Payable office. Checks will be issued weekly until all available federal funds have been expended.

The university’s initial amount awarded to students was $51,045. As of July 2, 2020, the University has awarded $368,706.80 to students who met all necessary criteria to receive awards and continues to review applications received.
