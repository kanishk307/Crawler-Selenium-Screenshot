Classroom Protocols

Our faculty are looking forward to teaching in person again. While we know that it is not yet possible for our classrooms to “return to normal,” the following approach will help us ensure that teaching can continue in a safe and responsible manner.

  • Class start dates and times will remain the same
  • Classes will be conducted in person and remotely simultaneously to reduce the density of large classes
  • Students who are in the classroom will be able to view remote participants on monitors that are being set up in the front of the classroom
  • Physical distancing of at least six feet will be required in all classrooms. Classroom seats will be identified to maintain physical distancing
  • Entries, exits, and pathways will be marked in classrooms and hallways
  • Signs marking room capacities will be posted for each room
  • Face coverings must be worn in accordance with the Public Health – Seattle & King County directive
  • Classes will end five minutes early for surfaces to be wiped down prior to the next class

Library Protocols

The Library is implementing protocols that include:

  • Maximum 50% capacity
  • Physical distancing including space from the main desk, all workspaces, and computers
  • Wipes provided at the front door
  • Large group study rooms limited to two people and small group study rooms limited to one person
  • Remaining closed to the public with keycard required for entry
