Phased Campus Safety Strategy

The different phases below correspond to different levels of health practices on campus depending on the health situation on campus, and to a lesser extent, in the surrounding community. Please refer to Ursinus.edu for current campus status.

Phase< Characteristics Staff & Faculty Students Visitors

Only essential functions—County in red phase

Only those maintaining essential campus operation on campus

Students at home, all instruction remote

As campus is essentially empty, community may use campus for walking with masks & distancing

Preparation for re-opening—County must be at least in yellow phase

Minimal presence on campus, staggered schedules, remote work where possible

Students remain at home, but preparing for return to campus, including quarantine and testing

As campus occupancy is low, community may use campus for walking with masks & distancing, minimal visits from prospective students

Phased return: first-year students

Faculty begin in-person CIE; other functions remain remote where possible

First-year students arrive, along with RAs and others in support roles. Upper class students quarantine and testing

Visitors to campus are discouraged during phase-in process. For In-person admission-related visits, please visit Interact with Ursinus.

Phased return: upper class students

Faculty begin full in-person semester; other functions remain remote where possible

Upper-class students arrive.

Visitors to campus are discouraged during phase-in process. For In-person admission-related visits, please visit Interact with Ursinus.

Campus remains healthy. Relaxation of some campus restrictions

Additional staff and faculty return to regular work on campus

Gradual reopening of common spaces, shared dining, some larger gatherings

Visitors are allowed to return in socially distanced groups

End of in-person instruction at Thanksgiving

Campus closes for Thanksgiving break; remote instruction to follow

Students go home; small subset may remain

Campus grounds open for visitors; buildings closed

If small outbreak; temporary quarantine of campus

Faculty and staff follow phase zero practices as possible. Instruction continues remotely

Students confined to rooms for a period of several days as contact tracing completed and isolation enforced

No visitors to campus
