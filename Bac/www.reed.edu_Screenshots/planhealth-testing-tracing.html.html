Training, Testing, Health Monitoring & Contact Tracing

To protect the health and well-being of our community, Reed is providing training, testing, an app-based health monitoring tool, and contact tracing through Multnomah County.

Training and Agreement to Comply with Public Health Expectations

All community members will be required to

  • comply with the safety measures outlined by Reed;
  • follow guidance from the Oregon Health Authority and the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) on COVID-19;
  • complete a mandatory online training before the fall academic term that will focus on disease transmission, how to protect yourself and others, and Reed-specific policies regarding COVID-19. On-campus students must complete the training before move-in. Off-campus students, faculty, and staff must complete the training before the first day of the fall semester: August 24.

On-campus residents will be required to comply with the public health expectation agreement in their housing contract.

Testing

Reed has contracted with Tempus Health to provide PCR COVID-19 testing. PCR testing can detect the presence of genetic material from the virus that causes COVID-19, whether or not there are symptoms. Reed will (1) have a campus arrival testing protocol for faculty, staff, and students who will be on campus at the beginning of each term and (2) provide surveillance testing of population samples throughout the academic year. Surveillance testing is done on individuals without symptoms of COVID to better understand if and how the disease is spreading in our community and whether our prevention strategies are sufficiently effective. It is done by testing smaller samples of the population in order to make inferences about disease progression.

Students

You can access diagnostic testing through the Health & Counseling Center (HCC) for symptoms consistent with COVID-19 and for exposure due to sustained contact with a person diagnosed with COVID-19. 

Faculty and Staff

Contact human resources and your primary care provider for diagnostic testing for symptoms consistent with COVID-19 and for exposure due to sustained contact with a person diagnosed with COVID-19.

Health Monitoring

Reed will provide an app-based tool for students, faculty, and staff to self-check for symptoms daily. The tool will also provide guidance for those who may be experiencing COVID-like symptoms.

  • Students, faculty, and staff will be trained regarding symptom monitoring; community members are expected to be familiar with the symptoms of COVID-19.
  • Students will be encouraged to complete a daily symptom self-check.
  • Students exhibiting symptoms should remain in their residence and promptly call the Health & Counseling Center at 503-777-7281 for instruction.
  • Faculty and staff will be encouraged to complete a daily symptom self-check.
  • Faculty and staff with COVID-like symptoms are not allowed to work on campus.
  • Faculty and staff experiencing COVID-like symptoms should contact their supervisor and human resources (HR) and remain at home. HR will provide instructions for returning to work.

Contact Tracing

Contact tracing is the way in which public health staff work with an individual diagnosed with COVID-19 to help them recall those with whom they have had close contact during the timeframe when they may have been infectious.

Multnomah County will take the lead on contact tracing. Should you be identified as a person who has had close contact with someone with COVID-19, you will be contacted by the county within 24 hours.

Students who may have been exposed will be contacted by Reed’s Health & Counseling Center (HCC). Faculty and staff who may have been exposed will be contacted by human resources (HR). The HCC will provide students with support and next steps, and HR will provide faculty and staff with support and next steps.

If you have not been notified but believe you have had close contact with an individual with a confirmed positive case, you can reach out to the HCC or HR with questions. Close contact is defined as physical contact with less than six feet of distance for a cumulative period of 15 minutes.
