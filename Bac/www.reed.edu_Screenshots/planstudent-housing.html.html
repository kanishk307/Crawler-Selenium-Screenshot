Student Housing

Reed’s housing plan for the 2020–21 academic year is focused on a shared commitment to protect the health of the campus community. Following guidance for residential activities outlined by the Oregon Health Authority (OHA) and the Higher Education Coordinating Committee (HECC), residence halls will be open and operate with reduced density and new protocols. While this means the college will not be able to accommodate as many students in on-campus housing, the college will provide off-campus housing resources and support.

Reduced Density

To minimize potential spread of the coronavirus, all bedrooms in residence halls and campus apartments will be treated as single occupancy. Each student will be assigned their own room for college housing. Moving to single occupancy will reduce on campus housing availability; instead of providing on-campus housing to 1,130 students, the college will only be able to accommodate approximately 820 students. Off-campus housing resources are listed below.

On-Campus Housing Priorities

The college will prioritize the following groups for on-campus housing:

  • First-year students
  • Incoming transfer students
  • International students
  • Sophomores
  • Students with on-campus housing as a disability-related accommodation

Pods

In partnership with the academic program, Reed's residence life office is implementing a pod (aka cohort) model as a risk-reduction measure for new students in some halls. More information about the pod model will be available in the coming weeks. 

On-Campus Housing Confirmations

All returning students will be told whether or not they will have on-campus housing by July 20 or earlier; however, specific room assignments will be made available depending on circumstances.

COVID-19 Housing Contract Addendum

Students will be required to sign a COVID-19 addendum to the 2020–21 housing contract that covers individual expectations related to protecting the safety and health of the college community. Students will be able to view the addendum in early July, before they commit to housing.

Quarantine and Isolation Housing Support

On-campus students who test positive for COVID-19 will be relocated to a stand-alone residence hall designated for isolation. Students identified as having experienced close contact with the positive individual will be instructed to quarantine in their residence hall room. Students in isolation or quarantine will receive delivered meals, cleaning supplies for their restroom and living space, academic support, and regular telehealth check-ins from a member of Reed's medical team.

Move-In Schedule

See Arriving on Campus.

Move-out Schedule

Residence halls will remain open until Sunday, November 22, the weekend prior to Thanksgiving. Students should plan to move out by that date. There will be an application process made available in October for students who need to remain on campus.

Off-Campus Housing

Reed is dedicated to providing students with the necessary information and tools to successfully reside in off-campus housing. This comprehensive off-campus housing resource guide is designed to help students navigate the process of finding and securing safe and affordable off-campus accommodation. It covers topics such as budgeting, securing off-campus housing, what to consider when signing a lease, transportation to and from campus, and cooking and eating off-campus.
