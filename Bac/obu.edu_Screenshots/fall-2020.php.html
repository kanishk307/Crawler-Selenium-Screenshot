COVID-19

Navigate this section:
  • Ouachita coronavirus update
  • Fall 2020 Information
  • Related News and Blogs
  • Announcement Archive

Ouachita has been planning since April for the return to on-campus learning, residential life and activities in the fall. This page outlines current plans and policies and will be updated as additional details are available. Additionally, current and incoming students will receive updates to their Ouachita email accounts, and short updates will be provided on Ouachita’s Facebook, Twitter and Instagram accounts.

These plans for the fall remain fluid as we seek to draw on the latest data and be nimble in our response to the ever-changing COVID-19 landscape.

Ouachita’s Health Monitoring and Action Team (HMAT), chaired by University Physician and Vice President for Student Development Dr. Wesley Kluck, have led the planning. The HMAT has worked collaboratively with offices across campus as well as with state and local health officials and other universities to recommend these protocols for the fall; it also is working through the COVID-19 Planning Guide and Self-Assessment for Higher Education, a 626-item checklist from the Johns Hopkins School of Public Health and the Council for Higher Education Accreditation. The university also follows guidance from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) and Arkansas Department of Health (ADH).

 

 
As we learn more about COVID-19 and additional data emerges, we know that personal responsibility and a few basic guidelines matter tremendously to keeping each individual and our broader community safe. You will see reminders of these around campus: wash your hands, wear a face covering and observe physical distancing. Even if you do not believe you have COVID-19 or are not “high risk” yourself, you could be an asymptomatic carrier of the virus; these are the most effective things we can do to protect others in our community. It must be a group effort to keep each other safe. Take care, Tigers!
 

Wash Hands: Remember to wash your hands frequently, either with soap and water or hand sanitizer (60%+ alcohol content). Each student will receive a personal hand sanitizer among other items in a welcome bag when they arrive on campus. Extra hand sanitizer stations will be available in public spaces throughout campus.

Face Coverings: Face coverings, such as face masks, will be required in our indoor spaces, including classes. Exceptions include while you are eating, when you are in your residence hall suite/apartment or when you are alone in a private office or other workspace. Masks are not required outdoors unless you are in a large group or physical distancing is difficult to maintain. Each student will receive a washable mask among other items in a welcome bag when they arrive on campus. You are encouraged to bring your own additional face coverings to have an ample, rotating supply to keep them clean.

Physical Distancing: Maintain a distance of 6 feet between you and others whenever possible. Physical distancing markers will be placed around campus as reminders, all room capacities have been adjusted across campus and traffic flow in hallways and stairwells may be adjusted to prevent close contact. The exception to this is when you are around students in your “Student Family Group,” which is explained further below. 

Symptom Monitoring: Students, faculty and staff all will be expected to monitor their own symptoms before reporting to class or work. If you are experiencing any of the symptoms below, go to the Self-Check Screening guide on the Centers for Disease Control website at https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-ncov/symptoms-testing/symptoms.html# to receive guidance on next steps, such as the need for a COVID-19 test, consultation with your doctor or self-quarantine. Each student will receive a personal thermometer among other items in a welcome bag when they arrive on campus.

  • Coughing/Shortness of Breath/Difficulty Breathing
  • Fever/Chills
  • New Sinus Congestion
  • Generalized Muscle Aches
  • Unusual, Sudden-onset Headache
  • Sore Throat
  • Fatigue
  • New GI Symptoms
  • New Loss of Taste or Smell

Surface Sanitation: Additional sanitation processes will be carried out by facilities management staff across campus throughout the semester; additionally, self-service sanitation supplies will be available in every classroom. Disinfecting foggers will be used nightly in classrooms and other indoor public spaces. Extra touchless hand sanitizer stations will be available in public spaces throughout campus and refilled nightly.

Air Quality: In consultation with HVAC and air science specialists, adjustments are being made to Ouachita’s indoor air flow to reduce the airborne spread of the virus. More outside air will be circulated in the buildings, air temperature and humidity levels will be optimized, plexiglass barriers will be installed in select locations and filtration systems are being optimized. Additionally, each classroom and meeting room and many common spaces will feature new air purification units that utilize photohydroinionization and UV technology that is even more advanced than HEPA technology.

Individuals at High Risk: Ouachita will work to provide accommodations for individuals who have underlying medical conditions that fall into “high risk” categories for COVID-19. Per the CDC, these conditions include: chronic kidney disease; COPD (chronic obstructive pulmonary disease); immunocompromised state (weakened immune system) from solid organ transplant; obesity (body mass index of 30 or higher); serious heart conditions such as heart failure, coronary artery disease, or cardiomyopathies; sickle cell disease; or type 2 diabetes mellitus. All of our students, faculty and staff have been invited to complete a health survey to identify risk factors; surveys may be completed at the following links: employees – https://www.surveymonkey.com/r/OBU_COVID_RISK, students – https://www.surveymonkey.com/r/OBU_Student_COVID_Survey. Faculty and staff needing accommodations should refer to the internal Human Resources website for more information. If you fall into a high-risk category, we recommend also consulting with your physician and consulting these CDC recommendations for additional protection measures.

Testing: At this time, the CDC does not recommend campus-wide testing of students, faculty and staff prior to returning to campus in the fall. Testing is available locally to individuals experiencing symptoms consistent with COVID-19. At this time, testing will not be available within Ouachita’s Office of Health Services. 

Health Services & Counseling Services: The Offices of Health Services and Counseling Services will be available for in-person visits by appointment only as well as through telemedicine appointments to minimize close contact among students as well as Ouachita’s health care professionals. Additional protective and sanitation measures will be in place, as well. Procedures for flu shots and allergy shots will be adjusted to ensure separation of well and sick patients; details will be available at a later date.

Quarantine & Isolation: If students need to be quarantined or isolated due to exposure to COVID-19, those who can return to their homes for the required 14-day (or longer) period will be asked to do so. A limited number of exceptions can be accommodated on campus for those who cannot travel home. Students who live in campus apartments may choose to stay in those facilities. Additional details about quarantine and isolation procedures are forthcoming.

Evans Student Center and Gathering Spaces: For all indoor campus life spaces such as Evans Student Center, we’ll follow guidelines for masks and physical distancing. We’ll also make Walker Conference Center and selected classrooms available in the evenings for social and study spaces. We anticipate increased use of our outdoor spaces such as the Evans Student Center overlook balcony, Neighbor’s Table and Ouachita Singers and Rachel Fuller Memorial Amphitheatre and are adding more picnic tables across campus.

Large Gatherings (Chapel, Fine Arts Events, Tiger Tunes, etc.): Large indoor gatherings for Chapel, fine arts events, and other purposes will be governed by the latest guidance in regard to attendance. Current guidelines restricting attendance and maintaining social distancing will prevent many large gatherings from happening as they typically do. Alternative arrangements are being explored for these, and additional details will be available later this summer. 

Social Life: Part of the Ouachita experience is a diverse and robust number of organizations and activities. Some events may need to be very different or even postponed to the spring. Our students are creative, and we’ll be creative together in adjusting our traditions and programs and in discovering new ways to interact together.

Athletics: Working with the NCAA and the Great American Conference, we’re planning for our student-athletes to practice and compete. In-person attendance will be based on the latest guidance from the state. The Ouachita Sports Digital Network will continue their good work of livestreaming many of our sporting events online. 

Campus Dining: We’ll follow the state of Arkansas’ current guidelines for restaurants, including reduced capacity, no self-service and increased sanitation. For example, the capacity for the Ouachita Commons cafeteria is about 800 and the current COVID capacity is about 400 due to current spacing restrictions. We’ll have more grab-and-go options and adjusted traffic flow to manage physical distancing. Sodexo staff will complete several additional training sessions this summer to implement the new protocols smoothly for diners.

Classroom Spaces: Ouachita has sufficient academic space to conduct most of our classes in person while observing CDC guidelines on six-foot physical distancing. Each classroom is being configured for a new “COVID-19 capacity” based on this standard. Campus auditoriums and other large spaces, such as Walker Conference Center, will make it possible for even our largest classes to meet at their scheduled times. In some limited cases, it may be necessary to divide classes and offer them at alternate times. Some lab courses, practicums, studio and performance-based classes may also require alternate arrangements, such as a combination of face-to-face and remote instruction. To reduce the risks of virus transmission, students will be required to wear face coverings in class. 

Students will be notified when their course schedules, accessed through the INFO web portal, have been adjusted to reflect alternate classroom arrangements. Any students who discover a conflict created by adjustments to the course schedule are encouraged to contact their advisor or dean and make alternate arrangements to avoid disrupting their academic progress.

Course Delivery Methods: Ouachita will provide as much face-to-face classroom instruction as possible, consistent with health and safety guidelines to protect students, faculty and staff. In some cases, the installation of new instructional technology will allow students who are not present to participate remotely. Faculty are also working to expand their use of Moodle and other remote learning tools to make class content available through a variety of methods. If a faculty member is in a high-risk category, additional adjustments may be made to their classes.

Move-In: Move-in for all students will be Thursday through Saturday, Aug. 13-15, on a staggered schedule to minimize crowds. For detailed information, see https://obu.edu/reslife/move-in.php for returning student information and https://obu.edu/studentlife/move-in-information.php for new student information. 

Student Family Groups: Within a residence hall, based on shared bathrooms and common spaces, each student is assigned to a “Student Family Group” (SFG). Within your SFG, expectations about physical distancing and face masks are relaxed. 

Visitation Policies: Guests during open hall nights will be limited to one guest per room.

Increased Cleaning Protocols: Enhanced cleaning in all common areas and high-touch surfaces will be added to current procedures. These protocols will follow CDC guidelines for cleaning and disinfecting facilities for COVID-19.
