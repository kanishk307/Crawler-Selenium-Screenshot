Guidelines for Planning the Resumption of Field Research

Field research is a vital component of research in disciplines ranging from astronomy to anthropology to public health. This is activity that is carried out at an off-site location such as a community or region; a research station or facility, e.g., a telescope; a research vessel, aircraft or other platform; or a national lab or foreign lab.

Prior approval of any field research is required by the relevant dean, director or vice president, with a report to the Office of the Provost.

The following are guidelines for planning the resumption of field research:

  1. The principles of safety, fairness, volunteerism and privacy govern resumption of field research, just as they govern resumption of on-site research at Columbia. See the Columbia Framework for Restarting Research.
  2. Research restart plans for field research must comply to the extent possible with the same public health constraints as any other on-site research activity, including density restrictions, physical distancing, and any other identified public health requirements for on-site research at Columbia. Exceptions may apply if a particular research facility, as a result of its physical layout, implements risk reduction guidelines that are functionally equivalent to those guiding on-campus research in Columbia’s campuses.  As always, field research must comply with the University’s travel policies.
  3. Such plans must also comply with any locally imposed requirements, e.g., quarantine requirements for travelers from New York.
  4. Restart plans for field research should outline the types of activities, the risks involved, risk mitigation strategy, and the plan for response in the event of a suspected or confirmed COVID-19 incident in the field.  Fieldwork restart plans must address the following concerns in relation to COVID-19:  travel risk, including both travel to the field site and travel while at the field site; food and lodging risk at the field site; and research activity risk at the field site.
  5. Field research restart plans must consider the risk to the field community, as well as that to the researcher and to the campus upon the researcher’s return.
  6. Field work requiring in-person social interactions, including human subjects research, will require a detailed plan to be reviewed in accordance with University and school procedures.
