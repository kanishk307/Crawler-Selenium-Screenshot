Campus Operations Spring 2020

We’re doing our best to support our normal daily activities during this time of uncertainty related to the global outbreak of COVID-19. Campus remains open, and services are available with modified hours. We’ll keep you and NIU’s coronavirus (COVID19) site updated with relevant information as this global health situation evolves.

The Center for Student Assistance will serve as a call center for students in need of services and support. Call 815-753-8300 or email studentassistance@niu.edu.

Campus Operation Status
Academic Advising Center
  • Services are available in a non-face-to-face format.
  • Email aac@niu.edu or call 815-753-2533 for assistance.
ACCESS Tutoring and Support Services
  • Services are available in a non-face-to-face format.
  • SI tutoring sessions in select courses from March 16 to April 4 will be held online through our Blackboard Collaborate account.
  • Effective March 23, all SI sessions will take place virtually.
  • If you have any questions, please contact us at NIUSI@niu.edu.
Administration and Finance
  • See contact information and updates on how to make emergency purchases, get reimbursed for university-related travel, contact someone about building maintenance, make departmental deposits, get mail for your unit or department, schedule video conferencing calls with colleagues and more.
Art Museum
  • The galleries are closed until further notice.
Bursar's Office
  • Our Cashiering Office is now working remotely. Therefore, payments can only be made online on MyNIU or by mail, not in person.
  • Account counselors will be available to review your account and advise payment options in non-face-to-face format Monday through Friday, 8 a.m.-4:30 p.m. 
  • Email bursar@niu.edu for student account questions or appointment requests and accountsreceivable@niu.edu for commercial account questions.
Campus Dining

At this time, Campus Dining Services remains closed. The Where to Eat page and Campus Dining homepage will be continuously updated with the latest information.
Career Services
  • Staff members are available remotely Monday through Friday, 8:30 a.m.-4:30 p.m.
  • Email CareerServices@niu.edu with your name, major, email address, phone number, the best time to reach you and the topic you'd like to discuss with our staff.
  • For employers or others with questions about engaging with us, please email CareerServices@niu.edu and we'll reach out to you to assist with your needs.
Center for Student Assistance
  • The Center for Student Assistance is available as a call-in service for students during this uncertain time.
  • Contact the CSA at 815-753-8300 or studentassistance@niu.edu.
  • The CSA can help students get connected to support for questions related to off-campus housing, food insecurity needs or legal matters.
  • Emergency food bags also are available through the Department of Police and Public Safety.
CHANCE
  • Services are available in a non-face-to-face format.
  • Email chance@niu.edu for assistance.
Child Development and Family Center
  • The center will be closed from Friday, March 20, to April 5, 2020.
College of Law
  • COVID-19 FAQ for admitted and prospective students
Community Counseling Training Center
  • In-office counseling services are suspended.
  • The clinic aims to reopen during the week of March 30 for telehealth services only.
  • The clinic is open for video and phone conferencing telehealth counseling services. Complete a request for services form online. 
Counseling and Consultation Services
  • Counseling and Consultation Services is currently closed for face-to-face appointments. Staff members are working remotely to provide support.
  • If you need counseling or consultation support, please call 815-753-1206. You can leave a message if you have a general question or speak to a counselor if you need immediate support.

  • See details about emergency food bags and community food pantries.

Jobs PLUS

  • Resources are available on the literacy clinic’s Facebook page, which includes literacy tips and read-alouds.

  • Please see a guide to library services, resources, research and instruction support during this period.
  • The Ask a Librarian tool, an online chat providing help from librarians, will operate during normal library hours during the spring semester.
