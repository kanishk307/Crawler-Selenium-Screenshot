Return to Campus

Information for Faculty and Staff

All faculty and staff are expected to comply fully with the policies, protocols and guidelines outlined below to ensure a safe return to campus. Adherence to the policies and guidelines requires the cooperation of everyone involved. Failure to do so puts our entire community at risk and may result in disciplinary action.

 

Important

No one should come to work on campus until they are notified by their unit they can do so. It is very important that all faculty and staff who are able to work remotely continue to do so unless otherwise instructed by their dean, department or program director, or supervisor.

Anyone returning to campus (or currently working on campus) will be required to complete the six steps below before they receive clearance to report. See “Before Returning to Campus,” below. This is a new guideline, effective May 28, 2020.

Returning to Campus - Phase 2

This section applies only to those who have been instructed and approved to return to campus in Phase 2 by their dean, department or program director, or supervisor.

"Return to Campus Guidelines for Faculty and Staff" (see link to PDF below) outlines the safety guidelines that are required to work on campus, the steps needed to be cleared to work on campus, workplace scenarios, training requirements and HR policies.

Please read these guidelines to ensure you understand the health and safety protocols that you must adhere to while on campus.

View Return to Campus Guidelines for Faculty and Staff (PDF)

If You Are Instructed to Return to Campus

If instructed to return, you will receive an email with the required steps to complete before returning.

  1. Complete a Health Screening Questionnaire.
  2. Sign a Return to Campus Expectations Agreement.
  3. Enroll in and complete “Mandatory COVID-19 Phased Return to Campus Training (200845)” in the Emory Learning Management System. View step-by-step instructions for enrolling.
  4. Enroll in the Emergency Notification System in PeopleSoft/Self-Service if you have not previously done so. This will be used for contact tracing in the event of COVID-19 exposure. Log into PeopleSoft, and for your initial sign-on, the Emergency Notification page will automatically open. Complete the form and OK to submit. You will be taken directly to Contact Tracing.
  5. Provide a list of close contacts with whom you regularly work at Emory (faculty, staff, or students) for contact tracing in the event of COVID-19 exposure. Log into PeopleSoft Self-Service > Click Notifications and Contacts > Click Contact Tracing. Complete the form and submit "OK." For future updates to the Emergency Notification System or Contact Tracing, select the Notification and Contacts tile in Self-Service.
  6. (Research Laboratory Only) Complete BioRAFT training for those who will be working in research laboratories.

Once you have completed all required steps, you will receive a confirmation email that you are cleared to return to campus (as directed by your supervisor/manager). Do not report to campus until you have been given this clearance and a date to report by your supervisor.

If you wish to check on the status of your progress, please visit the following link: https://returntocampus.emory.edu/

If you are a supervisor and wish to check on the status of your employees, please visit the following link: https://apps.hr.emory.edu/return-to-campus

Returning to Research

Phase 2: Metered Small-Scale Restart

May 28–June 21

This section applies only to those who have been instructed and approved to return to campus in Phase 2 by their dean, department or program director, or supervisor.

Emory University will commence a multi-phased plan to return gradually to campus for research.

During this phase, we expect to include, once approved, postdoctoral fellows and trainees as well as graduate students in return to research-related campus activities. It will be important for all faculty and staff who are able to work remotely to continue to do so unless otherwise instructed by their dean, department or program director, or supervisor. Our priority is to protect the health and safety of all members of our community and campus visitors by adhering to a low campus density during Phase 2. Our campus is safe to re-enter, but higher levels of activity will require implementation of a variety of public health measures that necessitate careful planning and monitoring.

If you have been approved to return to campus for work, you will receive ​information regarding BioRAFT training and guidelines from Human Resources.
