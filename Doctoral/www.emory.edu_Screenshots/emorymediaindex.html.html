Media

Emory experts are sharing their expertise with various media outlets as COVID-19 remains a top global public health threat. Faculty members across disciplines, including infectious diseases, drug development, public health, gynecology/obstetrics, business, nursing and law, aim to educate the public in safety precautions, prevention, preparedness, potential treatments and more.

Latest Updates and Coverage

Featured

Emory experts available to discuss COVID-19

Emory has a number of faculty members and health care professionals who can speak about many facets of the COVID-19 outbreak.

Emory doctors study link between thickness of blood, clotting and inflammation in COVID-19 patients

After noticing unusual blood clotting in many patients diagnosed with COVID-19, doctors at Emory University believe there may be a connection to the thickness of their blood, known as hyperviscosity, with inflammation and clotting.

Emory expert provides insight on caring for women planning pregnancy, currently pregnant or delivering a baby during the pandemic

Whether planning a pregnancy, currently pregnant or delivering a baby during the COVID-19 pandemic, an Emory University expert says critical information is limited about the effects of coronavirus on pregnant women and their newborns and much remains to be learned.

Behavioral studies in era of COVID-19 raise new concerns about diversity

The COVID-19 pandemic is accelerating an ongoing trend in cognitive psychology to conduct human behavioral experiments online.

Emory, partners receive $31 million COVID-19 testing grant

Emory University is sharing in a $31 million federal grant designed to rapidly transform innovative technologies into widely accessible COVID-19 diagnostic testing.

Emory researchers report lower ICU and ventilator mortality in critically ill adults with COVID-19

As COVID-19 has become the leading cause of death in the U.S., researchers at Emory University report a lower number of deaths than what most have reported for critically ill COVID-19 patients using mechanical ventilation.
