Other Resources

In these extraordinary times, the Coronavirus Aid, Relief, and Economic Security Act (CARES) has been implemented by the federal government, but we all need a little more help. Emory cares for you, is here for you and wants you to know that you are not alone. We have compiled this summary to help you know where to get the help that we all need.

Unemployment Compensation

Employees who are out of work through no fault of their own may qualify for unemployment benefits in accordance with state rules, which vary by state. In Georgia, the calculation is approximately 61 percent of the prior weekly earning, up to $365 per week, for a maximum of 20 weeks. Under the CARES act, the unemployment compensation has been greatly enhanced:

  1. Extends the state’s maximum number of weeks of unemployment by an additional 13 weeks.
  2. Adds a flat amount of $600 per week to the maximum allowable weekly amount.

Resources:

  • Determine unemployment compensation by state.
  • File Georgia Unemployment.

Mortgage Payments

There are two types of help in the new CARES act for homeowners who have been affected by the pandemic. First, it prohibits lenders from starting foreclosure proceedings on properties for at least 60 days, from March 18, for federally backed mortgages. Second, it allows homeowners who are challenged financially to request forbearance (pause) on the mortgage payment for up to 180 days. After 180 days, the homeowner has the option to request another forbearance for up to 180 days.

For non-federally-backed mortgages — that is, mortgages owned by banks and other private lenders — these measures do not apply. However, some states are working on mortgage relief with private lenders. In New York, lenders have been urged to waive mortgage payments for up to 90 days. If your mortgage is privately held, contact your lender to find out if any assistance is available.

Several banks have announced forbearance or other relief plans. See this article in Forbes.

Georgia has an existing mortgage-assistance program for homeowners who are unemployed.

Student Loan Payments

Under the CARES act, student loan payments are suspended interest-free for six months until September 30, 2020, for most student borrowers. This relief is retroactive to March 13, which means if a payment has been made since that date, you can request a refund.

Student loan programs with private lenders such as Perkins Loans and FFEL are not eligible.

Economic Impact Payments

Eligible taxpayers who filed tax returns for either 2019 or 2018 will automatically receive an economic impact payment of up to $1,200 for individuals or $2,400 for married couples and up to $500 for each qualifying child.

The economic payment will be reduced for individuals with a gross income of $75,000 and phased out completely at $99,000. For joint filers, the phase-out begins at $150,000 and is complete at $198,000. These amounts will be sent by direct deposit April 13, 2020 and are not subject to tax.

Rent Payments

There is no relief in the CARES act for rent payment (unlike mortgages). However, various states have taken action to prevent landlords from evicting renters who have been adversely affected by the pandemic. On March 17, 2020, Mayor Keisha Lance Bottoms issued an executive order declaring a temporary moratorium on evictions in the city of Atlanta for 60 days.

Tax Return

The 2019 income tax filing and payment deadlines for all taxpayers who file and pay their federal income taxes on April 15, 2020, are automatically extended until July 15, 2020. The relief is automatic and no forms need to be filed. Tax payments are also extended to July 15.

If you are owed a refund, you should of course file for a refund as quickly as possible.

Credit Card Payments

In times of financial distress, making timely credit card payments can be a challenge. While there is no universal relief plan, the website Credit Karma has compiled a list of credit card issuers and any relief plan they are offering borrowers.
