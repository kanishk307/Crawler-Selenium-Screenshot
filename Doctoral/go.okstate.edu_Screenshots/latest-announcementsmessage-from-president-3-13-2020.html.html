From the President

 

OSU-Stillwater, OSU-Tulsa Campuses,

 

It has been an unprecedented, rapidly changing week for our campuses. While we are in uncharted territory, nothing is more important than the safety and health of our University family.

 

As students depart for spring break and many employees also take time away, I want to recap the steps we have taken to do our part to help prevent the spread of coronavirus COVID-19.

 

Following spring break, on Monday, March 23, all in-person classes on our Stillwater and OSU-Tulsa campuses will be conducted online for two weeks. Faculty members will direct students on how to move forward with their online classes.

 

All University activities have been postponed or cancelled until April 5. Please contact event organizers if you have any questions. The University will be providing updates throughout the next few weeks.

 

In the meantime, we each have an important role to play in helping eliminate the spread of unofficial information and rumors. Your help is vital to our communications efforts moving forward. Please exercise care making sure your conversations are thoughtful and your comments are factual.

 

I appreciate the incredible coordination and cooperation we have received from our faculty and staff leadership. They have helped us assess the situation and determine the steps necessary to make sure we continue to provide instruction to our students.

 

Let me also express my appreciation to the OSU leadership team’s work with health officials and others to make the tough decisions we have had to make this week.

 

OSU will be providing updates as soon as possible. I encourage everyone to take all health and safety precautions.

 

All of us have a responsibility to care for each other, and that starts by staying informed and making smart decisions so we can keep our campus communities safe.

 

Sincerely,

 

V. Burns Hargis

 

Oklahoma State University
