Guiding Principles

RIT’s guiding principles for responding to the COVID-19 pandemic are rooted in concern for the health and safety of our students, faculty, staff, visitors, and other invited guests. Our plans are aligned and consistent with applicable local, state, and federal laws, as well as the guidelines published as part of New York’s Forward: A Guide to Re-opening New York and Building Back Better (“New York Forward”).  Our plans recognize our shared responsibility for keeping the RIT Community healthy and safe, and include symptom monitoring, physical distancing, reducing the density of individuals on the RIT campus, personal safety practices, regular cleaning and disinfection, restricting non-essential travel, contact tracing, and communication. As our knowledge and understanding of the pandemic continues to evolve, and additional guidelines are promulgated for colleges and universities, so too will this Workplace Safety Plan.

Faculty, staff, visitors, and other invited guests (the “RIT Community”) are expected to fully comply with the provisions of this Workplace Safety Plan and other policies, procedures, and guidelines designed to protect the health and safety of all individuals on the RIT campus. RIT students are expected to fully comply with the provisions of this Workplace Safety Plan and also other policies, procedures and guidelines applicable to them. Failure to do so may result in corrective action. The expectation is that every individual will take the steps necessary to protect each other and the community at large.

Procedures and Guidelines

On-Campus vs. Remote Work

RIT expects to be open for on-campus instruction in the Fall. As a result, faculty and staff members with requirements to be on campus will be expected to do so. However, in accordance with the requirements of New York Forward, RIT will limit the total number of occupants at any given time to no more than 50% of the maximum occupancy for a particular area. It shall be the responsibility of the divisional vice president to ensure that 50% of the maximum occupancy for a particular area is maintained. In order to accomplish this goal, if a faculty or staff member’s work can be accomplished remotely and there are no requirements for a faculty or staff member to be on campus, then they will be given the opportunity to work remotely. Additionally, if flexible work schedules can be implemented, managers/supervisors may create these arrangements. The remote work and flexible work schedule arrangements shall continue until such time that their work requires them to be on campus, or until the U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) and/or the New York State Department of Health (DOH) declare an end to the COVID-19 pandemic and notification to return to campus is issued by RIT. By providing the opportunity to work remotely and flexible work schedules, we will limit the density of the on campus population and support safety for those who are required to work on campus.   

Tele- or video-conferencing is encouraged for administrative functions and/or meetings. In-person meetings shall comply with the physical distancing requirements of this Workplace Safety Plan and the COVID-19 Maximum Occupancy requirements. Physical distancing markers, directional indicators, and designated areas for pick-ups and deliveries shall be observed at all times.

If a manager/supervisor desires to create a plan for their individual department, they may work with a Human Resources Manager to create such a plan. All individualized safety plans must comply with the requirements of this Workplace Safety Plan. A form to assist in the creation of this plan is available. Faculty and staff who have concerns about returning to work on campus due to a medical condition that places them in a higher risk group, or those who wish to seek an accommodation in order to perform the essential functions of their job, should visit the accommodation request form.

Symptom Monitoring

Each day, prior to coming to campus, all members of the RIT Community must be free of any symptoms related to COVID-19. These symptoms include:

  • Loss of sense of smell or taste
  • Chills or fever of 100⁰ F (37.8⁰ C)
  • Sore throat (not due to allergies)
  • Feeling like coming down with an illness (e.g., fatigue or muscle ache)
  • Unusual headache or eye pain
  • New cough or change in your cough
  • New or worsening shortness of breath (difficult to breathe)
  • Abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, or loss of appetite (skipping a meal)
  • Contact with a confirmed case of COVID-19

All members of the RIT Community will be asked to report, on a daily basis, regardless of whether or not they will be coming to campus that day, if they had any of the listed symptoms in the last twenty-four (24) hours that is new or unusual for them. All members of the RIT Community shall report this information to an individual designated by the appropriate divisional vice president or their designee as a Site Safety monitor.

RIT shall arrange for members of the RIT Community to be tested for COVID-19 exposure if an individual exhibits symptoms of COVID-19 or if they have been in close contact (as defined by the Monroe County Department of Health) with individuals that have tested positive for COVID-19. In addition, RIT shall engage in surveillance testing designed to determine if outbreaks are occurring in specific areas of campus. Anyone who has tested positive for COVID-19, or who has any symptom related to COVID-19, shall not come to work, or if at work shall leave campus immediately, self-quarantine for 14 days from the positive COVID-19 test result or onset of COVID-19 related symptoms, and consult with a health care professional. That individual will be asked to provide to the Director of RIT’s Environmental Health and Safety with all of the contacts they had with other individuals while on the RIT campus during the time period that the individual was infectious. This information shall be provided to contact tracers in accordance with the requirements established by the Monroe County and New York State Departments of Health.

All employees should follow RIT’s normal process for reporting absences due to illness and RIT’s Short Term Disability process based on their employment category:

  • Regular Employees
  • Adjunct Faculty and Staff
  • Student Employees and RIT Co-ops

Employees testing positive, or residing with someone testing positive, for COVID-19 shall be required to report this information to the Director of RIT’s Environmental Health and Safety.

Individuals with certain conditions are at an increased risk for severe illness. The CDC has compiled a list that identifies these conditions. Currently, they are:

  • Older adults (age 65 and older)
  • Chronic Kidney disease
  • COPD (chronic obstructive pulmonary disease)
  • Immunocompromised state (weakened immune system) from solid organ transplant
  • Obesity (body mass index (BMI) of 30 or higher)
  • Serious heart conditions, such as heart failure, coronary artery disease, or cardiomyopathies
  • Sickle cell disease
  • Type 2 diabetes mellitus

As more is known about COVID-19, and as new information becomes available, the CDC will update this list and so will RIT.

Faculty and staff who are at an increased risk for severe illness from COVID-19 infection may request an accommodation in order to perform the essential functions of their job, by completing the COVID-19 Related Accommodation Request form.

Physical Distancing and Face Coverings

Physical distancing requires the RIT Community to ensure that they maintain at least six (6) ft. of distance between themselves, unless safety or core function of the work activity requires a shorter distance. Any time members of the RIT Community are indoors in the presence of others or moving through space occupied by others, they must wear acceptable face coverings regardless of the distance between themselves and others. RIT shall provide face coverings to employees at no cost. Acceptable face coverings include:

  • Cloth face covering: Homemade or commercially manufactured face coverings that are washable. They must be washed or replaced on a daily basis. Unless the responsibilities of the member of the RIT Community requires use of a specific face covering, use of personal face coverings is allowed, provided that it permits for proper covering of the nose and mouth, and is in compliance with all applicable RIT policies, including but not limited to, RIT’s Policy Prohibiting Discrimination, Harassment and Retaliation (C6.0). Members of the RIT Community shall be responsible for procuring, washing, and/or replacing personal cloth face coverings.
  • Disposable mask: Commercially manufactured and disposed of on a daily basis.
  • Medical grade surgical mask: FDA approved for use in healthcare settings. To be replaced in accordance with manufacturer’s instructions.
  • N95 Respirator: NIOSH approved for use in healthcare settings or other approved areas with known respiratory hazards. To be replaced in accordance with manufacturer’s instructions.
  • Accessible mask: Masks provided to individuals based on requests for accommodations due to a disability as defined under applicable state and/or federal law. Requests for accessible masks will be considered on an individualized basis and in accordance with RIT’s Workplace Accommodations process.

Face coverings must be cleaned or replaced after use or when damaged or soiled, may not be shared, and should be properly stored or discarded. Face coverings are not required if outdoors and at least 6 feet of distance can be maintained from others, or if working alone in an office or private room, except as required by another policy or procedure. Faculty, in classrooms with barriers installed between themselves and students, may use face coverings while lecturing behind the barrier but are not required to do so. Tightly confined spaces (e.g., elevators, kitchenettes, restrooms, small conference rooms, cubicles) shall be occupied by only one (1) individual at a time, unless all occupants are wearing face coverings and all other physical distancing rules can be followed. If occupied by more than one person, the occupancy of these tightly confined areas shall be kept under 50% of normal maximum capacity. Compliance with COVID-19 Maximum Occupancy signs shall be required.

Hygiene

While on campus, the RIT Community shall limit the sharing of objects, including desks, supplies, and workstations. The touching of shared surfaces is discouraged. If shared surfaces must be touched, the RIT Community shall either wear gloves or sanitize or wash hands before and after contact. Hygiene and sanitation requirements from the CDC and the DOH shall be adhered to. These requirements include:

  • Washing hands with soap and water for at least 20 seconds
  • Use hand sanitizer when you cannot use soap and water
  • Avoid touching your eyes, nose, and mouth with unwashed hands
  • Cover your cough and sneezes
  • Avoid sharing of personal items
Movement on Campus and Contact Tracing

Posted signage throughout campus is intended to remind the RIT Community to adhere to proper hygiene, physical distancing rules, appropriate use of face coverings, and cleaning and disinfecting protocols. Posted signage, including any markings on floors and hard surfaces, shall be complied with at all times.

The RIT Community shall comply with reasonable requests for information relating to individuals who they may have had close contact with while on campus for purposes of contact tracing. This may include maintaining a manual continuous log of people entering RIT facilities, utilizing personal electronic devices and RIT information systems to allow location tracking, and using specific electronic applications.

Visitors/Contractors/Temporary Employees

All visitors, contractors, and temporary employees are expected to comply with applicable laws, regulations and guidelines relating to the COVID-19 pandemic. For businesses, this includes compliance with industry specific guidelines, both mandatory and recommended best practices, and this Workplace Safety Plan. For visitors, contractors, and temporary employees, this includes symptom monitoring, contact tracing, and health screenings. Prior to providing goods and services to RIT, businesses shall be required to present proof that they have read and understood their obligations to operate in accordance with their industry specific DOH Interim Guidance During the COVID-19 Public Health Emergency, RIT specific guidelines, and this Workplace Safety Plan. This information shall be provided to the Procurement Services Office.

Visitors, contractors and temporary employees are also required to adhere to the requirements of this Workplace Safety Plan. Failure to comply with applicable laws, regulations, guidelines, and/or this Workplace Safety Plan shall result in the removal of visitors, contractors and temporary employees until such time as assurances can be provided that compliance has been achieved.

Mental Health and Emotional Well-Being

COVID-19 has altered many facets of life for members of the RIT Community. These changes can take a toll on our emotional well-being and may result in stress, fear and/or anxiety. Please be aware that the following resources and services are available to support you.
