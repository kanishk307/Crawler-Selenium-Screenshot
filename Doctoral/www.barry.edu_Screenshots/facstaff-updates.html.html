Faculty and Staff Updates

May 11, 2020 4:00 PM EST

Campus Access

With parts of the state moving toward reopening, we’d like to remind all faculty and staff that Barry University continues to heavily restrict access to all campuses, to protect community health and comply with safer at home orders that remain in effect.

Should you need to access main campus, please be sure to notify public safety in advance by emailing John Buhrmaster, Director of Public Safety and Emergency Management (jbuhrmaster@barry.edu).

For access to the Law School, please email Law School Dean Leticia Diaz (ldiaz@barry.edu) in advance of your visit.

In both locations face masks are required in all communal spaces and no guests are permitted. Please be sure to bring your Barry University ID when accessing campus.

Travel Reporting Requirements

Additionally, ahead of any summer travel please keep in mind reporting requirements the university has put in place during the pandemic. All planned international or domestic travel, including cruise ship travel, must be reported to Student Health Services (305-899-3750 or healthservices@barry.edu).

A two-week quarantine upon return may be required before accessing any Barry University campus or site.

As a reminder, members of the University community should NOT come to campus if experiencing any cold or flu-like symptoms including, but not limited to, fever, cough or shortness of breath.

March 16, 2020 3:30 PM EST

A Message for Faculty and Staff Regarding Barry University Operations

The last few days have brought many changes to national and local guidance and expectations for addressing the spread of coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19). These changes are especially relevant for Barry University given the recent community spread of the disease in South Florida. Most notably, the expectation that individuals should work remotely has intensified, and the US Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) released new guidelines indicating gatherings of 50 people or more should be suspended for at least the next eight weeks. The closure of local public schools has further elevated the necessity for remote work. 

Following these changes, the Command Decision Team is implementing additional measures to protect the health and safety of our University community and to assist in slowing the progression of the virus throughout the wider community.

  • Today, we have determined the remainder of the semester will be taught remotely. We believe this will be helpful to students as they plan to return home, and to faculty as they coordinate their teaching schedules for the semester.  
  • Barry University is transitioning to a remote workplace, with only personnel supporting essential basic functions reporting to campus, effective no later than Thursday, March 19.
    • Supervisors of staff supporting essential basic functions will be in contact regarding next steps for on campus work. The scope of essential basic functions will be determined by the Provost and Divisional Vice Presidents and may evolve over time.
    • All other staff should communicate with supervisors to coordinate plans for remote work. Business operations will continue remotely. We are committed to completing the academic semester.
    • Helpful resources for remote work can be found here.
    • It is imperative that all staff and faculty ensure their contact information is up to date in Workday.
    • All faculty and staff who come to any campus are required to report any recent personal travel (domestic or international) utilizing mass transportation to Student Health Services, as well as any known potential exposure to COVID-19 discovered after coming to campus.
    • Residential students are being urged to go home; however, given our large international and out-of-state populations, we expect that some percentage of students will need to remain on campus. We are preparing to provide essential services for our remaining residential students for an extended time frame, including food service, campus safety, and facility support.
    • The transition to a remote working environment includes the suspension of Federal Work-Study. Federal Work-Study students will be paid for the remaining scheduled hours for the semester even though they are unable to complete the work requirement. The payroll department will facilitate this process.
    • Student employment is also suspended, with exceptions for remote work granted on a case-by-case basis by supervisors. Student employees will be paid for all hours worked through today.
    • All on campus meetings and events are cancelled until further notice. Effective immediately, all meeting organizers should arrange for meetings to occur remotely.
    • Daily and Sunday Mass is suspended until further notice.

This situation continues to change rapidly, and we will be vigilant to remain in alignment with the very latest guidance from federal, state and local authorities. Additional information regarding Residence Halls and Student Services will be posted later today at www.barry.edu/prepare.

We will continue to dedicate ourselves to navigating this together, always with health and safety at the forefront. Thank you for your ongoing flexibility.
