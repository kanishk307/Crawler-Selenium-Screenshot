Return to Workplace Guidance Document

Facilities Access

Only a small percentage of individuals should be currently accessing University facilities, and employees should not return to their workspaces unless instructed to by their supervisor. If you do not currently have access and have a legitimate need to access a University facility, communicate your need to your supervisor and request access approval.

Access for research purposes should follow the prescribed process outlined on the COVID-19 Research Resources page.

Employee Assistance Program

Clemson encourages employees to contact the Employee Assistance Program (EAP) for assistance with concerns related to the current situation. The EAP has resources available specific to COVID-19. For more information, please visit Clemson’s EAP site.

Supervisor Resources

The University’s return to workplace may involve employees temporarily working staggered and alternating schedules, and consequently State HR and Clemson’s Office of Human Resources strongly recommend you provide a minimum of seven calendar days’ notice of your employee returning to their workplace.

Return-to-Workplace Expectations

The return to workplace (RTW) process will be fluid, requiring flexibility and patience from all faculty and staff. This document is intended to address questions members of your team may have about returning to their workplace. The Office of Human Resources (OHR) prepared these return-to-workplace expectations to help keep all employees safe and protected from the spread of COVID-19.

  • Return-to-Workplace Expectations (PDF)

Return to Workplace Notification Template for Supervisors

The University’s return to workplace plan may involve employees temporarily working staggered and alternating schedules, and consequently State HR and Clemson’s Office of Human Resources strongly recommend you provide a minimum of seven calendar days’ notice of your employee returning to their workplace.

Supervisors can use this email template(DOC) to notify their direct reports.
