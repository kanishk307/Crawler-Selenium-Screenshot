COVID-19 Medical Reference Guide

This page provides guidance to students, faculty and staff who are sick, have tested positive or suspect they may have been exposed to COVID-19.

How to Protect Yourself

COVID-19 Screening and Testing Sites

Many health centers are continuing to test for COVID-19. Please utilize DHEC's comprehensive list of mobile testing clinics and telehealth virtual care providers to find availability in your area.

If you are sick:

  • Remain home.
  • Complete the University’s COVID-19 Self-Assessment on the my.clemson app or online. If the results are to contact your healthcare provider, proceed with this checklist.
  • Call your health care provider. If you do not have a health care provider:
    • Students contact Redfern Health Center at 864-656-3571
    • Employees contact the Joseph F. Sullivan Center at 864-986-1218
  • Notify the University.
    • Students contact Redfern Health Center at 864-656-3571
    • Main campus employees
      • Contact the Joseph F. Sullivan Center at 864-986-1218
      • Contact your supervisor
    • Innovation Campus, REC and Extension Office employees
      • Contact your local healthcare provider
      • Contact the Joseph F. Sullivan Center at 864-986-1218
      • Contact your supervisor
  • Organizations managing the areas should follow general cleaning and disinfecting guidelines from Clemson Occupational & Environmental Safety.

If you have tested positive for COVID-19:

  • Isolate at home immediately and avoid contact with others.
  • Notify the University. As part of the notification process, the proper authorities will be notified of areas that require cleaning. 
    • Students notify Redfern Health Center by sending a secure message to the nurse via MyHealth-e or by calling 864-656-3571. We ask that those calling please be patient as they are experiencing larger than normal call volumes.
    • Employees
      • Contact the Joseph F. Sullivan Center at 864-986-1218
      • Contact your supervisor
      • Contractors and visitors notify your University point of contact
  • Organizations managing the areas should follow general cleaning and disinfecting guidelines from Clemson Occupational & Environmental Safety.

If you feel you may have been exposed:

  • If possible, find out if the individual has tested positive or not.
  • You should monitor yourself closely for symptoms and check your temperature twice daily.
  • It is recommended that you self-isolate for 14 days after exposure, including avoiding trips to grocery stores or pharmacies. You should try to have others deliver supplies to you while avoiding contact with other.
  • If you develop symptoms, refer to the "If you are sick" section atop this page.
  • Special cleaning procedures are not required for exposed but asymptomatic individuals.

If you have been exposed to someone who is exposed:

  • You are not at an increased risk if the individual tests negative.
  • If that individual tests positive, or develops symptoms within 2 days of the contact:
    • You should monitor yourself closely for symptoms and check your temperature twice daily.
    • It is recommended that you self-isolate for 14 days after exposure, including avoiding trips to grocery stores or pharmacies. You should try to have others deliver supplies to you while avoiding contact with other.
    • If you develop symptoms, contact your healthcare provider for further advice.
    • Special cleaning procedures are not required for exposed but asymptomatic individuals.
