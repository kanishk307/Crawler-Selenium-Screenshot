Message from Provost Rowlands: May 14

To: Faculty, Staff & Students
From: Provost Sue Ott Rowlands
Date: 5/14/20
Subject: Requirements for Facial Coverings

Dear Campus Community,

As we prepare to safely return to campus operations in the COVID-19 environment, the university will continue to require facial coverings for anyone present on NKU’s campus. In keeping with Governor Beshear’s order, this requirement prioritizes the health and safety of our campus community.

Facial coverings are required for faculty, staff, students, vendors and visitors upon the individuals’ next return to campus and will continue through the summer and may be extended into the fall. 

  • Facial coverings must be worn at all times on campus except while eating or while alone in a private room or office.
  • Facial coverings should cover the nose, mouth, and chin.
  • Currently, reusable and disposable masks are available for essential employees working on campus and for students living in campus housing.
  • As the university expands its on-campus activities, reusable and disposable masks will be provided for faculty and staff who need to be present on campus. Pick-up will be available in the campus mailroom (9 a.m. – 2 p.m., Monday – Friday)
  • As the university expands on-campus instruction, masks for enrolled students will be available at the university bookstore.
  • NKU will follow the exemptions outlined by Governor’s order. Anyone with a medical condition that prevents them from covering their nose and mouth, should not do so. Children under the age of two also should not cover their face.

We understand this requirement may generate a number of questions, and we ask that you send your questions to covid19@nku.edu. We will address your questions and will compile the frequently asked ones.

Kentucky’s facial covering requirement is part of the Governor’s Healthy at Work effort to begin to ease restrictions amid COVID-19. For more details on the order and the minimum requirements for businesses to reopen, visit the Commonwealth’s website. 

As the COVID-19 Preparedness Team outlines other steps needed to safely return to campus, we will share weekly updates every Tuesday. We will also be asking for your input as we shape our fall experience in the COVID-19 environment.   

Thank you for your support of the university and its campus community. Please stay safe and well.

Sincerely,

Sue Ott Rowlands
Provost and Executive Vice President for Academic Affairs
