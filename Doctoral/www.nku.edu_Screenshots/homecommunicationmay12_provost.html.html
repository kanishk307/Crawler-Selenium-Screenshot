Message from Provost Rowlands: May 12

To: Students, Faculty & Staff

From: Provost Sue Ott Rowlands

Date: 5/12/20

Subject: Update from COVID-19 Preparedness Team

 

Dear Campus Community, 

We have reached a milestone in our COVID-19 environment­—completing the academic year in remote operations and instruction. When we assembled the COVID-19 Preparedness Team in late February, no one imagined we would not return to finish the spring term. I continue to be impressed with how our students, faculty and staff have risen to the occasion and adapted to the remote operations.

We are now entering a new phase as Kentucky and Ohio begin to ease restrictions and keeping our campus community informed and engaged is a priority. The COVID-19 work groups are discussing how to safely bring people back onto campus. 

This is not a decision that can be made in a silo, so our first order of business was to engage the health, business and community leaders in a discussion to understand the constraints and factors we should consider. There were many good points raised in the conversation last week—discussion on testing and health requirements as well as reminders that NKU serves as a regional leader. Our actions will ripple out into the community, so we must be mindful of this as we proceed. In fact, Northern Kentucky Chamber President Brent Cooper’s parting thought was, “Lead us, like you’re taking the lead in training our future.” 

We need to consider the social aspect as we plan courses and help our students understand the benefits of this new college experience. And, NKU must continue to innovate and develop new programs that will keep our faculty, staff and students safe as well as inspire students to attend during these uncertain times. Our Board of Regent Vice-Chair Andrá Ward asked us to not try to plan the future with what we know of the past. He said, “COVID-19 will not give us that luxury anymore. We need the imagination required to free ourselves from the linear model of higher education.”

This is just a highlight of the dynamic discussion that took place last week. The feedback and recommendations will be taken into consideration as the President’s Cabinet and the COVID-19 Preparedness Team outline the steps to transition back to campus operations. As we begin the summer term, here are a few decisions that have been made and updates to share:

  • Credits for reimbursements for room, board and parking for students due to the campus COVID-19 disruption are currently being processed.  As a reminder, the credits will be applied to outstanding balances first.  Check your student account over the next few days for those credits.  Refunds, if applicable, will be processed as either ACH payments or checks by the end of this week
  • We opened up the NKU CARES Emergency Fund last week and have received nearly 1,000 applications. These federal and state funds are intended to help students with financial need related to the disruption of campus operations due to COVID-19, including costs for food, housing, course materials, technology, health care and childcare. More information can be found on the NKU CARES website.
  • The moratorium on university business travel will be maintained through June. We are following Kentucky’s guidelines for personal, out-of-state travel, as outlined in this official Executive Order.
  • Remote operations will continue through June as well. The determination for July has not been made yet, but it is likely that remote working will continue through the summer. Human Resources is outlining new training and guidelines for when we return to working on campus.

The COVID-19 Preparedness Team provided a recommendation to the President’s Cabinet on facial coverings while on NKU’s campus. More details on the plan will be shared on Thursday. Also, please keep an eye out every Tuesday for recaps of the various work group and community discussions, as well as next steps in the coming week. We will also be asking for your feedback to help shape these decisions. As President Vaidya shared in the Spring Forum, we need your active engagement to successfully navigate and thrive in this post-COVID world.

You can also visit our website for all the university’s COVID-19 updates and send questions to covid19@nku.edu.

Thank you for all you’re doing to support NKU during this uncharted time. Please stay safe and well.

Sincerely,

Sue Ott Rowlands
Provost and Executive Vice President for Academic Affairs
