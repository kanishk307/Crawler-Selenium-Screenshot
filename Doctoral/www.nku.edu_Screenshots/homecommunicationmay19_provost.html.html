Message from Provost Rowlands: May 19

To: Faculty, Staff & Students
From: Provost Sue Ott Rowlands
Date: 5/19/20
Subject: NKU Moving Forward – Fall Recommendation

Dear Campus Community, 

The COVID-19 Preparedness Team has shared a recommendation for the fall semester with the President Vaidya and his Cabinet. We recommended a hybrid approach that allows our campus to be open with restrictions. We are calling this plan NKU Moving Forward. Under this approach, NKU’s campus would open in stages as follows:

  • June 2020: Continuation of virtual operations with only essential personnel on campus;
  • July 1, 2020: A limited opening of some campus offices to offer services with reduced staffing; and
  • August 1, 2020: A wider opening of a blended, flexible environment that includes some in-person interaction with modifications that minimize the risk for our campus community. We will closely follow the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention and Kentucky’s guidelines.

We based our recommendation on three guiding principles: the health and safety of students, faculty and staff; student success; and academic excellence. NKU Moving Forward is a flexible plan that can incorporate new health guidelines and allow us to adjust our response based on the COVID-19 situation.

As President Vaidya shared in the Spring Forum, your feedback on this plan is essential. We all must take an active role to enhance NKU to be a more student-ready institution. This will be critical as we navigate the COVID-19 environment. Please click this link to view all the details and share your input. We will be taking feedback through Wednesday, May 27.

The COVID-19 Preparedness Team also recommended that the university continue to require facial coverings for anyone present on NKU’s campus. As we communicated last week, this requirement has been continued for the summer to prioritize the health and safety of our community. It follows Governor Beshear’s Healthy at Work effort to begin to ease restrictions amid COVID-19.

Our facial covering requirement impacts faculty, staff, students, vendors and visitors on their return to campus. We will be rolling out an awareness campaign to reinforce the message and to instill this safety measure into our campus culture. Keep an eye out for the “Norse Up/Cover Up” posters as we prepare to return to campus.

To more detailed information on the requirement, a facial coverings guidance has been posted on our COVID-19 webpage. Any questions not addressed in the guidance should be sent to covid19@nku.edu.

The university will continue to monitor the environment and may extend the facial covering requirement into the fall. Any adjustments to this requirement will be communicated in our weekly update messages. You can review all our COVID-19 communications and link to the NKU Moving Forward plan on our COVID-19 website.

We appreciate all you are doing for the university and its campus community. Please safe and well during the upcoming holiday weekend.

Sincerely,

Sue Ott Rowlands
Provost and Executive Vice President for Academic Affairs
