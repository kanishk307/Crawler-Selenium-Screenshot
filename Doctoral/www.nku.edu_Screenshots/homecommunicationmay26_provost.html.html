Message from Provost Rowlands: May 26

To: Faculty, Staff & Students
From: Provost Sue Ott Rowlands
Date: 5/26/20
Subject: Share Input on NKU Moving Forward Plan

Dear Campus Community,

I hope you had a chance to spend time with those close to you and enjoyed the holiday weekend. As we return to the work week, please take time to review our NKU Moving Forward plan that was shared last week. It’s a phased-in approach designed to incorporate new health guidelines based on the COVID-19 situation.

In our discussions on how to return to campus, three principles guided our decisions: the health and safety of students, faculty and staff; student success; and academic excellence. Opening our campus in stages allows us to address each principle and monitor the impacts of a phased-in return. We will still be in remote operations in June with only essential employees on campus. In July, some offices will begin offering on campus services with limited staff.

This all builds towards a wider opening in August that will have a blended, flexible environment. We are still working out how to manage density on campus and anticipate some type of staggered shifts. Please also note that parking fees and enforcement will begin again in August. As we outline the plan to keep our campus density at a level that aligns with the CDC guidelines, we will share it with campus for your input.

And, thank you to all of those who have shared feedback over the last week on our NKU Moving Forward plan. Your insights are essential to ensuring the plan supports all our campus constituents and better positions NKU to be a student-ready institution. If you have not had a chance to share feedback, click this link by tomorrow, May 27 to share input under each section. Please take the time to share your thoughts. Any questions on the NKU Moving Forward plan should be sent to covid19@nku.edu.

As more businesses and establishment open under Governor Beshear’s Healthy at Work effort, please stay safe as you venture out.

Remember our campus remains in remote operations in June. We will hold the Summer Faculty Institute online from June 22-26. Faculty who participate will receive a stipend, and there will be more information in my Provost Update email.

Thank you for all you do for our university.

Sincerely,

Sue Ott Rowlands
Provost and Executive Vice President for Academic Affairs
