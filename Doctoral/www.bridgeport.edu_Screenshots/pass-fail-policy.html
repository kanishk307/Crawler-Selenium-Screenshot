Pass/Fail Policy FAQs

 

How do I select pass/fail?

You should first speak with your academic advisor to be sure that you select courses that are eligible for pass/fail. After you have determined that your selected courses are eligible for the pass/fail option you should complete this form.

Are program pre-requisites eligible for pass/fail?

Any program requirements, including program pre-requisites, are not eligible for pass/fail; except for general studies majors. It is important that you check with your academic advisor to ensure that you select only courses that are eligible.

How will pass/fail grades impact my grade point average?

Courses selected for pass/fail will not be factored into your semester or cumulative grade point average.

What if I change my mind?

Once you select the pass/fail option for your course the decision cannot be reversed.

Does my instructor know?

Your course instructor will enter the grade you earn for your course. The pass/fail will be entered based on this grade in the Registrar’s Office.

What grade do I need to earn to pass?

For courses selected as pass/fail, grades of D- or higher will be entered as pass.

Can graduate students take courses pass/fail?

This policy only applies to undergraduate students. Graduate student grading remains unchanged.

What courses qualify for pass/fail?

It is important that you confer with your academic advisor regarding your specific classes. Generally speaking, electives and general education courses will quality. However, if you are taking a specific course for general education credit that has been outlined by your program, that course cannot be counted because it falls within program requirements.

What courses do not qualify for pass/fail?

Any courses within your major cannot be taken on a pass/fail basis. This includes program requirements that fall outside your academic department, and pre-requisite courses required for the major. If you are concerned about whether or not a class will qualify for pass/fail, please contact your academic advisor.

What is the deadline to elect pass/fail?

The final deadline is May 1, 2020. However, because academic advisor approval is an important step, you will need to contact your advisor well in advance of this date. No late requests will be accepted.

Are there other options?

The course withdrawal deadline has been extended to May 8, 2020 in case you determine that it is better to take a W in a course. You should talk with your academic advisor before deciding to take a W in a class. If you decide that a W is your best option, please complete this form.

Who should I contact for questions about this policy?

You should contact your faculty academic advisor. You can also contact the Academic Advising Center.
