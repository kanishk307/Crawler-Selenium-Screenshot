      Changes in Campus Housing

      Last Updated: Wednesday, March 18th, 2020 at 05:54 PM.
      • COVID-19
      • Campus Communications
      • FAQs
      • Students & Families
      • Faculty & Staff Resources
      • COVID-19 Related News

      March 18, 2020 • 3:30 p.m.

      Dear Residents:
       
      New developments in the COVID-19 pandemic make it clear that we need to decrease the density of people on our campus. To diminish the ongoing transmission of this dangerous infection, now is the time to return to your permanent residence. Per an executive order from Governor Gretchen Whitmer, services such as libraries, gym and recreation facilities, restaurants, theatres, and large gathering places have been closed. For those of you who still remain in the residence halls, you need to return home for your own health and safety, as well as the health and safety of others.
       
      Resident students will have until Sunday, March 22 at 5:00 p.m. to move out of the residence halls. Students who have completed the check-out process by this time will receive a credit of $900 (see Refund Process below). 

      Students who wish to remain in University Housing will need to first have their request approved. To apply to remain on campus, submit the REQUEST TO REMAIN IN HOUSING form and explain why you must remain on-campus during this time. This information must be received by noon on Thursday, March 19, 2020.

      If you do not respond, we will assume you have left campus and will discontinue building access. If you have already left campus, please contact Housing to arrange to complete the move-out process. Students who are approved to remain should expect the residence halls to consolidate.
       
      Note:

      • While Housing & Residential Life will ensure that we have sufficient housing available for students who truly have no other alternatives, we cannot promise that you will be able to remain in your current housing assignment. 
      • If you are assigned a new housing assignment you will be expected to move in order to remain eligible for housing.

      Refund Process
      Students eligible for a credit may select one of two options:

      •  through a credit towards next fall’s on-campus housing
      • through a credit on the student’s account (less any outstanding balance with the University, if a balance exists), which may involve e-Refund through Student Accounts.

      For students who receive federal financial aid, it is strongly encouraged that you contact UM-Flint Financial Aid to see how this credit may impact your financial aid determination. You are responsible for investigating and understanding the potential impact.  
       
      Students who receive institutional funds for their room and board – for example, housing scholarship recipients, Mpowering students, RAs, etc. - will not be eligible for this credit.  

      Directions for Checking Out
      1. Pick up an “Express Check-Out” form and envelope form at the front desk of your residence hall. 
      2. Plan to pack and clean your room, work with suite-mates who might be staying to talk about common bathroom and living area cleaning and remove all of your belongings, food before you leave and remove all your belongings, food, and trash. Then fill out a Room Condition inventory https://forms.gle/LcCGGnjpT2fq4XJG8 
      3. Bins/Carts are limited and can be checked out at the desk when you are packed and ready to move belongings to a car waiting in the loading zone. Cars should be moved from nearby campus lots into loading zones just before you are ready to take belongings to be loaded. 
      4. When your bedroom is empty, close, and lock the door. Fill out all the necessary information on your “Express Check-Out” form, seal your key in the envelope and bring it to the desk. The Desk Assistant will initial the form and provide you with the white copy for your records.
      5. Complete and sign form indicating the preferred method of refund.
       
      If you have any questions or concerns, please direct them to flint.housing@umich.edu. Those of you who are planning to move out must do so by 5:00 p.m. on Sunday, March 22, 2020.

      Please continue to stay connected to campus during this time, and we will continue to keep you updated on any additional changes as a result of the COVID-19 virus. 
       
      In good health,

      Christopher Giordano
      Vice Chancellor for Student Affairs
