      April 3, 2020 • 9:07 a.m.

      Dear Timekeepers,

      In an effort to provide you with the resources to support your needs as we navigate through these unprecedented times, we offer the information below, as well as links to Q&As and FAQs.  We recognize that coding timesheets and scheduling time away from work during these ever changing times can be confusing and we are here for you and can help!

      There are a few new charts which are helpful to walk through the most recent additions to our absence from work plans.  The chart links are listed at the bottom of this email. We also have started a Google Sheet to capture questions and provide answers to your campus timekeeping questions.  Our hope is that we can provide you with the most up-to-date answers using the google document. An invitation to use this google doc will be sent to you soon. Of course, you can still reach us using our contact information at the bottom of this email.

      What seems most important for you to know right now, is how to code the current biweekly timesheets for both regular staff and temporary employees (non-student, student and workstudy). The current biweekly payroll runs March 22, 2020 through April 4, 2020.  

      Working Remotely:

      If a staff member or temporary employee (non-student, student or workstudy) is working remotely, they simply need to record their time as they normally would on their timesheets.

      If a staff member or temporary employee is not working remotely, then please see the quick tips below and the google document.

      UM COVID-19 One-Time Bank until March 31, 2020:

      If an employee was using their COVID-19 one-time bank, they should do so until March 31, 2020, or until they run out, whichever comes first.  Please remember that part-time or temporary employees receive a prorated amount of the COVID-19 80 hour one-time bank. COVID-19 time should be recorded on the timesheet as if the employee was working versus providing a lump sum of hours in one timesheet.  Please see the charts & FAQs links below to determine the correct prorated amount of time. If the employee meets the COVID-19 criteria, please record the time away from work as “RPN” plus a second row of “PAN”. Please note, workstudy students may need to code their time differently.  See below.  

       

      Federal Emergency Paid Sick Leave (EPSLA):  Starting April 1, 2020

      EPSLA is a new bank of up to 80 hours of paid time off for COVID-19 related absences.  Part-time and temporary employees, including student employees, are eligible for a prorated amount.  The criteria for using this new code is very similar to using the COVID-19 one-time bank the University put into place on March 13, 2020.  Employees absent due to COVID-19 criteria need to start using the EPSLA timekeeping code on 4/1/2020 even if they still have a balance of hours remaining in the UM COVID-19 one-time bank. 

      • Full time employees are eligible for up to 80 hours.

      • Part-time employees are eligible for a prorated amount of 80 hours based on their percentage of effort in the past six months (September 14, 2019 through March 13, 2020).  Example: Employee has a 75% effort, they are eligible for 60 hours of paid time from EPSLA.  

      • Temporary employees, including student temporary employees, are eligible for a prorated amount of 80 hours based on either 1) The employee’s normal scheduled hours or 2) The average hours in a pay period over the past six months.  The supervisor or department head will need to look at which of the two provides the better benefit to the employee in order to determine the correct prorated amount. This is slightly different than how to determine the prorated amount under the University’s COVID-19 one-time bank.  Please see the attached chart regarding using the “PEL” code starting 4/1/2020.

      Important:  You may not see the “PEL” code in the table of timekeeping codes for biweekly employees.  Please change the view to be “day” then type in the date of “4/1/2020” which will then allow you to see the “PEL” code in the available timekeeping codes.

      Workstudy Appointments:

      Starting March 24, 2020, workstudy students were no longer able to work remotely or on campus.  However, as long as they had workstudy available they can continue their workstudy funding, even while not working.  They can continue to use their workstudy funding until they exhaust their workstudy funding or until 4/25/2020, whichever is sooner.  They may also be eligible for the EPSLA and UM COVID-19 one-time bank should their workstudy funding be exhausted in the month of April, and they meet the criteria.  More information can be found in the Q&As listed in the attached link or in our shared google document. While they have workstudy funds available and they are not working, please record “REG” and “PAN” for the normal time they would have been scheduled to work, until they exhaust their workstudy funds. If they worked on March 22nd or March 23rd, please record “REG” for that time.  

      Extended Family Medical Leave Act (EFMLA):

      Under new federal legislation, employees may be eligible for expanded paid time off to provide child care and cannot work remotely due to school closures and child care closures related to the COVID-19 pandemic.  Information regarding qualifications, limitations and other information can be found in the Q&As listed in the attached link or in our shared google document. Employees requesting to use EFMLA time need to submit a request form available by emailing Suzi Bye (sbye@umich.edu).

      • Paid Time Off Chart for March 13th-March 31st - https://hr.umich.edu/sites/default/files/u-m-covid-19-time-off-03-18-20.pdf
