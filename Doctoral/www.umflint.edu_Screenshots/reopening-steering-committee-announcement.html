      Reopening Steering Committee Announcement

      Last Updated: Monday, May 11th, 2020 at 04:01 PM.
      • COVID-19
      • Campus Communications
      • FAQs
      • Students & Families
      • Faculty & Staff Resources
      • COVID-19 Related News

      Colleagues;

      I hope this email finds you well, and that you’re staying healthy. 

      On behalf of the UM-Flint Cabinet, I want to extend our sincere appreciation to all employees who are doing the absolute best to navigate this period of time. We know this situation is difficult in a variety of ways, and you have our admiration for your willingness to continue to sacrifice and do what is best for the university and its students. 

      A question that is on everyone’s mind is, “When will the university reopen?”

      It is a question that will be answered, but as you probably are aware, it is a complicated process of how this will happen, when the time is right, and when it is safe for people to return to campus. It is important to caution everyone that this will not be a return to the normalcy we experienced in the pre-coronavirus environment. Rather, we will be adapting new protocols and practices that will be in the best interest of the health and safety of the campus community. 

      Governor Whitmer recently released a statewide plan for reopening when she announced the latest executive order. This will be one of the many documents that informs our reopening process. We expect our repopulation of campus to be like a slow turn of a dial and not a flip of a switch. 

      To help work through the myriad issues surrounding this very important question, I have formed the Reopening Steering Committee. This group is heading up various subgroups that are looking at virtually every aspect of our operation through the lens of a thoughtful and careful reopening. The members of the Reopening Steering Committee include:

      • Melissa Brown, Interim Director of University Communications and Marketing
      • Nicholas Gaspar, Director of Online and Extended Learning
      • George Hakim, Director of Facilities and Operations
      • Raymond Hall, Chief of Public Safety
      • Jen Hogan, Director of Event and Building Services
      • BJ Hull, Vice Chancellor for University Advancement
      • Mike Lane, Director of Environment, Health and Safety
      • Beth Manning, Director of University Human Resources
      • Shelby Newport, Associate Provost and Dean of Undergraduate Studies
      • Julie Snyder, Associate Vice Chancellor & Dean of Students

      These committee members are reaching out broadly across campus to gather input and insight to help establish a phased approach to reopening. In addition, they are working with colleagues across all U-M campuses, so there is a coordinated approach that makes the best use of resources and public health information that we can use as a guide for the UM-Flint campus. It will be an ongoing process that will happen methodically over a period of time with the well being of all as our highest priority.

      If you have any questions or ideas, please feel free to reach out to me or any of the committee members. We are also planning to host an online reopening Q&A session with members of the Reopening Steering Committee. Details regarding that online event will be sent soon.

      We appreciate your patience as we continue to collect information to make informed decisions about the eventual reopening of the campus. 

      Be well,

      Mike

      Michael J. Hague
      Vice Chancellor for Business and Finance
