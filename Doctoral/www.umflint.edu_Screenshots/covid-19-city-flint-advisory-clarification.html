      COVID-19: City of Flint Advisory Clarification

      Last Updated: Sunday, March 22nd, 2020 at 08:07 PM.
      • COVID-19
      • Campus Communications
      • FAQs
      • Students & Families
      • Faculty & Staff Resources
      • COVID-19 Related News

      March 22, 2020 • 7:55 p.m.

      Dear Campus Community,

       
      Today, Flint Mayor Sheldon Neeley issued a Shelter In Place Advisory for City of Flint residents. This advisory recommends voluntary residential sheltering in place.  
       
      For clarification, this advisory's recommendations are similar to the current operational practices at UM-Flint. Classes will continue in the alternative mode we switched to last week. University departments are allowing remote work when possible, and with supervisor's approval. Other academic and student services will remain open to students, faculty, and staff. 
       
       It is important to continue practicing social distancing, hand washing, and other recommendations from the Centers for Disease Control. If you are ill, please stay home and contact your healthcare provider. Read more about UM-Flint's COVID-19 response here. 
       
      We continue to monitor this evolving situation, and will inform the campus community of any changes. 
       
      Have a good week and good health,
       
      Deba Dutta
      Chancellor
