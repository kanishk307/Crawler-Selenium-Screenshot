CARES Act Student Emergency Relief Grants

The Coronavirus Aid, Relief, and Economic Security Act (CARES Act) is one of several acts passed by the US Congress and signed into law by President Donald J. Trump in the spring of 2020 for the purpose of providing relief to Americans and American businesses during the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic.  Institutions of higher education were supported by the Higher Education Emergency Relief Fund (HEERF) as part the CARES Act.

Students who were enrolled for the spring 2020 semester as of March 13, 2020, and based on U.S. Department of Education guidance, meet all of the following criteria can apply:

  • The student incurred expenses directly related to the disruption of campus operations due to Covid-19;
  • The student is Title IV eligible as determined by the 2019-20 Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA).
  • The student was NOT exclusively enrolled in distance education or online courses as of March 13, 2020.

Yes, graduate students may apply.

All students who meet eligibility requirements for the CARES Act Emergency Financial Aid Grant will have an application link sent to their Mercer email account on May 19, 2020. Students must submit their application by 5:00 p.m. on June 1, 2020, to be considered for funding.  Title IV eligible students that have not completed a 2019-20 FASFA must also have their FAFSA processed and received at Mercer by June 1, 2020, 5:00 p.m. deadline.

Eligible expenses are those expenses included in Cost of Attendance and include, but are not limited to the following expenses: food, housing, course materials, technology, health care, and child care.  Applicants are required to confirm that they incurred eligible expenses related to the disruption of campus operations due to COVID-19 by submitting a CARES Act Application.

Yes, you may apply without receipts. There is no documentation required.

Section 484 of the HEA states that Title IV eligible students must:

  • Be enrolled or accepted for enrollment in a degree or certificate program.
  • Not be enrolled in elementary or secondary school.
  • For currently enrolled students, be making satisfactory academic progress.
  • Not owe an overpayment on Title IV grants or loans.
  • Not be in default on a Title IV loan.
  • File “as part of the original financial aid application process” a certification that includes: a statement of educational purpose and student’s SSN.
  • Be a U.S. citizen or national, permanent resident, or other eligible noncitizen.
  • Have returned fraudulently obtained Title IV funds if convicted of or pled guilty or no contest to charges.
  • Not have fraudulently received Title IV loans in excess of annual or aggregate limits.
  • Have repaid Title IV loan amounts in excess of annual or aggregate limits if obtained inadvertently.
  • Have Selective Service registration verified.
  • Have Social Security Number verified.
  • Not have a federal or state conviction for drug possession or sale, with certain time limitations.

Yes, you may submit a CARES Act Application. However, you must be eligible for Title IV funding as determined by having completed and filed the 2019-20 FAFSA.  If you submit a CARES Act Application, the application will not be reviewed until your Title IV eligibility has been determined based on the 2019-20 FAFSA.

No, unfortunately, under Department of Education’s new guidance, undocumented students, Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) students, and international students are not eligible for CARES Act funding.

The CARES Act allocation will be divided between eligible undergraduate and graduate/professional students based on the percentage of enrollment at Mercer. Payments will be distributed to eligible undergraduate and graduate students based on need as determined by the 2019-20 FAFSA with an emphasis on providing additional support to the neediest populations. Funding for eligible students will range from $200 to $600.

The distribution of funds to eligible students will begin the week of June 1, 2020. If you meet the eligibility criteria and submitted your CARES Act Application, please contact Financial Planning if you have not received a CARES Act payment by June 15, 2020.

The CARES Act does provide some relief to students, borrowers and parents. Each student’s specific situation may be different. Additional information can be found at https://studentaid.gov/announcements-events/coronavirus.

Funds will be released through your student account based on your previously selected refund method.  We recommend you review your refund options on the Bursar’s website.

No, balances owed on student accounts will not be deducted from the CARES Act emergency grant funds.  Any balance on the account is still the responsibility of the student.

The University’s Financial Planning Office is always available to look at each student’s unique situation and evaluate all financial aid options. Please contact your Financial Planning Counselor for any special circumstances.

The Internal Revenue Services has issued the following guidance: Higher Education Emergency Relief Fund and Emergency Financial Aid Grants under the CARES Act.

No, CARES Act funding is not considered financial aid awarded to you and will not impact your current financial aid package.

COVID-19 Exposure, Health and Wellness

Coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) is a respiratory illness that can spread from person to person. The virus that causes COVID-19 is a novel coronavirus that was first identified during an investigation into an outbreak in Wuhan, China.

COVID-19 is a new disease, and the CDC is learning more about how the virus spreads. Please refer to the CDC website for the most current information.

Patients with COVID-19 have mild to severe respiratory illness with symptoms of fever, cough and shortness of breath. In severe cases, patients with COVID-19 have developed pneumonia in both lungs.

There is currently no vaccine to protect against COVID-19. The best way to prevent infection is to avoid being exposed to the virus that causes COVID-19.

The same measures that are urged to prevent the spread of any respiratory virus are increasingly important:

  • Avoid close contact with people who are sick.
  • Cover your mouth and nose with a tissue or your sleeve (not your hands) when coughing or sneezing.
  • Wash your hands often with soap and warm water for at least 20 seconds. Use an alcohol-based hand sanitizer that contains at least 60% alcohol if soap and water are not available.
  • Avoid touching your eyes, nose, and mouth.
  • Frequently clean and disinfect high touch surfaces with household cleaning spray or wipes.
  • Practice social distancing (avoid shaking hands, maintain a 6-foot distance from individuals who are symptomatic and consider using teleconferencing instead of face-to-face meetings).

Practice other good health habits, including getting plenty of sleep, being physically active, managing your stress, drinking plenty of fluids, and eating nutritious food.

If you are a student and develop a cough or shortness of breath accompanied by a fever, immediately call the Student Health Center phone line at (478) 301-7425. The line is monitored 24 hours a day, seven days a week. Employees should call their health care provider. It is critical that employees do not report to work while experiencing symptoms including fever, cough, shortness of breath, runny or stuffy nose, body aches, headache, and chills. Faculty, staff, and students can also access a coronavirus self-checker on the CDC’s website.

If you recently traveled to countries that are listed as a CDC Warning Level 3 Travel Health Notice for COVID-19, students should contact Mercer University Vice President for Student Affairs Dr. Doug Pearson by email at pearson_dr@mercer.edu or by phone at (478) 301-2685 and employees should contact Human Resources by phone at (478) 301-2786, as soon as possible. If you traveled internationally to other locations, it is recommended that you conduct a self-observation period and remain alert for subjective fever, cough, or difficulty breathing for 14 days as a precaution.

Faculty and staff who anticipate traveling abroad through Aug. 31, 2020, should register that travel online. While this registry is typically reserved for University-sponsored travel abroad, we are now asking that all international travel either for University business or for personal reasons be registered using this site.

  • Temperature checks at entry points to high-traffic areas

  • Signage and posters across all campuses with reminders about social distancing and healthy practices

Access and Accommodations

  • Students already receiving accommodations with The Access Office can log into Accommodate to request new accommodations.
  • Registered students are requested to communicate with their instructors about the accommodation and implementation in the online environment.
  • Students not yet receiving accommodations will need to complete the process to become approved for accommodations. Please go to https://access.mercer.edu/students/new/ and follow the procedures.

Please contact the Access and Accommodation Office. Office hours are 8:30 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. Staff can be reached at 478-301-2810 or by email at access@mercer.edu.

Access and accommodations can be implemented in the online environment. Canvas training can provide guidance on how to change settings to provide extended time on tests, exams, and quizzes. Many classroom accommodations will be managed by the student and in coordination with the Office Access and Accommodations if needed.

Accessible materials will need to be used in course development including text-based documents, description of images that are content related, videos with captioning/transcripts, and websites.

  • To check a website, use www.Wave.webaim.org.
  • Captioning: Visit EASI for programs that will assist in adding captions to your own videos.  For videos produced by others, be sure that there are captions or a transcript (double check the captions for accuracy).
  • You can produce transcripts of Zoom recordings. See this website for directions. Please use this feature to have a transcript with each of your videos.

Faculty intending to use Zoom for live meetings and are aware of a student with hearing impairment should contact Katie Johnson in the Access Office to discuss.

The Access Office is contacting individual faculty regarding specific student needs with tips and resources.

The Access Office has information on remote accommodation implementation as well as information on how to ensure your online content is accessible, including applying extra time for exams. Contact Katie Johnson for any questions or concerns at access@mercer.edu or 478-301-2810.

Faculty and Staff

Employees who have underlying health conditions as outlined in Governor Kemp’s Executive Order and who wish to submit a request to work remotely, or do a combination of work from home and their office, should submit the request by email to their supervisor and copy Candace Whaley in Human Resources (whaley_ce@mercer.edu). Each request will be reviewed by HR and the employee’s supervisor(s) for final approval by the appropriate Senior Vice President. Human Resources will notify the employee whether the request has been approved once it has been fully reviewed.

Employees who have minor children at home requiring adult supervision because their childcare facilities/camps are not yet operational may also request to work remotely. Employees should discuss their individual situations with their supervisors, who will consult with the Provost or relevant Senior Vice President to seek approval, as long as the employee can continue to effectively work remotely. If approved, employees will be permitted to work remotely through July 2, or until such time as their childcare facility/camp reopens, whichever is sooner.

Currently, remote work approvals will extend through Thursday, July 2nd. Employees approved to telework are reminded that all teleworking arrangements are subject to change in response to the evolving situation and essential work of the University. As such, some employees may be asked by their supervisor to come to campus as needed.

Employees who do not work their full schedule must take leave in accordance with the University Sick/Vacation Leave policy.

Employees who do not work their full schedule must take leave.  The prior authorization to use any form of accrued leave and the advancement of up to 2 weeks of sick leave for those with insufficient leave accruals ended as of Friday, April 17th.  Beginning Monday, April 20th, employees who need to take leave must do so in accordance with the University’s Sick/Vacation Leave policy.

Employees must communicate with their supervisor when they will need to be absent from work for qualifying reasons under the sick leave policy or request vacation leave in advance.  Also, employees should continue to use the appropriate leave code to track their leave usage on their timesheet or Bear Time report.
