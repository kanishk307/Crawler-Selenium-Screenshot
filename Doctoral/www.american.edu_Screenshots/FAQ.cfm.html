Frequently Asked Questions

The following Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) reflect American University’s current policies and resources. This page is designed to provide the AU community with up-to-date answers to help you navigate the university’s response to the evolving COVID-19 situation.

Don’t find your question here? If you have a question which is not answered below, please suggest a new question and we’ll do our best to update this page with the appropriate information.
Suggest a Question

General Questions

  • What preparations has AU taken to handle COVID-19?
  • What classes and events has the university canceled?
  • Where should I report if I receive a conﬁrmed positive test for COVID-19?
  • What should I do if I am concerned about being exposed?
  • What safety precautions is AU taking to clean and sanitize our community?
  • What teaching and research support is available from Bender Library?
  • I have books from Bender Library that I need to return, what should I do?
  • Are campus tours canceled?
  • Are Fitness Center facilities available for use?
  • What do I need to do to pause deductions for the fitness center?
  • Will I have access to buildings on campus?
  • Where can I get more information on COVID-19?
  • What are the key security issues I need to consider when using Zoom?

Fall 2020 General Questions

  • Will classes be held online or in person in the fall?
  • When will I know the format of my fall courses?
  • Will my section be online or in person, and how can I tell?
  • Do I have an option in how I participate if one option is preferable to me or not available?
  • Will there be changes to the Academic Calendar in terms of add/drop or refund deadlines?
  • What can I do if I can’t connect to Zoom, Blackboard or other platforms during class time?
  • I don’t have reliable access to internet and/or the university systems. What should I do?
  • I don’t want to take courses through Zoom. Can I be guaranteed that I can take courses for my program in person?
  • What if I really want to come back to campus for a section, but I’m unable to get off that waitlist? (especially as it relates to buying plane tickets, signing leases, etc.)
  • How can I meet with my academic advisor in the fall 2020 semester?
  • What if I have a question about my fall 2020 course?
  • Will there be trainings or workshops to prepare for online learning?
  • What if I’m not back on campus and in another time zone, will I be beholden to the assigned time of my course?
  • What if my section is scheduled to be in-person and I’m unable to come back to campus?
  • Why can’t online sections have unlimited seats?
  • When will the fall semester begin and end?
  • How will public spaces be cleaned and sanitized?
  • What is the status of campus in-person events/gatherings?
  • What is the protocol for visitors to campus?
  • Is the shuttle operating on a normal schedule?
  • What if a class is held in person, and I do not feel comfortable attending?
  • How will the university determine who can attend in-person classes?
  • How will classes be structured to ensure safety (physical distancing)?
  • What happens if the university has to go fully remote again in the event of a second wave of COVID-19?
  • Will there be a broader pass/fail option for fall classes?
  • Will there be changes to the add/drop period?
  • When do I have to make a decision for a temporary leave by?
  • Will I receive a discount for any classes held entirely online?
  • Will there be changes to Library operations?
  • What is Washington College of Law’s (WCL) plan for the fall semester?

Commencement

  • I’ve heard about plans for December in-person ceremonies for spring 2020 graduates?
  • What are the requirements for earning my degree, certificate, and/or honors?
  • When will spring 2020 graduates receive their diplomas?

Health and Safety

  • Will everyone be required to wear a face covering while on campus?
  • What is the policy on physical distancing while on campus?
  • Will the university do contact tracing and how? What is the protocol?
  • What is the university’s testing capacity? How frequently will students/community members be tested? 
  • Will my student health insurance cover testing for COVID-19? What if I’m a faculty or staff member?
  • How will policies on face coverings and physical distancing be enforced? Will there be consequences for students/staff/faculty who do not follow the protocol?
  • What is the university’s process in the event of a spike in COVID cases? What if our campus becomes a hot spot?
  • How is the university ensuring that contract employees follow the same health and safety precautions and requirements?
  • What will wellness services look like – physical as well as mental health?
  • How will the university handle students who travel to campus (do not live in the residence halls)? Will they be screened every time they enter?
  • If a student tests positive for COVID-19, will they be sent home? What health services are available on campus? What about a faculty or staff member?
  • Will the university be providing PPE to all students, or will students be expected to provide their own?
  • Will hand sanitizing stations be placed throughout campus?

  • If I need accommodations for online class, how can I arrange that?
  • What support is available?

  • Will financial aid be available to me for summer courses?

Campus and Student Life

Student Health

  • What if I am experiencing increased anxiety or stress related to concerns about family in one of the affected countries? Similarly, what if I notice a student, colleague, or other person who seems to be experiencing related difficulty?
  • I am on campus and may have come in contact with someone who has coronavirus and/or I feel as though I may have coronavirus, what should I do?
  • I am off campus, may I use my school insurance?

The Student Experience

  • What will be done to ensure a traditional campus experience as much as possible?
  • With limited activities on campus, how will AU build community?
  • What accommodations will be made for students in high-risk groups?

Housing and Residence Life

  • For students staying on campus, how should we address health concerns?
  • How is the university handling mail and packages?
  • Will there be students living in the residence halls?
  • Who will be eligible to live in the residence halls?
  • What if I already had an on-campus room reserved for fall 2020?
  • Will there be specific housing considerations for students with preexisting medical conditions that put them more at risk than the general population?
  • How will the residence halls promote safety?
  • Will there be changes in housing fees?
  • How can I return to campus to attend AU if I do not get selected for a residence hall? If that occurs, will AU assist students with off-campus housing?
  • What will be the protocol to ensure physical distancing in residence hall bathrooms and other common areas?
  • How will move-in be handled, and when will move-in days be assigned?
  • Will there be an isolation/quarantine building or area?
  • What happens if I live off-campus and test positive for COVID-19? Do I self-isolate at my local residence or in the campus isolation building?
  • What will on-campus housing for spring 2021 look like?
  • How will I get help with arranging housing in DC?
  • Can I still choose to come back and live on campus, even if none of my class sections are being offered in person?
  • If I am eligible for financial aid for on-campus housing, can I use it for off-campus housing?
  • I have more questions about residence life and on-campus housing?

Dining

  • What dining services will be available on campus?
  • What cleaning and safety measures will be put in place in the fall?
  • How will dining be set up to ensure physical distancing?
  • Will buffets continue to be available?
  • Will there be new meal plan structures and costs?

Campus Store

  • What are the safety measures at the AU Campus Store?
  • How can I obtain textbooks?
  • What happens if I drop my class or the professor changes books?
  • When can I pick up my Textbook order?
  • When will Rental Textbooks be due back for the Fall2020 semester?
  • Can I work at the AU Campus Store?

Athletics and Recreation

  • What was the University COVID-19 Health & Safety Task Force and Athletics COVID-19 Task Force process for determining the safe return of student-athletes, coaches, and staff to campus?
  • When will student-athletes, coaches, and staff return?
  • What guidance has been given by the NCAA, Patriot League, and other university affiliations?
  • When will competitions begin?
  • What COVID-19 prevention education will staff and students receive?
  • What are the plans for competitions, and how will the health of fans, players, and staff be addressed?
  • What are plans for intramural and club programming?
  • Will the fitness centers be open for use?

Transportation and Parking

  • Will the university shuttle continue to operate?
  • What parking restrictions are in effect on and off campus?

  • How can I find information about graduate financial aid?

International Students

International Students - Travel and Immigration

  • The US State Department suspended non-immigrant visa services in all countries. How will this affect me?
  • Can I stay in the United States with an expired F-1/J-1 visa?
  • As an incoming or current international student, could I pursue my education from abroad in fall 2020 semester or must come to the U.S.?
  • U.S. has travel restrictions for my country. Could I enter the U.S. through a third country?
  • As an international student, will I be required to quarantine upon entry to the U.S.?
  • If I have a valid visa, may I go back to my home country this summer and return to the U.S. by August 24 for the beginning of fall semester?
  • Could I return to the U.S. with the unexpired student visa in my passports?
  • The U.S. Department of State suspended non-immigrant visa services in all countries. How will this affect me?
  • How does the recent Presidential Proclamation suspending Chinese students and scholars’ entry to the U.S. affect me?
  • Can I stay in the United States with an expired F-1/J-1 visa?
  • If I am abroad and unable to enter the U.S. due to visa issues, what are my options for pursuing my education at AU in the fall semester?
  • What if I am unable to make it on time for the beginning of the semester due to limited airlines? Will AU allow me to enter late?

International Students - Enrollment Matters

  • I am outside the U.S. and there is a significant time difference between my location and AU. Will my classes only be available during the regularly scheduled time or will there be other options?
  • I am a student in a country that doesn’t support Google and Blackboard. Is there another way for me to access online resources through AU?
  • Will international students with full time enrollment in face-to-face classes be allowed to take more than one online class, since immigration regulations normally limit the amount of online instruction?
  • How does retroactive withdrawal affect international students?
  • How many online classes can I take this summer?
  • Do I have to be full-time this summer?
  • As a current F-1 or J-1 student, will I be able to take online classes in fall 2020 semester while inside or outside the U.S?
  • Will I have the option of taking more than one online class as part of my full-time enrollment requirement as an international student?
  • How can I maintain my immigration status in fall 2020 if I do not have full course of study left in my last semester?
  • If I cannot enter the U.S on time for fall 2020 classes, what are my enrollment options as a current or incoming student?
  • If I decide to take the fall semester off as a current student, what are my options?
  • Do I need to maintain full time enrollment while pursuing online education outside the US?
  • If I decide to remain at home for the fall, do I have the option to take courses at a university at home and would my credits transfer to AU?
  • I am facing financial challenges and seeking assistance from the university for the fall. Are there any scholarships for international students?

International Students - Jobs, Internships, CPT, and OPT

  • Can I do remote/telework inside or outside the U.S. to fulfil my internship requirements for credit internships?
  • If I am authorized for practical training; internship (CPT) or optional practical training (OPT) for fall 2020, can I work virtually to fulfil my employment requirement?
  • I am an international student with authorized off-campus employment for an internship or practical training. Is there any guidance from Department of Homeland Security for situations like this?
  • As a current student, do I need CPT authorization if I do an internship outside the US?
  • If I am graduating in August or December 2020, could I apply for post-completion optional practical training (OPT) from outside of the US?
  • How will ISSS assist international students who are graduating and are currently in the US with optional practical training (OPT) applications?
  • I applied for Post-completion Optional Practical Training (OPT) based on my Spring graduation. What happens if I cannot finish my degree requirements on time?
  • If I am authorized for practical training; internship (CPT) or optional practical training (OPT) for fall 2020, can I work virtually to fulfil my employment requirement?
