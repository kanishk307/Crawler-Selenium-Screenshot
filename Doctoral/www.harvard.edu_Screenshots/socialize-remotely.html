Although universal social distancing is imperative to slowing the pandemic and protecting vulnerable people, these measures can take an emotional and psychological toll on all of us. It’s important to create a routine, build downtime into your schedule, take breaks from news and social media, and practice good self-care.

The Socialize Remotely website, created by the Office of the Vice Provost for Advances in Learning (VPAL) and Harvard University Information Technology (HUIT), serves as a cross-School connector for virtual events, a promoter of School specific content, and a social outlet. Log in with your HarvardKey to browse events and add your own.

Additionally, the following resources and activities can help you fulfill our human need for social interaction while still maintaining critical physical distancing measures.

Art, Movies, Music, & Culture
  • Stream free movies from the online Harvard film archive.
  • Sign up for free online workshops through GSAS Engage; classes range from photography and knitting to live music and exercise. Open to all with a HarvardKey.
  • Host a movie night with friends and family from afar by installing the Netflix Party Chrome browser extension to enable synchronous playback and group chat.
  • Stream free kids' television shows through Amazon Prime.
  • Explore U.S. National Parks with Google’s immersive, virtual tours.
  • Tour hundreds of museums and exhibits from around the world with Google. Download the Google Arts & Culture app to experience tours in virtual reality.
  • Listen to Boston Symphony Orchestra performances and some of the best touring jazz musicians with Live From Scullers.
  • Watch and listen to live virtual concerts from around the world.
  • Enjoy world-class theater performances online from the National Theatre.
  • Revisit favorite recipes, try a new one, and watch free online cooking classes from Milk Street Cooking School through April 30.
  • Learn a new language with Duolingo.
  • Travel through time with the Internet Archive to access millions of free books, movies, music, and more.
Education
  • Explore hundreds of free online courses from Harvardx.
  • Harvard Graduate School of Education's Usable Knowledge Series offers guidance for cultivating empathy, building resilience, and supporting young people.
  • The National Association of School Psychologists offers advice for how to talk to children about coronavirus.
  • The Boston Children’s Museum staff manages Beyond the Chalkboard, which provides hundreds of free activities for math, science, literacy, health, culture, and art. They're also hosting a Make-A-Thon challenge for the month of April.
  • MOS at Home includes family STEM activities, live streams, podcasts, and more from the Museum of Science.
  • The Field Museum's Brain Scoop videos explore natural history with the museum's Chief Curiosity Officer.
  • PBS Learning Media provides free virtual lessons, activities, and educational materials for grades pre-K through high school.
  • Scholastic learn at home offers daily projects to keep kids in Pre-K through grade nine reading, thinking, and growing.
  • Skype a Scientist live sessions help children stay excited about learning — from ants and dinosaurs to the deep sea and the moon.
  • Sesame Street has curated content to help care for one another during the COVID-19 health crisis.
  • The Kennedy Center artist-in-residence Mo Willems is offering daily lunch doodles.
  • The National Baseball Hall of Fame educational resources include lesson plans and activities to teach science, math, social studies, the arts, and character education through baseball.
  • Explore this running list of educational resources and companies offering free subscriptions due to school closings.
Exercise & Games
  • Take walks, go for a run, play games that don’t involve physical contact, and enjoy being outside — just remember to follow local regulations for stay-at-home-orders and maintain a safe social distance of 6 feet from people who aren't part of your household if you venture outdoors.
  • For the young and the young-at-heart, check out the Boston Children Museum's 100 ways to play.
  • Participate in a live virtual workout with Harvard Recreation group.
Mindfulness & Spirituality
  • Mindfulness at Work provides free online meditation classes on a variety of topics for benefits-eligible faculty and staff.
  • Harvard's Center for Wellness and Health Promotion offers virtual yoga, meditation, and movement classes as well as a virtual relaxation room with guided relaxation and meditation recordings.
  • Harvard Divinity School is sharing spiritual resources that include weekday contemplative gatherings, tips for caring for self and others, and a growing list of reflections.
  • Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health offers a weekly interactive forum on Wednesdays about mental health issues related to the outbreak and its impact.
  • The Headspace app provides free meditations, sleep, and movement exercises.
  • The Ten Percent Coronavirus Sanity Guide includes free meditations, podcasts, blog posts, and talks.
Social Media
  • Regularly call, text, or check-in with friends and family.
  • Schedule virtual meals, coffee breaks, or game nights with family & friends over FaceTime, Google Duo, and other video calling apps.
  • Follow the Office for the Arts at Harvard and Outings & Innings Twitter feeds for online museum resources, virtual concerts, educator videos, and more.
  • Join the Be a Hero. #StayHome social media movement launched by Ariadne Labs. Everyone is invited to unite as a community and share what they’re doing to stay connected. Download free and fun graphics that you can share on social media to inspire others.
