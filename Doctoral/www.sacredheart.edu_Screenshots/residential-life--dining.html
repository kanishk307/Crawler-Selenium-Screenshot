Residential Life & Dining

Residential Halls

  • Per the state’s reopening guidance, students sharing a room or suite will be considered a “family unit” and will not be required to wear a mask while together within the room.
  • Masks will be required in all common areas such as hallways, lobbies, bathrooms and study/lounge areas.
  • Common areas will be cleaned frequently and thoroughly, and there may be plastic partitions in between sinks in bathrooms.
  • There may be some restrictions regarding guests in the residence halls.

Move-In Days

  • Early dropoff: Students who want to drop off their belongings into their rooms prior to move-in days will be able to do so by appointment from August 8-18. Information will be coming from residential life about how to make an appointment. This will be for drop-off only. No unpacking should take place until the official move-in days.
  • Freshman Move-in will take place August 25-27. Day and time will be determined by room assignments and will be sent out to freshmen in late July.
  • Upperclass Move-In will be August 28-30 and also based on room assignment. Look for information in late July.
  • Travel Planning: Students who need to make reservations on public transportation, such as planes or trains should choose a date in the correct time frame above and then should notify residential life of the date of arrival on campus. You can make that notification by emailing reslife@sacredheart.edu. Be sure to educate yourself on any quarantining regulations that may be in place here at the time of your arrival.

For more information on residential life and move-in days, visit the residential life website.

Dining Services

  • SHU Dining is expanding its list of items available for take-out on all menus, incorporating mobile-ordering capability and expanding hours to accommodate reduced crowd sizes.
  • Seating areas will be arranged to limit the number of people gathering at once. 
  • The University is adjusting meal plans to reflect the reduction in the number of days students will be able to use their fall plan. The adjustment, which varies by plan, will be reflected in your June billing statement.
  • The eliminated Columbus Day four-day weekend and an earlier move-in date will in part offset reduced meals after Thanksgiving, and we have made a prorated reduction to freshman and sophomore plans to reflect the net number of days that students will not be on campus in preparation for any possible virus resurgence.
  • The Red & White meal plans for upper-class students, which are smaller declining balance plans, will have increased fall-to-spring carryover allowances.
