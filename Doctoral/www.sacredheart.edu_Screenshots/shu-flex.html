SHU-Flex

Sacred Heart is planning to begin fall semester classes as planned on Monday, August 31. However, as part of a plan that we are calling SHU-Flex, we are condensing the undergraduate academic calendar for safety reasons.

Introducing SHU-Flex

  • Academic calendars will remain unchanged.
  • All courses can be delivered online and on-ground simultaneously. 
  • SHU is eliminating the Columbus Day weekend break, but plans to conclude in-person classes before Thanksgiving break. The plan ensures that students receive the same number of instructional hours as always, with no changes to their already assigned course schedules, but will mitigate risk by limiting trips back and forth to campus that may require additional testing and possible self-isolation. Since most of our graduate students are nonresidents and would present less risk, the graduate calendars will not change, although graduate programs are encouraged to complete on-ground activities before Thanksgiving when possible. 
  • In-person instruction will end on 11/24/2020, with very rare exceptions, the rest of the semester will be delivered virtually.
  • To plan for all contingencies, SHU-Flex ensures that the University’s faculty will be prepared to deliver course material both face-to-face and virtually to allow for flexibility for students in various circumstances as well as social distancing requirements. It also enables them to seamlessly switch to alternative modes of teaching at a moment’s notice should a spike in the virus require a change in plans.
  • Masks will be worn everywhere on campus except in students’ rooms, including classes, common areas and traveling between buildings.
  • Sanitizing wipes will be available outside of classrooms. Students and faculty must sanitize their area upon entering and leaving class.
  • Classes will be held in rooms at 50% capacity to allow for social distancing.
  • Gloves, masks and face shields will be provided to students who need to come in contact with others for their clinical or lab experience.
  • Some internship positions will be able to transition online, depending on the position and the placement. Others will not be available this fall.
  • Any student who refuses to wear a mask in class will be directed to leave and will receive an unexcused absence.

For Freshmen, First-Year & Transfer Students

We recommend that all freshman and transfer students bring a laptop computer to allow for participation in and outside of the classroom environment. For maximum success, your device should be capable of supporting these tools.

The laptop you use should have a baseline technical requirement of:

  • Windows 10 or MacOS 10.15 
  • 256 SSD GB hard drive 
  • 8gb of ram
  • i5 core processor 
  • 13-inch display 
  • wireless network connectivity 
  • microphone and camera  

We also recommended that you have headphones to improve your online web conferencing interactions.

Course work may also utilize Google and Adobe tools, so a scanner app (e.g. Genius Scan) is also recommended. In addition, you should also have multiple internet browsers installed on your computer (Firefox, Chrome for PC users or Safari/Chrome for Mac users). Additional technology resources may be added at the discretion of the course instructor.

Students who use Macs are responsible for ensuring that they are using a newer operating system and that the course instructor can view all submitted assignment files on a PC computer. We do not recommend that you use phones, iPads, Chromebooks, readers or similar tablets as a primary device for completing course assignments. 

If you need access to a computer, you may use any of the following spaces when classes are not in session, as long as you follow our risk-mitigation measures (mask, social distancing, etc.): HC111, W122, W239, RH-art lab, E1006, E1039, E1129, cyberlab, W109, W114, W118, W133, W141, W146, W208, W213, W217 and W213.  

If you have additional questions related to computer options, please refer to the laptop acquisition information on the website for a detailed description of laptop options supported by our Factory team. 

You can also contact our support team at TechSupport@sacredheart.edu. For any questions or general information, contact InfoTech@sacredheart.edu.

Internet Connections

Internet connections vary across the U.S. and can affect video calls and conferencing. Per FCC regulations, a high-speed broadband connection is determined to have download speeds of 25 Mbps. The higher the number, the better. You can check your speed by visiting Fast.com. 

Printing

When you are on campus, you may print documents in room HC113. This room is available to students 24/7. You may also print/scan and copy at any of the 25 existing multifunction printers around campus.
