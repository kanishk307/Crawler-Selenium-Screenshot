Overview/General

  • Why is Belmont Making These Schedule Adjustments?

    • The rapid spread and still-unknown threat of the Coronavirus and COVID-19 find us in the middle of uncertain and extraordinary times. Having considered all available information and in following guidance from the Centers for Disease Control and local/state health department officials, Belmont is electing to act with an abundance of caution with three main goals driving our decisions:

    • Caring for the health of our students, faculty and staff
    • Preventing the virus from infiltrating our campus
    • Contributing to public health efforts to stem the spread of this virus throughout our community

    In our view, these extraordinary circumstances call for extraordinary (and, at least at Belmont, unprecedented) actions. Across the country announcements are being made of closures, cancellations, postponements, and other disruptions to plans and life. Each of these disruptions are bound to cause an array of frustrations, inconveniences and sacrifices, all in the name of helping to curb this public health crisis. We are fully aware that Belmont’s own changes will have similar effect on our students and their families; we ask your patience and understanding as we venture through this together.

    Please take note: Given the evolving nature of this COVID-19 situation, Belmont has made the decision to move all courses online for the remainder of the Spring and Summer 2020 terms. We recognize this raises many questions, and University administrators are working diligently to manage all the details this transition requires. We ask for your patience and understanding. More information will be communicated soon and please check this site often for updates as well to Frequently Asked Questions.

    What Changes are Being Made to the Spring Schedule?

    • Belmont administrators have been closely studying the collective efforts being made to flatten the curve of coronavirus cases across the United States. Unfortunately, it has become clear that those efforts are not working quickly enough and may not start taking significant effect for several more weeks. Given the potential for exposure and CDC recommendations for social distancing, Belmont has made the following decisions:

    • Belmont will be moving to a remote learning model for all students through the conclusion of the summer term, including final exams.
    • Commencement ceremonies currently scheduled for May 2 will be rescheduled for August 7. All graduating seniors will be invited to participate in what will be a special ceremony for our Spring 2020 graduating class on that date.  Graduates will not be required to attend the August commencement event, but we promise to make it a special celebration for you and your family. Regardless, your official graduation date will not be delayed, and your diplomas will be delivered. More details will be made available soon.
    • All in-person, organized group activities on campus through at least May 2 are canceled. This includes the Best of the Best Showcase, President’s Concert, visual or performing arts events, O’More Fashion Show, athletic competitions, Scholarship and Awards Day, Be Belmont Day and any other events that require large gatherings of people. In addition, summer courses and summer orientation sessions will be conducted virtually, and all summer campus (including athletic, academic and music camps) will be cancelled.
    • In order to catch up on lost class time, Easter Break will be canceled, and remote instruction will continue on April 9, 10 and 13. While we understand the potential impact to some in our community, given the circumstances, it was deemed necessary to cancel Easter Break to add those three days (April 9-10 and 13) back to the academic calendar. We ask that all students make every effort possible to participate as needed in their remote classes on those days despite the frustrations and inconveniences that come with this schedule change. Please know that we are asking the same of our faculty and staff. If your specific circumstances are extraordinary in nature, please work with your professors to determine if accommodations can be granted.

    • All international and domestic Maymester study abroad programs and May mission trips as well as summer study abroad/study away programs and mission trips have already been canceled, and impacted students and faculty have been notified.

  • New

    How is Belmont instituting the Coronavirus Aid, Relief and Economic Security Act?

    • On April 9, 2020, the Department of Education (DOE) issued a letter to all University Presidents announcing plans to distribute funds to higher education institutions as part of the Federal CARES (Coronavirus Aid, Relief and Economic Security) Act. A portion of these funds are earmarked to issue emergency aid grants directly to students to offset financial burdens brought on by the COVID-19 pandemic. The amount of funds received by each institution was calculated based on the percentage of Pell-eligible students enrolled.

    DOE guidelines for distributing these emergency grants are clear in asking each institution to place priority on students with the greatest need. Using the Expected Family Contribution (EFC, as captured by the FAFSA) as a means to determine qualifying students, Belmont will be distributing $1,000 emergency grants to more than 2,000 students. Students meeting this criteria have already been notified by Student Financial Services that they will receive the emergency aid, and the grants will be issued directly to those students as soon as the DOE delivers the funds to Belmont.

    While only some of our students qualified to receive this emergency aid, every student is important to Belmont. We empathize with the individual challenges and setbacks being lived out across our entire student body. To any students who did not fall under the need criteria as outlined above but are experiencing significant financial hardship related to the COVID-19 crisis, please contact Student Financial Services to discuss your circumstances and see if we can help. This dedicated team can be reached at 615.460.6403, or at caresgrants@belmont.edu. We ask for your patience as this team works to respond to and help as many students as possible.

  • New

    Will Belmont implement a Pass/Fail option for Spring Semester courses?

    • The reality of the coronavirus epidemic continues to significantly impact our lives, including how faculty approach teaching and supporting students as well as how students have had adapt to new learning methods while also coping the personal impact.

    While up until this time Belmont has not modified its approach to grading for the semester, please know it is not due to lack of awareness of or empathy for these realities. Rather, it is because we have been considering solutions that take all relevant factors into account. A move to pass/fail, while being adopted widely among other Universities, is not necessarily the best option for every student. Such a move also affects things like how other colleges/universities view your transcripts (transfer credit, graduate/professional school admissions, etc.), grade point average calculations (which could impact financial aid, graduate/professional school admission, employment opportunities, etc.), academic progress, professional certifications,  etc. 

    It is for these reasons that Belmont is implementing an approach that provides students the option to elect a pass/fail approach for a portion of their courses. Details are as follows:

    • Each student is allowed to select up to seven (7) credit hours as Pass/Fail AFTER grades have been posted for the spring 2020 term.
    • This allows students to pursue their best graded outcome to the benefit of their overall academic performance, while also providing an option for those courses where the graded outcome was lower than expected.
    • This is a one-time only exception to the University’s standard grading policies resulting from the aforementioned circumstances.

    Additional information to assist students in deciding whether/how to exercise the pass/fail option can be found here.

Campus Services

  • How do I sell back my textbooks I no longer need as the semester wraps up?

    • Buyback will be conducted online only this semester.  Below are the instructions to initiate your buyback quote with MBS:

    1.  Access your Online Bookstore •  https://bncvirtual.com/belmont • Go to Menu -> Sell Your Textbooks. • Log in using the same email address you used to purchase your books to review your eligibility for Guaranteed Buyback* and Customer Loyalty.  When you purchased your books in your Online Bookstore, you may have bought course materials with Guaranteed Buyback value. Make sure you include these when it’s time to sell your books back!
    2.  Review your books or enter ISBNs • Books purchased through the online bookstore are listed so you can choose which books you intend to sell back. • If you would like to add additional titles, simply enter the ISBN for each book you wish to sell back.
    3.  Verify your list
    4.  Click “Submit Your Buyback” • Your quote will not be processed until you click this button. • A finalized quote will be emailed to you. • Your buyback quote is valid for 30 days after creation.
    5.  Confirm and update your account • Ensure your delivery address is correct. • Verify your payment method(s).  **Be sure you add the address you are living at if requesting a check when you are not at Belmont to ensure the check goes to the correct place.  If you don't fill any address, it will default to send the check to Belmont, which could complicate you receiving your buyback check in a timely manner.
    6.  Print the quote from your email • Include a copy of your quote with the shipment of books you wish to sell back. (One copy of your quote per six books shipped)
    7.  Print the free shipping label.
    8.  Send your books • We will send you an email when we receive the books, check them in and mail your check or credit your PayPal account. Please allow 2-4 weeks to receive the funds.
    9.  If you have any additional questions, contact the 24/7 Customer Contact Center at 1.800.325.3252 or email BruinBooksHelp@bncservices.com.

  • How do I return my rental books I rented through Bruin Books?
    1. Visit the Bruin Books portal at https://bncvirtual.com/belmont
    2. Click Log in to Your Account.
    3. Use the email address you used to order your books for this semester.  If you have forgotten your password, click forgot password? to reset.
    4. Once you are logged in, you will see Your Rentals.  Below that you'll see a link, Rental Returns.  
    5. Click the link and you will see all the books you have rented for this semester.  Select them to return and click return Selected Books.
    6. Proceed to print the return label and shipping label as you finalize the rental return.

  • If I have travelled internationally, what should I do?

    • Please keep in mind that Belmont is complying with all federal, state and CDC standards regarding transmission prevention. U.S. citizens are advised to avoid all international travel, and citizens abroad were told to arrange an immediate return to the United States. If you are returning from countries with a CDC Level 3 Travel Health Notice please be prepared to follow CDC guidelines for transmission prevention upon your return.

    Belmont faculty, staff and students who have been travelling in any of these Level 3 countries over spring break are asked to please notify us at healthinfo@belmont.edu and to fill out this short surveyto record the details of travel.
