CARES Act Disclosures


  • New

    What will happen to my summer camp at Belmont?

    • Due to COVID-19 safety precautions, Belmont University has made the difficult decision to cancel many summer activities previously scheduled for campus for the safety and health of all involved. This includes Belmont athletics, academic and music camps as well as outside organizations like M-Fuge who use our campus to conduct their camps. We look forward to hosting these events again in the future.

  • Updated

    Will our campus visit be canceled?

    • In response to the COVID-19 crisis and out of an abundance of caution and care, effective Monday, March 23 Belmont University is suspending all IN-PERSON admissions activities, including daily campus visits and Be Belmont Day, through at least May 31. If you were registered for a Daily Campus Visit between now and May 31, we encourage you to reschedule here for a later date. We are eager to host you and show you all Belmont University has to offer! If you have any questions, please contact the Admissions office, which remains open, at 615-460-6785 or admissions@belmont.edu. 

  • Updated

    What will happen with the April 4 Be Belmont Day for admitted students?

    • In response to the COVID-19 crisis and out of an abundance of caution and care, Belmont University transitioned Be Belmont Day from an in person to a virtual experience. Recordings of those sessions can now be found on the Be Belmont Day website. If you have any questions, please contact the Admissions office, which remains open, at 615-460-6785 or admissions@belmont.edu.

  • We have reserved campus space for an outside event. Can we still hold it on campus?

    • Please contact Belmont’s Event Services team at 615-460-8500 for information on any reservations for events to be held on campus.

  • Updated

    Is Belmont’s library open?

    • Due to Nashville Mayor John Cooper’s March 22 “Safer at Home” order, Belmont is limiting the number of people on campus to essential services only. The Lila D. Bunch Library is closed until further notice.

     

  • Are the Gallery of Iconic Guitars and Hart Studio Museum open for visitors?

    • Due to the ongoing coronavirus pandemic and our desire to reduce the spread of COVID-19, the Gallery of Iconic Guitars and the Frederick Hart Studio Museum are currently closed until further notice.
