As faculty prepare to begin offering courses remotely, Belmont's Student Life team is also working hard to develop virtual outlets that will provide students with additional resources to supplement their academic experience. The menu highlights a number of way students can “plug in” to campus life experiences from afar as well as get support to benefit their wellness, career development, spiritual life and more. 

Academic Resources

  • Advising and Course Questions

    • Your faculty are working diligently to deliver the same quality courses in a new format. They are available to you for class questions and  advising appointments for Fall 2020 course registration. Contact them directly by phone or email using the Campus Directory or via Blackboard.

  • Updated

    Bunch Library

    • Due to Nashville Mayor John Cooper’s March 22 “Safer at Home” order, Belmont is limiting the number of people on campus to essential services.

    Belmont’s Lila D. Bunch Library is now closed to all in-person services as is the Gallery of Iconic Guitars and the Frederick Hart Studio Museum. Students, faculty and staff may still access the library’s online resources and request assistance through our chat service: https://belmont.libanswers.com/ or through our email reference@belmont.edu. To speak to a library staff member, please call 615-460-6782 between the hours of 8:00 am – 4:30 pm Monday through Friday.

    DUE DATES FOR ALL BOOKS CURRENTLY CHECKED OUT FROM BUNCH LIBRARY HAVE AUTOMATICALLY EXTENDED TO AUGUST 3

  • GPS Office

    • The Growth & Purpose for Students, or GPS team, is available to provide assistance to students in need of direction and discernment, academic planning and overall academic success. Email them at gps@belmont.edu or call 615-460-5702 with questions, and GPS staff will be happy to arrange a Blackboard Collaborate session or phone appointment.

  • Learning Centers

    • The Belmont Learning Centers (the Writing Center, Language Learning Center and Math/Science Center) have moved their services online. Starting March 23, students can access virtual tutoring assistance in math, physics, biology, chemistry, programming, writing and languages. On the Learning Center website simply choose the appropriate center, select a tutor’s name with the appropriate shift time and join the Collaborate room. The Learning Centers also offer study skill refreshers, text book breakdowns and learning and note taking guides.

Campus Life Resources

  • BOLD

    • The Belmont Office of Leadership Development (BOLD) offers “The B.E.A.T- Belmont Ethics and Accountability Training” on Blackboard for students to continue pursuing their co-curricular certificate in leadership while they are away from campus. Completion of this self-paced leadership training program is required by Friday, April 24, 2020. If a student wishes to sign up for the program, they may register here. Students may also continue to earn BOLD Service/ Service Learning from home. 

  • Career and Professional Development

    • Career and Professional Development staff are available for phone and video conference appointments or can advise you via email as you search for internship and job possibilities. They also offer students a number of online tools to assist in the exploration of majors and future careers. Schedule a phone or virtual career coaching appointment and check out resources in Handshake. Learn more on their website or contact the team at 615.460.6490 o careerdevelopment@belmont.edu.

  • Counseling Services

    • Your wellness remains our top priority, and we know this health crisis only adds more anxiety to all of our lives. For any student who needs some extra assistance at this time, our Counseling Services team has a number of options for you. All students have free and confidential access to TAO (Therapy Assistance Online) 24/7. Counseling Services is also able to provide videoconferencing therapy sessions to students currently residing in the state of Tennessee. If you are interested in more information about these services, please contact the Counseling Office at 615-460-6856 or counseling@belmont.edu. Counseling Services has also updated their website to provide additional links to supportive resources during this time of higher stress.

  • Student Engagement

    • The Student Engagement office is working with University clubs and organizations to hold virtual leadership meetings and will work with student org leaders to encourage ways to connect with members while students are off campus. Contact them at soa@belmont.edu or 615-460-6407 with questions. Also, follow Student Engagement on Instagram @BelmontToday to learn about opportunities for students to connect with the Belmont community during this season of transition.

  • Residence Life

    • Residence Directors are on site and, along with Campus Security, are monitoring our residence halls and parking garages to ensure your belongings and vehicles are safe. In addition, our entire Res Life team is assisting students who need to retrieve necessary belongings to aid in their time away from campus. 

    As a reminder, to minimize stress on our families, the deadline for being moved completely out of residence halls is not until May 31. Residence Life has emailed all residential students with details of a safe and orderly process for scheduling move out. Additional details can be found on Res Life’s FAQ page.

  • University Ministries

    • University Ministries will also be offering one-on-one pastoral care conversations online or by telephone with students who want them. Just complete the online request form or contact the office at 615-460-6419 or university.ministries@belmont.edu. UM will also be posting the Lenten Devotional Guide on their blog and will offer online Daily Meditations/Prayer via Instagram Live beginning Monday, March 23 at 10 a.m.

  • Wellness Content

    • In partnership, Counseling Services, Fitness and Recreation and University Ministries will be hosting “Wellness While We Wait,” wellness-related content that will be posted to the Belmont YouTube channel  and promoted on Instagram accounts. Follow the Instagram accounts of FitRec, UM and Counseling Services to learn more details. FitRec will also be working on promoting additional virtual fitness opportunities as all on campus fitness facilities are now closed to aid in prevention measures.

Auxiliary Resources

  • Updated

    The Belmont Store

    • Due to Nashville Mayor John Cooper’s March 22 “Safer at Home” order, Belmont is limiting the number of people on campus to essential services. The Belmont Store retail location is closed until further notice, though online shopping is still available 24/7. Visit thebelmontstore.com for additional information.

  • Updated

    Pharmacy

    • Due to Nashville Mayor John Cooper’s March 22 “Safer at Home” order, Belmont is limiting the number of people on campus to essential services. Belmont’s campus Pharmacy is an essential service so it will continue to operate but under reduced hours. Individuals may pick up prescriptions as needed from 10 a.m. – 2 p.m. Monday thru Friday. For questions or to order refills, call the Pharmacy directly at 615-460-6040.

  • The UPS Store & Campus Mail

    • The UPS Store at Belmont is open and will hold your packages at no charge for up to 8 weeks (they can also forward packages to your home address as needed, though additional postage may be required—to begin the process, simply complete this mail forwarding authorization form). The UPS Store is open Monday through Friday from 8 a.m.-7 p.m. and Saturdays from 9 a.m.-5 p.m. They can be reached at store6797@theupsstore.com or 615-460-6636.
