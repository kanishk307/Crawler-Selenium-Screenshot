CARES Act Disclosures


  • How do I move my class online?

    • The campus utilizes Blackboard (Bb) as our primary vehicle for the online delivery of course materials, etc., and all faculty should have access to Bb already. If you do not have access to Bb, or if you need assistance with Bb, please contact our instructional technology team, Geoff Price (Geoff.Price@belmont.edu) or Chris Rains (Chris.Rains@Belmont.edu), directly. Belmont also has a campus license for a lecture capture product, Panopto, as well which you may be interested in using to deliver online content. Again, Geoff and Chris would be happy to help if you have questions.

  • What does this new schedule mean for academic advising?

    • Academic advising must proceed. Please consider using all tools available and at your disposal – DegreeWorks planner, email, phone, text, video conferencing, Blackboard collaborate, etc to reach out to your advisees.

  • Will Belmont be holding its May 2 commencement ceremonies as planned?
    • No, commencement ceremonies previously scheduled for May 2 will be rescheduled for August 7. All graduating seniors will be invited to participate in what will be a special ceremony for our Spring 2020 graduating class on that date. More details will be made available soon.

  • How should I talk to my students about their clinicals and internships?

    • When possible, these types of activities should continue. Please work with your students who participate in these types of activities to help them work through the necessary steps to continue to make progress, if possible. Encourage students to ask their supervisor if they could work remotely. Think and get creative to look for other opportunities and options to help your students.

  • Which employees need to report to campus?

    • Due to Nashville Mayor John Cooper’s March 22 “Safer at Home” order, Belmont is limiting the number of people on campus to essential services. Our goal will be to minimize as much as possible the number of employees who must leave their homes to perform job duties. Your safety and wellbeing are important, and we do not take these decisions lightly. Employees will be contacted by their Senior Leader and/or supervisors to determine how essential services will be covered and remote work plans.
     
    While Belmont does not meet the standards of an "essential business" according to this order, we do still have thousands of current and prospective students and families counting on us to be fully engaged during this period. Our work-from-home approach will allow for the continuation of operations, even though we will be taking even more drastic steps in reducing the number of employees who will be on campus. 

    All employees are asked to be diligent and committed in avoiding behaviors that promote contracting and spreading the COVID-19 virus. Avoid non-essential gatherings and follow all social distancing best practices.

  • Will Belmont still be closed for Good Friday since Easter Break is canceled?

    • To accommodate lost class time due to the extension of Spring Break, the planned Good Friday holiday will be canceled. Belmont has now moved to a remote learning model for all students through the conclusion of the spring semester, but online sessions will still be conducted April 9-10 and 13. Employees should work with their supervisors to determine staffing and service needs. Employees who work on Good Friday may take the holiday at a future date.

  • What should managers keep in mind during the spring schedule changes?
    • Because the campus will now be open on Good Friday, April 10, ensure you have adequate staffing to meet service needs.  Employees who work on Good Friday may take the holiday at a future date.
    • Plan now for the possibility of disruption to Belmont’s day-to-day operations.
    • Review your Business Continuity Plan for essential operations during an emergency situation and minimize disruption of critical activities.
    • Be flexible and work with your staff members as they work from home.
    • Follow travel guidelines for yourself or your faculty and staff released previously and posted on our website. 

  • What if I need to continue working on campus?

    • Due to Nashville Mayor John Cooper’s March 22 “Safer at Home” order, Belmont is limiting the number of people on campus to essential services. Our goal will be to minimize as much as possible the number of employees who must leave their homes to perform job duties. Your safety and wellbeing are important, and we do not take these decisions lightly. Employees will be contacted by their Senior Leader and/or supervisors to determine how essential services will be covered and remote work plans.

    Employees who will be performing essential duties on campus are asked to please follow social distancing and good health practices:

    • Avoid hand shaking or any personal contact.
    • Keep your distance from others – 6 feet is the standard guideline.
    • Avoid group meetings unless absolutely necessary, in which case be sure to spread out and maintain that 6 foot distance. Your manager can advise on available tools to connect groups of people virtually.
    • Keep those hands clean! Aside from washing, hand sanitizer is available across campus. Tell your supervisor if you are not sufficiently stocked in your area.
    • Know that members of our valued housekeeping crew are taking extra steps to clean hard surfaces according to CDC guidelines, and using CDC approved cleaning products.

  • I’m feeling sick. What should I do?

    • Please communicate openly and actively with your supervisor about your health and the health of those around you – even if you are working from home. If you are feeling ill or determine that you have been around someone with a positive or suspected confirmed case of COVID-19, notify your supervisor and let us know by emailing healthinfo@belmont.edu. Of course, if you are ill please call your doctor.

    Employees who participate in Belmont’s health plan may access the following via BCBST member site:

    • 24/7 Nursechat at 800-818-8581
    • Extensive health library with information on staying healthy

You may also get in touch with Rev. Todd Lake (todd.lake@belmont.edu; 615.460.6628) or Rev. Heather Daugherty (heather.daugherty@belmont.edu; 615.460.6419) for prayer and pastoral support.

  • What practices are being implemented to keep employees safe on campus?

    • Due to Nashville Mayor John Cooper’s March 22 “Safer at Home” order, Belmont is limiting the number of people on campus to essential services, so many offices and facilities will be impacted. Our goal will be to minimize as much as possible the number of employees who must leave their homes to perform job duties. Your safety and wellbeing are important, and we do not take these decisions lightly. Employees will be contacted by their Senior Leader and/or supervisors to determine how essential services will be covered and remote work plans.

    For those employees who do need to continue their on campus work, the University has purchased large quantities of hand sanitizer that is being placed at areas across campus. Belmont is also following CDC cleaning protocol with CDC-approved disinfectant and encouraging the following practices:

    • Practice social distancing—eliminate face-to-face meetings and opt for email, phone or web-based options instead.
    • Wash your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds. If soap and water are not available, use an alcohol-based hand sanitizer.
    • Avoid touching your eyes, nose, or mouth with unwashed hands.
    • Avoid close contact with people who are sick.
    • Avoid hand shaking, hugging, fist bumps and sharing cups or eating utensils.
    • Cover your mouth and nose with a tissue before coughing or sneezing. Then immediately discard the tissue in the trash and disinfect your hands.
    • Clean and disinfect frequently touched objects and surfaces.

  • I’m working from home. What do I need to know about cybersecurity?

    • Cybersecurity controls are vital to employees’ ability to work from home safely and securely. Steps will need to be taken before accessing campus network resources from off site, and compliance in a number of areas will be required. Please remain vigilant for scams especially those related to COVID-19.  Criminals are using many different types of malware to compromise systems and steal data.  One thing that is common to many of these attacks is that they are initiated using social engineering.  Below are some common social engineering attack methods that you should be looking out for:

    • Phishing: A fraudulent attempt to steal your personal information, get you to click on a link, or open an attachment in an email. To protect yourself from phishing, avoid clicking on links or opening attachments in emails from people you don’t know even if they appear to be from a government official or claim to provide information about COVID-19.
    • Vishing: Similar to phishing but attackers reach out by phone instead of email. If you plan to transfer your office phone calls to your cell phone or home landline phone, please do not share information with someone calling you until you have verified their identity.  You can do this by calling them back at a known number or sending them a text.
    • Chat or Text-Based Social Engineering: Similar to phishing but attackers use instant messaging to communicate instead of email. If you receive a text message (SMS, Facebook Messenger, iMessage, etc.), please do not click on links or open attachments sent to you until you have verified the sender’s identity.  You can do this by calling them back at a known number or emailing them at their official university email address.

    Remember if you have received approval from your supervisor to work from home, it is incumbent upon you to protect the university data and resources you have access to.  You are responsible for familiarizing yourself with and adhering to our work from home policy that can be found at http://www.belmont.edu/lits/work-from-home.html This site also provides more details about securely working from home.  Thank you for your continued patience as we work to support you.
