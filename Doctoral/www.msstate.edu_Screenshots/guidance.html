COVID-19 Guidance for Exposure and Contact

Employee believes they have been exposed to COVID-19.

Employee actions

Stay home and contact University Health Services (UHS) by phone. If this occurs while you are at work, move away from others and call UHS. Do not arrive at the clinic without calling first.

Supervisor actions

Continue encouraging employees to follow spread-reduction steps.

Employee is tested at a facility other than the University Health Center.

Employee actions

Notify University Health Services (USH) and follow instructions they provide.

Supervisor actions

Follow additional guidance from this chart.

Employee is waiting on test results.

Employee actions

Continue to follow the guidance provided by University Health Services. Continue to self-isolate.

Supervisor actions

Contact Brent Crocker at 662-325-4521 for further instructions.

Employee receives a positive (+) test result.

Employee actions

Follow UHS instructions for self-isolation from a positive result. Report any problems to UHS.

Supervisor actions

Contact Brent Crocker at 662-325-4521 for further instructions.

Employee receives a negative (-) test result.

Employee actions

Continue social distancing and basic precautions.

Supervisor actions

Continue encouraging all employees to follow spread-reduction steps.

Employee is not feeling well and/or has symptoms that may be consistent with COVID-19.

Employee actions

Stay home and contact UHS at 662-325-2055 for further instructions.

Supervisor actions

Continue encouraging all employees to follow spread-reduction steps.

Employee has no symptoms but works in an area with someone who has been tested.

Employee actions

Results have not been returned. Continue to take basic precautions and report any concerns to UHS at 662-325-2055. You will be notified if you were listed as a close contact*.

Supervisor actions

Continue encouraging all employees to follow spread-reduction steps.

Employee has no symptoms but is a close contact* with someone with COVID-19.

Employee actions

Notify University Health Services (UHS) at 662-325-2055 for further guidance.

Supervisor actions

Contact Brent Crocker at 662-325-4521 for further instructions.

Employee has no symptoms but works in the same building or office as someone who is confirmed to have COVID-19.

Employee actions

Working in the same building does not generally qualify as close contact. Monitor symptoms. Contact UHS at 325-2055 with any questions.

Supervisor actions

Continue encouraging other employees to follow spread-reduction steps.

Employee has completed the required isolation but has shown some symptoms in the last three days.

Employee actions

Continue to self-isolate and contact University Health Services for further guidance.

Supervisor actions

Continue encouraging other employees to follow spread-reduction steps.

Employee has completed the required isolation and has shown no symptoms in the last three days.

Employee actions

Employee may return to work if cleared by their healthcare provider or University Health Services.

Supervisor actions

Continue encouraging other employees to follow spread-reduction steps.

*According to the CDC, a Close Contact is defined as:

a) being within approximately 6 feet (2 meters) of a COVID-19 case for at least 15 minutes; close contact can occur while caring for, living with, visiting, or sharing a healthcare waiting area or room with a COVID-19 case

– or –

b) having direct contact with infectious secretions of a COVID-19 case (e.g., being coughed on)

Contact tracing is a process used to determine if other individuals may have come in close contact with someone who is COVID-positive and would need to be notified. This determination should only be done by officials in University Health Services or at the Mississippi State Department of Health.

View a summary of CDC recommendations for management of exposed persons by risk level and presence of symptoms.

COVID-19 Reporting Process

Employee notifies University Health Services (662-325-2055) and their Supervisor.

  1. UHS determins best course of action for the employee.
    • UHS notifies close contacts
  2. Supervisor contacts Brent Crocker at 662-325-4521. Web form is completed.
    • Additional instructions and stand email template are provided for the building occupants.
    • The Web Form notifies EHS and Campus Services. Sanitation need assessment is conducted.
