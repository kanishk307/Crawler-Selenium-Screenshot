Academics and Research

(Updated July 1)

What is the status of summer school?

All summer full session and summer session A classes will be delivered fully online. Summer session B will allow face-to-face courses, labs, studios, etc.

If I’m a student enrolled in a face-to-face course in Summer Session B, do I need to do anything special?

Students enrolled in Summer Session B courses with a face-to-face component should fill out this Student COVID-19 Self-Assessment form every day before coming to campus and attending class.

What will classes look like in the fall?

The University plans to hold classes on a face-to-face basis this fall with a strict set of guidelines that will prioritize the safety of our students, faculty and staff. Those guidelines include: all students and employees wearing face coverings in shared indoor spaces and social distancing protocols in place around campus, with reconfigured classrooms that will have reduced seating. The University also has contingency plans for additional remote and hybrid classes that support teaching and learning in a flexible manner, should public health guidance dictate those approaches.

Campus Facilities and Events

(Updated June 15)

What is the Campus Dining schedule?

Reduced food service will be available to students who have decided to stay in their on-campus residence halls.

The Galley:

  • Breakfast: 9-10 am
  • Lunch: 11:30 am-1 pm
  • Dinner: 4:30-6 pm

Pop-Up Market in The Galley:

  • Breakfast: 9-10 am
  • Lunch: 11:30 am-1 pm
  • Dinner: 4:30-6 pm

    What is the status of the Earl K. Long Library?

    Beginning on Monday, June 15, the first and second floors of the library will reopen with limited hours to members of the University community. The dates and hours of operation are: Mondays: 8 a.m.-4:30 p.m., Tuesdays: 8 a.m.-6 p.m., Wednesdays-Fridays: 8 a.m.-4:30 p.m., closed on weekends. Library faculty and staff are also available to remotely assist students and employees, and all electronic resources continue to be available.

    What is the status of the UNO Bookstore?

    The bookstore is open from 8 a.m.-4:30 p.m. Monday-Thursday and 8 a.m.-2 p.m. on Friday with a maximum of 10 customers in the store at a time. Students can also theunoshop.com. Students with questions about e-books or returns should contact Kathleen Davis at kcdavis@follett.com.

    What about campus mail?

    For now, USPS, UPS and FedEx are delivering all mail and packages (including those sent to students and departments) to Campus Mail. The department name and the main 2000 Lakeshore Drive address is sufficient. Campus Mail will hold mail and packages for pick-up during its regular window hours 8:30 a.m.-4 p.m. Monday-Friday. Campus Mail is located in the UC room 112.

    How is the UNO Federal Credit Union affected?

    The credit union’s main branch in the Homer Hitt Alumni & Visitors Center is open from 9 a.m.-4 p.m. That includes both the lobby and the drive-thru.

    Are any events still being held on campus?

    Access to campus is limited to the University community and partners. At this point, in our current Phase B of reopening, indoor venues are limited to 50 people or 25% of capacity, whichever is less.

    Is campus closed?

    The University has begun a phased methodical return to campus. For now, in our current Phase B of reopening, that only includes students who have remained in their residence halls, employees, designated contractors, tenants and customers of the UNO Federal Credit Union. At this stage, there is only one open entrance/exit on campus, Alumni Drive at Elysian Fields, which will be monitored by the UNOPD. All other entrances and exits are closed.

    For students who remain in the residence halls, what health services are available?

    Wellness

    (Updated June 26)

    If I am a UNO employee or student and I believe I have come into contact with someone infected with COVID-19, what should I do?

    Employees who believe they were exposed to someone infected with the virus should alert their supervisor and Human Resources. They should contact their health provider and get tested. They should not return to campus until they’ve been medically cleared. If a student believes they were exposed to an infected person, they should notify Student Affairs (studentaffairs@uno.edu) and consult with their health care provider for next steps.

    What mental health resources are available to all members of the UNO community?

    The Louisiana Department of Health has launched a mental health hotline, “Keeping Calm Through COVID,” at 1-866-310-7977. It is open 24/7.

    Travel and Study Abroad

    Updated March 20)

    What is the status of UNO's summer study abroad programs?

    All 2020 summer study abroad programs in Austria, Japan, Italy, France and Ireland have been canceled. Affected students, faculty and staff have been notified. If you have questions, email isp@uno.edu or visit https://www.uno.edu/international-center.

    What is the status of University-sponsored travel?

    Until further notice, the State of Louisiana has prohibited all state-sponsored international travel. Additionally, the U.S. State Department is advising U.S. citizens to avoid all international travel due to the global impact of COVID-19. Out-of-state travel, whether by ground or air transportation, must be approved in advance by the Commissioner of Administration and will be evaluated based on business necessity. These restrictions apply to new, as well as previously approved travel requests.

    Student Resources

    (Updated June 15)

    What if I'm a student and I don't have a laptop or computer?

    The Earl K. Long Library has a limited number of laptops available for students to check out. If you wish to check out a device, please email libref@uno.edu.

    What if I’m a student who lives off-campus and I’m struggling to find Internet access?

    The Earl K. Long Library has a limited number of WiFi hotspots for students who are enrolled in summer school. Students can check out a hotspot by emailing libref@uno.edu. The devices will be distributed on a first come-first served basis.

    How has COVID-19 affected the Louisiana Tuition Opportunity Program for Students (TOPS)?

    Governor John Bel Edwards signed a proclamation to ensure high school seniors and current college students are able to meet requirements to be eligible for the Taylor Opportunity Program for Students (TOPS). The information about requirement adjustments can be found here.

    I currently have federal student loans and the COVID-19 outbreak may affect my ability to make payments. What are my options?

    Congress passed the Coronavirus Aid, Relief, and Economic Security (CARES) Act into law. The CARES Act provides several education-related provisions outlined here.

    What about federal loans and pell grants?

    Congress passed the Coronavirus Aid, Relief, and Economic Security (CARES) Act into law. The CARES Act provides several education-related provisions outlined here.

    What if I’m a student experiencing a loss of income due to COVID-19?

    Students who are experiencing a loss of income may be eligible for unemployment benefits. Students may also be eligible for SNAP benefits in response to COVID-19.
