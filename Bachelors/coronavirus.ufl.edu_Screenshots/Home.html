COVID-19 Response

REOPENING PLAN

The University of Florida is moving forward with its reopening plan, as approved by the Board of Governors June 23. The plan outlines how the university, under the guidance of UF Health, will strive to create a safer, healthier campus environment and continue its research and education mission using screening and testing, enhanced protective measures, comprehensive communications and other tools. We welcome input on the UF plan and expect to remain flexible as conditions evolve.

July 1: UF released campus events and gathering policies in response to COVID-19, effective July 1, 2020. 

July 1: Students received an email from Vice President D'Andra Mull that offers guidance on how students can be screened before the return to campus. For additional questions, visit UF Health Screen, Test & Protect.

July 1: UF announced the reopening plan and fall schedule will be released July 10.

June 23: The Board of Governors approved UF's Reopening Plan, which outlines the university's path forward for the fall semester. More details will be available July 10.
