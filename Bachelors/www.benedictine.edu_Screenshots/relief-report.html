Higher Education Emergency Relief Fund Report

Benedictine College signed and returned the Recipient’s Funding Certification and Agreement for Emergency Financial Aid Grants to Students under the Coronavirus Aid, Relief and Economic Security (CARES) Act.  Benedictine College received $710,248.00 and will distribute these funds in direct cash grants to eligible students.

As of May 22, 2020, Benedictine College has distributed $615,248.00. This statement will be updated every 45 days.

Benedictine College estimates that 1,450 are eligible to participate in programs under Section 484 in Title IV of the Higher Education Act of 1965. As of May 22, 2020, 1,135 students received a Emergency Financial Aid Grant.

Benedictine College created two methods to distribute these funds. The largest portion ($615,248) was issued through a general distribution which started on May 12, 2020. This pool was distributed to nearly 1,135 students in increments of $1,000, $600, $400 and $125 based on their level of financial need. No action was required on the part of the student for the first distribution. The remaining portion ($95,000) remains available for students to apply for reimbursement.

General Distribution Criteria:

  1. Criteria One: Verified Title IV eligible students.
  2. Criteria Two: Expected family contribution (EFC) is at or below the cost of attendance (COA), which is set at $46,000.

All students who meet the criteria received funds. The amount they received varied based on their level of unmet need. Those with no need but have an EFC below COA received a standard amount.

Unmet Need Tiers:

  1. Tier One ($14,000+ of unmet need): $1,000
  2. Second Tier ($13,999 to $8,000 of unmet need): $600
  3. Third Tier ($7,999 to $1 of unmet need): $400

No need but EFC is at or below COA:
 Standard amount of $125 per student. 

Application for Emergency Funds:
The application opened on Friday, May 15, 2020. The deadline for submitting an application is June 15, 2020. A student must apply using this online form. Funds will be awarded until the grant funds have been exhausted.
