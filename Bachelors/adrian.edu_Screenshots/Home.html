Adrian College COVID-19 Updates

Apr 6, 2020
Tweet

ADRIAN COLLEGE — Mulch is being spread, flowers are blooming, green grass is growing and just like every other year, Adrian College’s campus is simply beautiful during springtime. The only thing missing are the people who make Adrian College the fine educational facility it is. However, it is just a matter of time before the campus will be back to normal and filled once again with the wonderful energy, vitality and comradery of students, faculty and staff that Adrian College is known for.

To help you understand what is going on during the uncertain times brought on by the Coronavirus, Adrian College’s senior staff has decided it cannot overcommunicate with our students and the community, and will continue to provide regular updates. The senior staff meets each week to determine the issues and actions that should be shared widely so we can all stay informed.

Last week, Adrian College President Jeffrey Docking sent out four personalized videos; to all students, incoming students, graduating students, and alumni and friends of the college. To view the videos, use the following links:

https://vimeo.com/402622631 - All students

https://vimeo.com/402607653 - Incoming Bulldogs

https://vimeo.com/402344105 - Spring grads

https://vimeo.com/403354340 - Alumni and friends

The College is reminding students that the May Term (May 6-June 3) will be online. Enrollment and registration are currently open for the May term, online at https://apply.adrian.edu/. The Registrar’s Office is open and available to help with the enrollment and registration process. For information on available courses, go online to http://adrian.edu/students-staff/.

Adrian College recently decided that the Summer Term (June 10-July 22) courses will also be online.

As a reminder, registration is now open for all current students for the fall semester. For more information or to sign up, go online to the Registrar’s Office, http://adrian.edu/academics/registrar/. Housing applications are also being accepted, http://adrian.edu/admissions/incoming-student-information/housing-information/.

Adrian College has remained open during this outbreak. Housing has continued to be available for students as well as curbside library services, Academic Support Services, and professors are providing online classes. Ritchie Dining also remained open and is offering carry-out services as ordered by Governor Gretchen Whitmer.

The College has received questions regarding room and board reimbursements. Our senior staff is currently looking into these questions and a separate correspondence will be sent from the Business Office soon to all affected students.

Students are reminded to retrieve personal housing items prior to May 3. Students should check their College email for a Google sign-up sheet to select a checkout date and time. All students must fill out an express checkout waiver in the Caine Student Center before they depart from the college. Forms are available on a table in front of the student mailboxes. Once completed, take the form and key and return it in the drop slot by the mailroom door on the wall. Any student unable to retrieve their belongings or unable to leave by May 3 needs to fill out an appeal form to remain on campus. See the following link: https://forms.gle/15cqJCxBz11ecgLj7. For more information, e-mail housing@adrian.edu.

Adrian College is deeply grateful for your understanding, cooperation and partnership during this unprecedented time. The College will continue sending updates via email and adding additional reports to adrian.edu to keep you informed.

Adrian College COVID-19 Update, 3-30-2020

ADRIAN COLLEGE (3.30.2020) — President Donald Trump on Sunday, March 29, 2020 extended federal social distancing guidelines through April 30, to slow the spread of Coronavirus (COVID-19).

President Trump said in a White House news conference that modeling estimates show that the virus will likely peak in two weeks. He added the United States will be “well on our way to recovery, we think by June 1.”

Adrian College’s senior staff created the following information to respond to the most commonly asked questions. We hope this information is helpful, but please do not hesitate to call the College if you have additional questions.

Are classes online for the remainder of the semester?

Yes. Because of the social distancing extension, Adrian College will continue classes, labs and studios online through the May semester, including exams. Adrian College will also determine at a later date if there will be face-to-face summer classes.

Will graduation be postponed?

Yes. The extension of limiting groups to 10 people or less until April 30 has forced Adrian College to postpone its May 3 commencement.

Is the College committed to another date and time for graduation?

Yes. Adrian College President Jeffrey Docking told his senior staff on Monday the College will recognize the accomplishments of its seniors and graduate students at an appropriate time.

“When the threat of Coronavirus is over, we are fully committed to providing a graduation ceremony for our students,” Docking said. “We understand the amount of hard work and commitment it takes to earn a degree from Adrian College. My senior team is looking at all options available to reschedule the graduation ceremony at a time that is convenient for students and families in the not-too-distant future.”

Is the College open?

Yes. The College has remained open and is following all of Michigan Governor Gretchen Whitmer’s orders, pursuant to Executive Order 2020-9. Most departments, including academic support services, residence halls and food services, remain open at this time.

Are custodial services still on campus cleaning high-traffic areas?

Yes. Custodial services remain on campus and our staff is committed to providing a safe, healthy environment for the College community.

Are there any confirmed COVID-19 cases on campus?

To the best of our knowledge, there are no reported or confirmed COVID-19 cases on campus. Adrian College’s student Health Center Director, Dr. Emily Kist, continues to monitor the situation closely and sends out regular updates.

Have any students remained on campus?

Yes. Adrian College currently has 153 students on campus. Students have been allowed to remain in their dorms for a variety of reasons. A number of students have asked to remain on campus for reasons including: their homes are located in areas where a large number of people have the virus. Some students have stated they have vulnerable family members and they don’t want to pose a health risk. Other students are relying on the campus internet to complete courses, as well as utilize carryout food services in our dining areas.

Adrian College is very grateful that Sodexo at Ritchie Marketplace has continued offering to-go lunch and dinner options, and is following the Governor’s social distancing and facility cleaning guidelines.

Is Adrian College abiding by all social distancing guidelines?

Yes. David Stimpson, Chairman of the Lenawee County Board of Commissioners, on March 27 released Emergency Order 2020-02 requiring all Lenawee County businesses and operations that are open to screen employees daily for COVID-19 symptoms and implement a plan to provide for social distancing. Adrian College is following the emergency order and will be screening any employee who physically reports to the College campus. In addition, updates concerning COVID-19 are being sent out on a regular basis to students, faculty and staff. Flyers with updates on this ongoing health issue will also be posted at strategic locations around campus.

Does the College security staff still have a presence on campus?

Yes. Adrian College’s campus currently has security coverage, 24/7.

Can a student who went home retrieve personal items they left in their residence hall?

Yes. Sunday, May 3 is the deadline for students who want to retrieve items from their room. Please contact Melinda Schwyn, Dean of Students/Student Affairs, to schedule a pick-up.

Is the bookstore open?

The Bookstore is currently offering online orders only. For more information, go online to https://arringtonbookstore.adrian.edu/ or email bookstore@adrian.edu.

First positive COVID-19 case reported in Lenawee County

ADRIAN COLLEGE (3.25.20) — The Lenawee County Health Department (LCHD) announced on Tuesday that the first positive test result for the Coronavirus Disease 2019 (COVID-19) has been reported in Lenawee County. An unnamed adult female from Lenawee County tested positive and is currently self-isolating. The LCHD has contacted those who have been in close contact with the patient to assess for symptoms and provide quarantine instructions. The LCHD said in a press release that it will monitor the woman.

Individuals who have been in close contact with someone who has COVID-19 should self-quarantine for 14 days and monitor themselves for fever, cough, and shortness of breath. COVID-19 symptoms may appear in as few as two days or as long as 14 days after exposure to the virus.

“Residents in Lenawee County can help prevent the spread of COVID-19 by staying home and following Governor Gretchen Whitmer’s “Stay Home, Stay Safe” executive order,” Martha Hall, Health Officer of the Lenawee County Health Department, said in a press release. “Everyone must do their part to prevent the spread of COVID-19. Your actions today will affect not only your future, but the future of your friends and family.”

To prevent the spread of COVID-19, the LCHD recommends:

• Washing hands with soap frequently 

• Cover coughs and sneezes with a tissue or cough or sneeze into an elbow 

• Avoid touching the face

• Stay home when sick

• Avoid close contact with sick people

• Only leave home for essential services

• Keep six feet between themselves and others when having to leave home

People who are concerned that they may have COVID-19 should call their healthcare provider. They will likely be assessed by phone to determine if they should be tested for COVID-19. If testing is indicated, they will provide the person with instructions to get tested. If a person does not have symptoms, they do not need to be tested.

Individuals may also call the LCHD at (517) 264.5226, Option 5, Monday through Friday between 8 a.m. and 4:30 p.m., or the Michigan Department of Human Services Hotline at (888) 535.6136, seven days a week between 8 a.m. and 5 p.m.

Information about the COVID-19 situation locally, and in Michigan, is rapidly changing. For more information, visit www.lenaweehealthdepartment.org.

Adrian College updates list of event cancellations

ADRIAN COLLEGE (3.20.19) — While there have been no confirmed cases of the Coronavirus Disease 2019 (COVID-19) on Adrian College’s campus or Lenawee County, the College is taking extra precautions and following Michigan Governor Gretchen Whitmer’s recommendations to ensure the containment of this health threat. Governor Whitmer recently tightened group gathering regulations throughout the State, limiting all events or assemblages to 50 people or less to help contain the spread of COVID-19, which has forced numerous cancellations and postponements at Adrian College.

“We are very disappointed in having to cancel our campus events, but understand it is in the best interest of our students and community to do so,” Adrian College President Jeffrey Docking said.

Consistent with the Governor’s earlier executive orders, Adrian College discontinued all face-to-face classes and now offer online courses so all of its students can finish the semester. The Campus’ wireless networking service remains functioning and available to all students. With additional restrictions in place, Adrian College has extended its online classes through April 10.

At this time, a final decision has not been made regarding postponing Adrian College’s Commencement ceremony. Should the May 3 event be cancelled, arrangements will be made to conduct the ceremony at a later date after restrictions are lifted.

“If we are forced to cancel our scheduled Commencement ceremony, Adrian College is fully committed to give our graduates the recognition they deserve by offering an alternative commencement date for those able to attend,” President Docking said.

All other dates and times concerning academic schedules remain in place. All students who complete their classwork will still officially graduate on May 3, as previously planned, with or without a ceremony.

The College continues to work with its international students and supports whatever decision they and their families make concerning a return to their homes. Adrian College will work with these students to complete academic requirements.

Adrian College’s residence halls and dining service remain open, as well as all administration offices and student support services.

Ritchie Marketplace will be open from 11 a.m. to 1 p.m. and 5 to 7 p.m., and the Caine Student Center’s Grill & Zime will be open from noon to 7 p.m. Paws & Go C-store is open 24 hours, offering selections of to-go items. All three locations are accommodating Adrian College students by offering a wide variety of carryout food selections.

The Student Health Center remains open and its Director, Dr. Emily Kist, is emailing the campus community regularly with updates.

Custodial services on campus have been increased to ensure all facilities are cleaned regularly.

In addition, campus security has also been increased to ensure Adrian College is following state orders, for the health and safety of its students, faculty and staff.

Student workers should contact their employment supervisor regarding continual work options.

The Adrian College campus is no longer open to the public until further notice.

Ritchie Marketplace remains open, converts to mandatory carryout operation

ADRIAN COLLEGE (3.16.20) — In order to comply with the most recent Governor Gretchen Whitmer order banning restaurants from offering dine-in services through March, Ritchie Marketplace converted to a full mandatory carryout operation at 3 p.m. today, March 16. Guests will be provided with all the items needed to take their food to go and there will be no seated dining inside any area of the cafe. Slight modifications have been made to stations to convert self-service areas into staff-served areas to limit customer contact, and staff ask that guests attempt to keep a distance of six feet while within both Ritchie Marketplace and Caine Student Center.

Nicholas Moorehead, General Manager of Sodexo at Adrian College, expects the food service facilities to continue to maintain the same levels of service in regards to food options under the current order, though certain items will need to be requested from staff if previously found in a full self-serve area.

“Staff will continue constant cleaning of customer contact areas to ensure the health and safety of our patrons,” Moorehead said.

Gov. Whitmer limits all events and assemblages until April 5

ADRIAN COLLEGE (3.16.20) — Adrian College continues to monitor and conduct regular physical counts on campus during the COVID-19 outbreak. The Ritchie Marketplace has opened additional space to allow limited seating in separate spaces, due to Governor Gretchen Whitmer’s Executive Order to cancel all events over 250 people and all assemblages in shared spaces over 250 people beginning on Friday, March 13 at 5 p.m. The following announcement was released by the State of Michigan earlier today.

Today, Governor Gretchen Whitmer signed Executive Order 2020-5 to cancel all events over 250 people and all assemblages in shared spaces over 250 people beginning on Friday, March 13 at 5 p.m. and ending on Sunday, April 5 at 5 p.m. The governor’s executive order also closes all K-12 school buildings to students from Monday, March 16 until Sunday, April 5. Child care facilities will remain open during this time, whether they are attached to schools or free standing. 

Under this executive order, all events and shared-space assemblages of more than 250 people are temporarily prohibited in the state. Certain assemblages are exempt from this prohibition, such as those for the purpose of: industrial or manufacturing work, mass transit, or the purchase of groceries or consumer goods.

“This is about protecting the most people we can from the spread of coronavirus,” said Governor Whitmer. “My administration will continue to do everything we can to mitigate the spread, and to ensure our children, families, and businesses have the support they need during this time. We are going to get through this, but we must be flexible and take care of each other.”

“Prohibiting large assemblages is the smart thing to do to mitigate the spread of COVID-19,” said Michigan Department of Health and Human Services Chief Deputy for Health and Chief Medical Executive Dr. Joneigh Khaldun. “It’s crucial that everyone in Michigan follow these orders and make smart choices that will slow the spread of the virus, including practicing good hygiene and social distancing. We will continue to work with the governor and our partners across state government to protect Michigan families and businesses.” 

Patients with confirmed infection have reportedly had mild to severe respiratory illness with symptoms of: 

• Fever 

• Cough 

• Shortness of breath

The best prevention for viruses, such as influenza, the common cold or COVID-19 is to:

If you think you have been exposed to COVID-19, call your health care provider. If you do not have a health care provider, call the nearest hospital. 

Wash your hands often with soap and warm water for 20 seconds. If not available, use hand sanitizer.   

Avoid touching your eyes, nose, or mouth with unwashed hands.   

Cover your mouth and nose with a tissue or upper sleeve when coughing or sneezing.   

Avoid contact with people who are sick.   

If you are sick, stay home, and avoid contact with others. 

Replace handshakes with elbow bumps. 

Stay at least 6 feet away from others when in a public setting. Information around this outbreak is changing rapidly.

The latest information is available at Michigan.gov/Coronavirus and CDC.gov/Coronavirus.

Adrian College responds to Gov. Whitmer recommendations to limit spread of COVID-19

ADRIAN COLLEGE (3.12.20) — In light of the current recommendations from the office of Michigan Governor Gretchen Whitmer, Adrian College intends to limit the risk of COVID-19 exposure by reducing in-person events scheduled on campus.

“I urge all Michiganders to take these recommendations seriously and to share them with their friends, families and coworkers,” Whitmer said, “It is on all of us to be safe and be smart for ourselves, our loved ones, our coworkers, and the public at large. We encourage schools, universities, businesses and other organizations to use their best judgement about what steps are most appropriate to keep people safe and slow the spread of the disease.”

Following the recommendations and guidelines from the State of Michigan, Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, the World Health Organization and other agencies, as of noon on Thursday, March 12, Adrian College canceled all face-to-face classes through Friday, March 13. Classes will resume on Monday, March 16 in online format. Face-to-face classes will resume on Monday, March 30 unless otherwise notified.

The following action has been implemented to ensure a quick response to Governor Whitmer’s requests:

Conferences and Events

• Pursuant to Governor Whitmer’s recommendations to cancel or postpone large gatherings, conferences and sporting events (e.g. events with over 100 people), Adrian College will be cancelling or rescheduling the following events:

• Bruiser Briefcase Bash, March 13, 2020

• Bulldog Ice Show, March 15, 2020

• Spring Concert, March 20, 2020

• Sweetheart Prom, March 21, 2020

• Convocation, March 25, 2020

• The President’s State of the College Address, April 3, 2020

• At this time, Honors Night and Ribbons of Excellence Day, March 31, 2020 will not be canceled. Alternative ways to deliver presentations are being planned.  

• Decisions will be made on a case-by-case basis whether to cancel smaller conference groups or individuals who are renting College facilities.

Adrian College Preparations

Academic Affairs:

• Implementing online learning beginning March 16, 2020. All faculty members have Blackboard access for all classes, enabling professors and students to have synchronized lessons.

• Face-to-face classes are expected to resume March 30, 2020.

• The Shipman Library will continue to be accessible 24/7.

Housing and Student Life:

• Dr. Emily Kist in the Student Health Center provides the following protocols for students who are concerned they may have symptoms:

   • If a patient calls the Medical Clinic and is concerned they have been exposed to Coronavirus, they will NOT be brought into the clinic.

   • Students with this concern will be given the number to Lenawee Health Department who will direct them to the next steps.

   • If a student comes to the Medical Clinic, they will be given a mask and the Health Department will be contacted.

   • Travel areas of concern: China, Italy, South Korea, and Iran as well as any cruises.

   • If a student elects to go to the Bixby Medical Center ER on their own, they must call ahead and inform ER staff of their symptoms. Staff will instruct them on the course of action.

   • If a student “fails” a travel screen in the Medical Clinic, they will be sent to the Bixby Medical Center ER. Student will then call the Bixby Medical Center and be instructed to follow defined protocol.

• Additional hand sanitizer stations will be made available throughout campus.

• Any student who feels ill is asked to self-quarantine until the medical procedures above are followed.

Dining Options:

• Students are encouraged to avoid communal dining when possible. Beginning Friday, March 13, 2020 the College is suspending all rules limiting students from taking food from Ritchie Marketplace to allow for social separation. Sodexo will provide carry-out containers and students will be allowed to take food from the dining hall. Additional space will be made available in Ritchie Marketplace to allow students more space for lunch and dinner, if they prefer sit-down dining.

• Grab-and-Go options will remain available at the Caine Student Center.

Athletics:

• All winter and spring championships have been cancelled by the NCAA [https://www.ncaa.com/live-updates/ncaa-cancels-remaining-winter-and-spring-championships-due-coronavirus-concerns] and a decision will be made shortly regarding MIAA conference sports activities. 

• Athletes who are ill will be quarantined. 

• Fitness Center and weight room will remain open with additional cleaning measures. enforced. The College has taken additional steps to increase cleaning frequency of all equipment. Hand sanitizer will also be available. Custodial support will be reallocated to high traffic areas including the Fitness Center.

• Sauna and steam rooms are closed until further notice.

• WOW Cafe will be closed until further notice.

Baby Bulldog Center

• At this time, the Baby Bulldog Center will remain open until further notice.

College Offices

• All Adrian College offices will remain open. Staff should continue to report to work as usual. Student workers and interns should contact their supervisors for further instruction.

• Employees and student workers should follow the guidelines from the recent Governor Whitmer communication regarding actions to mitigate transmission of COVID-19. https://www.michigan.gov/coronavirus/0,9753,7-406-98178_98155-521467—,00.html.

Additional Information

The College is taking every precaution to protect the health and safety of its campus community, as it is always a top priority. Your cooperation will help ensure an appropriate response across our institution to appropriately address this unprecedented health issue. Adrian College’s website will be regularly updated with any new information or changes to keep the community informed on the progress of the COVID-19 outbreak.

Sincerely,
