Coronavirus Information

Following a three-month comprehensive planning process involving faculty, staff, and administrators across 16 task groups, Penn State officials have decided the University can meet or exceed the Pennsylvania Department of Education guidelines for colleges and universities to return to campus and looks forward to welcoming back faculty, staff, and students to resume on-campus classes and other activities this fall.

Penn State has announced the University, including all campuses, will resume on-campus work and learning in the fall semester. For the latest information, see the University’s Coronavirus Information updates website. Information specific to Penn State York is below.

Message from Dr. David Christiansen, Chancellor, June 24, 2020

Penn State President Eric Barron announced on June 15 that faculty and staff members would begin a gradual return to campuses with a goal of resuming residential instruction on Monday, August 24. This return will be conducted in a series of phases to ensure that a healthy campus environment has been prepared for all individuals. The Penn State York Return to Work Committee has been created to implement University safety guidelines, including the enhanced cleaning of public spaces; reconfiguration of offices and classrooms; comprehensive signage; determination of occupancy limits for classrooms, labs, and offices; and a variety of social-distancing measures, including a requirement that masks must be worn at all times while on campus.

Faculty and staff members wishing to visit campus briefly to pick up documents or materials should contact campus Police and Public Safety at 814-810-7880.

The University has also established a process by which faculty and staff members are permitted to return to campus on a regular basis, starting as early as July:

  1. Faculty members wishing to return to campus to resume in-office or in-lab work should submit their requests to Bob Farrell, who will review them with the Penn State York Return to Work Committee. Once this review is complete, the campus will submit the request to the Office of the Vice President for Commonwealth Campuses (OVPCC) in University Park for final approval. Faculty members will be allowed to return to campus in phases, with top priority given to individuals who need on-campus access to labs and equipment. We will notify faculty members once the OVPCC has approved their return to campus. We anticipate that the entire review process may take two-three weeks. The University continues to expect that faculty members who can work at home should continue working at home throughout the summer.
  2. Staff members (including tech service workers) wishing to return to campus to resume in-office work should indicate so to their supervisor. Supervisors will submit their plan for staff members to return to yk-returntowork@psu.edu. Once our Return to Work committee has reviewed these requests, we will send them to the OVPCC in University Park for final approval. We will notify staff members once the OVPCC has approved their return to campus. We anticipate that the entire review process may take two-three weeks. The University continues to expect that staff members who can work at home should continue working at home throughout the summer.
  3. Penn State students may not come onto campus until the beginning of the fall semester on Monday, August 24.
  4. Members of the public may not come onto campus until the beginning of the fall semester on Monday, August 24.

These guidelines will evolve over the summer as the University and campus continue prepare for the resumption of in-class instruction in August. In the meanwhile, I wish you all to stay healthy!

David

Frequently Asked Questions Regarding Penn State York’s Response to the Global Coronavirus Outbreak

Penn State York is providing a campus-specific set of Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) to help provide information during this global coronavirus outbreak. The FAQs will be updated as information becomes available. We realize that our students, faculty, staff, and the community have specific questions about how the campus will handle modified operations.

Also visit the University’s FAQs page concerning coronavirus for more information.

 

Is the campus closed?

Buildings and offices at Penn State York are locked down, with all business being conducted remotely. The campus is not open to the general public for face-to-face services. Please refer to the contact list for information on how to reach departments for services.

Students are no longer permitted on campus, and all classes are being taught remotely. Students who need assistance accessing a remotely delivered class should contact Penn State IT at 814-865-HELP (4357) or ITservicedesk@psu.edu. The University is assisting students who lack a computer and/or access to broadband internet.

Faculty members are still permitted to come to campus to continue their teaching but they are strongly encouraged to teach from home. If teaching from home is not possible, faculty members should be teaching from their computer in their office. Only as a final resort will classrooms be unlocked for instructors to deliver their classes. Faculty members will be able to teach from a limited number of classrooms, as each room needs to be cleaned after each class.

Staff members (including technical service employees) are permitted to come to campus only if they receive permission from their immediate supervisor. Telecommuting is being promoted and, when necessary, supervisors will identify alternative work assignments that can be completed at home.

As ordered by the Governor, all employees must wear a cloth mask while working and performing essential duties on campus.

Penn State York faculty and staff should continue telecommuting through at least May 31, unless instructed otherwise by their supervisors.

 

Academic Affairs

I am not comfortable with remote delivery of my classes. Where can I get more information to make it easier for me?

Visiting Remote Learning at Penn State is a good place to start! Your professors can also provide you with specific information about the courses they are teaching.

Is tutoring still available while face-to-face instruction is suspended?

Yes, the Nittany Success Center (NSC) is offering Synchronous Online Tutoring currently. The staff will return to in-person tutoring when classes resume on campus.

Request a Tutor online to view our tutoring schedule, make appointments, review instructions for online tutoring, reset password directions and also watch a video demonstrating how online tutoring works. Contact information for the NSC is also listed there.

I have not heard from one of my professors. What should I do?

Start by emailing your professor directly using the email address on the course syllabus that you were given at the beginning of the semester. If you don’t hear back from the professor within 24 hours, please contact YK-Academic-Affairs@psu.edu.

I am involved in undergraduate research this semester with one of my professors. When can I resume my work?

Until the campus reopens for face-to-face instruction, students may not come to campus for this purpose. The hope is that students and their faculty mentors will be able to once again collaborate in person at the beginning of the fall semester.

I’ve worked really hard to earn my college degree. What is happening with the commencement ceremony?

A University-wide spring virtual commencement was held on Saturday, May 9, at 2:00 p.m. To view the virtual commencement and special pages dedicated to York graduates follow this link Spring 2020 Virtual Commencement.

How can I obtain a copy of the commencement program?

Each graduate will receive one copy of the commencement program. The programs will be sent to the graduate’s mailing address listed in Lion Path, and should arrive in late summer.

Will face-to-face instruction resume when the fall semester begins?

The University is actively exploring this possibility and will deliver guidance for all Penn State campuses by June 15.  At that time, the campus will be in a better position to answer this question.  Penn State York is likewise planning for a return to campus now so that, when the time comes, all members of the campus community will be able to do so safely.

More information and how to get started with Virtual Life Hacks

You@PSU

You@PSU is a unique and free web portal dedicated to student health, happiness and success. You@PSU emphasizes individual wellbeing and self-awareness in order to connect students to information, campus resources, peers and opportunities—whenever they need it.

You@PSU was created to mirror the lifestyle of college students. With expertise in three main areas — Succeed, Thrive and Matter —You@PSU helps students assess and improve their health, sense of purpose and life trajectory. You@PSU works best when answers are honest and open. For that reason, all personal information that students provide on You@PSU is kept private.

  • Step 1:  Visit https://you.psu.edu. On the homepage, students can get a breakdown of the portal or just jump right in.
  • Step 2:  Students, faculty and staff can sign up using their university email account. As an extension of campus, You@PSU keeps all personal information that students provide on the You@PSU portal private.
  • Step 3:  Discover. Once inside, students can start to customize their profile or take one of three Reality Checks. Both features are designed to cater content like tips, quizzes, videos and campus resources based on general needs and interests of individual students.
  • Step 4:  Revisit. The You@PSU portal is intended to be used throughout a student’s college life. With changing content and dynamic tools, there’s a use for YOU from move-in day to landing that first job.

Online Synchronous Tutoring video demo

Do I need to use my Penn State email to login to the tutoring schedule?

Do I need to set up an account in the scheduling system?

  • Click on START OR JOIN ONLINE CONSULTATION at the bottom of the page.

I am a student who is hurting financially. Is there any support available for students who may be experiencing difficulty?

Penn State has Student Emergency Funds that can help students experiencing financial difficulty. Contact Scott Simonds for more information.

I have purchased the Penn State Student Health Insurance Plan (SHIP). How do I access coverage if I need to see a doctor?

Details about the Student Health Insurance Plan (SHIP), including coverages, customer care contact information, and a provider directory, are available through the First Student website. For more information, students may contact the Student Insurance Office at 814-865-7467 or e-mail uhs-insurance@psu.edu.

Contacting Campus Offices

Penn State York is committed to providing information and support to the campus and community during the coronavirus pandemic. Most faculty and staff members are working remotely to help contain the spread of the coronavirus as recommended by the state and federal government. The campus will continue to provide quality services throughout this crisis. Please continue to call campus offices and communicate via your Penn State email. Phone calls and emails will be answered between 8:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m., Monday through Friday. Those calls and emails placed after hours will be answered the next business day. If face-to-face meetings are necessary, please schedule an appointment with the appropriate office.

Buildings on campus are locked, but those who must visit campus will be able to gain access. If there are not people working in the building, visitors can call Police and Public Safety at 717-771-8444.
