HPU Dining Updates
All meals are MAGIC! HPU Dining has increased the magic meals on all food plans so a student can choose any food location. We are investing in technology so a student can now view wait times at all 15 locations and choose a location according to wait times. All dining facilities are operating at half capacity. Mobile ordering and pick up will be available at all food locations on campus. The card swipe will be replaced by the card scan to limit interaction. At Farmer’s Market and The Café, our hospitality team will now serve each student, eliminating self-serve stations. Our food locations will also be part of the university’s Clean Team, maintaining sanitization protocols continuously.

View the Fall 2020 HPU Dining Brochure It provides helpful information about meal plans. You can also visit the HPU Dining website.

Events
To further reduce opportunities for people to be in close proximity with one another and in accordance with the latest statewide recommendations as of June 12, High Point University is asking faculty, staff and students to cancel, postpone, or adjust any university-hosted meeting or gathering that will have 10 participants or more. This includes all camps and conferences and other events such as performances, social events and professional meetings. This does not preclude small internal meetings, where physical distancing should be implemented.

Adjustments to events should include technology to allow remote participation. Cisco WebEx technology is available to all High Point University community members and can be used to move an event, or portions of it, online.

As you postpone, cancel, or adjust an event, please communicate quickly and clearly with participants.

Unless the benefits clearly outweigh the rising risk of disease transmission, we recommend adjusting all meetings, even if it will involve less than 10 people. If a meeting is essential, we recommend the smallest possible group. The CDC reports that the virus can spread between people who are within six feet of one another. Consider if the venue allows people adequate space to spread out. Adjust meeting rooms and formats as needed. Please remind meeting participants to take personal precautions and practice good hygiene, and clean and disinfect commonly touched surfaces in all frequently used public areas. As is currently recommended by the Centers for Disease Control, cloth face coverings will be required in all classrooms, labs and studios. Face coverings are strongly recommended in all other public spaces on campus as well.

We will continue to monitor the spread and containment of COVID-19 and make adjustments to these plans if necessary.

NOTE: Information about Move-In Weekend is available here. Information about Convocation for the Class of 2024 and Fall 2020 Family Weekend is below in the FAQ section.

Campus Shuttles
Buses will be disinfected after every shift, a minimum of two times per day. Buses will operate at half capacity. Occupying aisle seats will not be allowed. Masks will be mandatory on the bus. Disposable masks will be available if a student doesn’t have one. Empty seats will be marked off.  Drivers will wear masks or face shields. For campus shuttle routes and information, visit www.highpoint.edu/transportation.

Recreation
As of June 24, in accordance with an executive order issued by North Carolina Governor Roy Cooper, recreation centers are currently not open. The Slane pool is open, and guidelines such as physical distancing and limiting capacity apply to these spaces. Should the executive order change, more information will be communicated to students. Some wellness activities will continue to be provided virtually.

Residential and Common Spaces
HPU’s roommate agreement is thorough and includes standards for cleaning, personal hygiene and use of shared spaces. In order to maintain high standards of health and safety, regular room checks that have always occurred will continue by authorized individuals, including, but not limited to, residence hall staff. The university’s Clean Team is frequently disinfecting high-traffic touch points across campus.

We encourage students to follow guidelines outlined by the CDC and products recommended by the EPA to conduct routine cleaning in their private residential spaces. Sanitize frequently touched surfaces (for example: tables, doorknobs, light switches, handles, desks, toilets, faucets, sinks and electronics) with household cleaners and EPA-registered disinfectants that are appropriate for the surface, following label instructions. Labels contain instructions for safe and effective use of the cleaning product including precautions you should take when applying the product, such as wearing gloves and making sure you have good ventilation during use of the product.

Counseling Services

The mission of the Office of Counseling Services is to provide comprehensive, developmental counseling services to all university students who may experience emotional, behavioral and/or psychological concerns during college life. All clinical staff are North Carolina Board Licensed Clinical Mental Health Counselors or North Carolina Board Licensed Clinical Social Workers. We contract with a North Carolina Board Licensed Psychiatric provider to manage psychiatric medication needs.

We recognize that this time in life brings exploration of your abilities to manage stress, build relationships, balance work and play, and learn more about wellness. It is a time for change and growth. Our office can assist students in many ways during this important time in their life.

Counseling services are available to currently enrolled students at High Point University and are covered by student fees. Any costs associated with medication that may be prescribed by our psychiatric provider are the responsibility of the client.

Events for Move-in Weekend are still being planned! The best place to stay tuned for updates is our Welcome Week website.

We look forward to hosting students and parents for this meaningful HPU tradition. Set aside 90 minutes for Convocation.
