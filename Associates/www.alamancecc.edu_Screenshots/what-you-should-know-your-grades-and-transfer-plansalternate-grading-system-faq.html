Alternate Grading System Spring 2020 FAQ

As a result of the unusual circumstances surrounding COVID-19 including the transition of face-to-face courses to remote learning, a new grade option will be available for all students for the Spring 2020 semester. To relieve pressures resulting from the disruptions that students are experiencing, ACC is providing an opt-in “Pass” grading system.

Below are our most frequently asked questions regarding the Spring 2020 Alternate Grading System.  

For University Transfer Students:

I am planning to transfer to a four-year university. What should I do?

Our advice is that if you have a grade of A, B, or C, you should keep that grade on your transcript rather than changing to a Pass grade. However—see next question.

I’m planning to transfer and I have a very high GPA. I know I’ll end up with lower grades than normal grades this semester due to COVID-19. Should I use a PT grade or keep my final grade if it is a B or C?

If a high GPA is important for you and significant for your transfer plans, you can opt to replace a B or a C with a Pass-Transfer grade. The PT grade will not impact your GPA. However, we recommend that you speak to your advisor to make sure you are making the best decision possible.

I plan to transfer to one of the UNC System institutions. Will my transfer university give credit for my ACC classes with a grade of PT?

UNC institutions will take Spring 2020 classes with a grade of PT because it indicates that you earned a grade of C or higher, which is required for transfer.

What should I do about a Pass-Transfer grade if my transfer institution is not a UNC school?

If you plan to transfer to a four-year institution out of state or a private institution, you should check with that institution if at all possible before changing a grade to PT.

I am a transfer student. Can I use a grade of PN?

We do not recommend a grade of Pass-Non-Transfer for a student who wants to transfer that class credit to a four-year institution. A grade of PN will not count toward graduation or transfer to a four-year institution for a student in one of these programs: Associate in Arts, Associate in Science, Associate in Engineering, Associate in Fine Arts.
Your transcript will indicate that a PT = minimum grade of C and a PN = minimum grade of D.

 

For Non-Transfer Students:

I am not planning to transfer my credits to a four-year institution. I plan to go directly to work when I complete my degree. What should I do if I have a low grade due to COVID-19?

If you earn a D in a class and you are concerned about its impact on your GPA, you can change that grade to a PN. A PN will not impact your GPA but will fulfill your graduation requirements. If you earn an A, B, or C, we recommend that you keep that grade. However, if you have a high GPA and earn a C due to COVID-19, you can change that grade to a PT, which equates to a C or higher and will also not impact your GPA.

I am not in a University Transfer program. Can I still use the PT grade even if I don’t have plans to transfer to a four-year school?

Any student, whether a transfer student or not, can opt to use the Pass-Transfer grade as long as their final grade is a C or higher.

I’m not a University Transfer student. Can I use the PT grade if my final grade was a C or higher?

Yes, you can. If you want to use a Pass grade, we recommend that if your final grade was higher than a D, you choose the PT, not the PN, grade.

I am in a program that has a requirement of a minimum grade of B. Should I use the Pass option?

If your program has any minimum grade requirements higher than a C, you should not use the Pass option. The Pass options will not meet that minimum grade standard. Talk to your advisor about your options if you earn lower than a B in your program classes.

I am thinking about eventually entering a program that has minimum grade of B requirements. Should I use the Pass option?

If you think you might want to enter a program with higher grade requirements, and you have earned a B, you should not use the Pass grade options. If you do, your original grade will not appear on the transcript, only the Pass grade that you selected to replace it.

Reviewers will only know that the PT grade was issued for a grade of C or higher. If you earn a B, keep that grade on your transcript. If you earn a C or lower, it will not count toward entry into that program, and you should talk to your advisor about your next steps.

 

General FAQ:

What is the difference between a PT and a PN?

PT stands for Pass-Transfer and equates to a grade of C or higher. PN stands for Pass-Non-Transfer and equates to a grade of D or higher.

Can I change the grade back from a PT to the original grade in the future?

No. Once the change is made, it is permanent.

Does my original final grade remain in my records?

If you replace a grade with one of the Pass options, only the Pass grade will show on your transcript as your official grade. The college will not issue records with the original grade included.

I receive financial aid. Will choosing a PT or a PN affect my financial aid?

No, but you should check with your financial aid counselor if you have questions or concerns about opting for one of these grades.

Will choosing a PT or a PN impact my GPA?

No. It will not improve your GPA, but it will also not hurt your GPA.

I am repeating a class hoping to earn a higher grade to improve my GPA. What should I do?

If the grade you earn this spring is higher than the previous grade earned, we recommend that you keep the grade. If it is lower than the previous grade, talk to your advisor about what you should do. Changing it to a Pass grade will not impact your GPA this semester but will also not replace the original grade.

I want to talk to my advisor but can’t remember who that is. How do I find my advisor?

You can find your faculty advisor in Self-Service. Click on Student Planning, and that is where you will find the name of your assigned advisor. Step-by-step instructions are provided below:

  1. Go to ACC's home page and click on the "Quick Links" tab at the top of the page.
  2. Click on the "Self Service" link.
  3. Click the "Log In" tab at the top of the page.
  4. Enter your ACCess User ID/Username (Ex: jfsmith123) and password you created in the steps above.
  5. Click "Sign In"
  6. Once you are logged in, click the "students" tab to access your student information.
  7. To find your academic advisor, click on the student "planning" tab and then on advising. 

What does a withdrawal mean?

In Spring 2020, students who withdraw from classes due to COVID-19 will earn a grade of WE, which means Withdraw-Emergency. A WE will not impact your GPA but it will not count toward graduation or fulfilling your degree requirements. You will have to re-enroll in and pay for the course in another semester. In Spring 2020, students on financial aid who earn a WE (withdraw due to COVID-19) will not be penalized.

What does an incomplete mean?

In Spring 2020, students who cannot finish a class due to COVID-19 will earn a temporary grade of IE, which means Incomplete-Emergency. You will have until December 10, 2020, to complete the work for the course, and the work you have completed so far this semester will count toward that grade. You will not have to re-enroll in or pay for another section of the course. If the grade earned is not satisfactory to you, you will have from December 10, 2020, to January 20, 2021 to change the grade to one of the Pass grades explained above.
